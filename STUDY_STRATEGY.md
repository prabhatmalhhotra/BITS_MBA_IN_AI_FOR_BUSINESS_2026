# Study Strategy — BITS WILP MBA (AI for Business)

A working professional's playbook: how to actually finish this MBA with a strong CGPA without burning out.

---

## 1. The reality of WILP

- 4 courses per semester, each with **32 hours of live instruction** spread over ~14 weekends.
- Every course has **3 evaluation components**: continuous (EC-1), mid-semester (EC-2), comprehensive (EC-3).
- You are working full-time. Realistic study budget: **8–12 hours/week** during the semester, **20+ hours/week** in the exam month.

---

## 2. The 3-pass learning method

### Pass 1 — During live class (Saturday / Sunday)

- Attend live. If you must miss, watch the recording within 48 hours.
- Don't take notes during class — listen and follow. Mark only timestamps where the faculty emphasised a concept.
- After class, write a **one-page summary** from memory. This is the single most powerful retention move.

### Pass 2 — Mid-week deepening (Tue–Thu)

- Read the corresponding chapter from the textbook (1–2 hours).
- Solve 5 short questions and 1 long question from this vault.
- Update your own formula sheet / definitions sheet.

### Pass 3 — Pre-exam consolidation (4 weeks before EC-2 / EC-3)

- Re-do all chapter summaries, then attempt full PYQ papers timed.
- Build a **20-line cheat-sheet per subject** with formulas, definitions, frameworks. Carry it for open-book exams.

## 5. Per-subject study tactics

### Quantitative subjects (Stats, Accounting, Finance, Economics, OM)
- 70% problem solving, 30% theory.
- Maintain a separate **formula sheet** per subject.
- Practice every numerical type **at least 3 times**.
- For Comprehensive (often open-book), prepare an indexed sheet pointing to where each formula/example is in your notes.

### Conceptual subjects (MPO, Marketing, Strategic Mgmt)
- 30% memorisation (frameworks, models), 70% application (cases).
- For each model, prepare: definition + 2-line example + 1 diagram.
- Read 2–3 Harvard / Indian business cases per chapter.

### Tech subjects (AI Apps, Applied DS, Applied ML/DL)
- 50% concept, 50% hands-on coding.
- Maintain a **GitHub repo** for every assignment.
- Reproduce every classroom demo end-to-end.

### Electives
- Pick electives that align with your day job to maximise learning ROI.
- For AI electives: build 1 portfolio project alongside.

## 6. Continuous Evaluation (EC-1) hacks

- **Quizzes** are usually open and you can attempt 2–3 times: take the first one to see the question pool, prepare, then attempt for marks.
- **Assignments** typically need ~6 hours over 2 weekends. Start the day they are released.
- Answer the question that is asked, not the question you wish was asked. WILP graders mark on rubric.
- Submit by **T−24 hours** to avoid LMS issues.

---

## 7. Mid-Sem (EC-2) preparation — 3-week plan

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| T−3 | Re-read first half of syllabus (typically Modules 1–6). Make summaries. |
| T−2 | Solve 3 PYQ Mid-Sem papers (timed, 2 hours each). Identify weak topics. |
| T−1 | Memorise formulas + definitions. One paper per day. Sleep on time. |

Mid-Sem usually covers **first 50–60% of syllabus**. Closed-book in most subjects.

---

## 8. Comprehensive (EC-3) preparation — 4-week plan

| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| T−4 | Re-read entire syllabus. Build chapter summaries. |
| T−3 | Build the **open-book cheat-sheet** (formulas + frameworks + indexed examples). |
| T−2 | Solve 3 full PYQ Comprehensive papers (timed). |
| T−1 | Solve 1 paper/day. Sharpen cheat-sheet. Sleep early on T−1. |

Comprehensive covers **entire syllabus, weighted toward post-mid material**.

---

## 9. Capstone Project (Sem 4)

- Pick a problem from your **own workplace**. Easier data access, easier mentor sign-off, easier panel defence.
- AI for Business projects that work well: customer churn, demand forecasting, document automation with GenAI, fraud detection, marketing mix optimisation.
- Define problem → baseline → ML model → business impact in INR/%.
- Final deliverables: Dissertation (60–80 pages) + presentation + viva.

---

## 10. Mistakes to avoid

1. **Skipping live classes** for "I'll watch the recording later" — recordings pile up.
2. **Ignoring EC-1** — it is the easiest 30 marks; squander it and you need 80+ in mid+comp combined.
3. **Studying only the textbook** — handouts call out specific chapters; faculty slides indicate exact exam scope.
4. **No formula sheet** — guarantees panic in numerical exams.
5. **Cramming the night before** — every WILP exam rewards consistency over heroics.

---

## 11. Quick wins for CGPA

- Pick electives in Sem 3/4 strategically — easier graders + topics aligned with your work.
- Comprehensive open-book? **Do not under-prepare** — the curve rewards depth, not just access to notes.
- Always attempt every question. WILP partial-marking is generous on showing process.
- Form a 3-person study group; rotate "topic owner" each week.
