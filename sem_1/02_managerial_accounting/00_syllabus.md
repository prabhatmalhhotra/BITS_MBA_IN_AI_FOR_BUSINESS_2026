## Course description (verbatim)

Basic concepts, double-entry accounting, journal, ledger, trial balance, profit & loss account, balance sheet, cash-flow statement, financial statement analysis, ratio analysis, cost-volume-profit analysis, inventory valuation, cost accounting and budgetary control systems, financial analysis and forecasting.

## Modular Content Structure

**Self-reading**: Types of business structures — Sole Proprietorship, Partnership, LLP, OPC, Private and Public Companies.

### Module 1 — Introduction to Accounting
- Concept and importance of accounting in business
- Importance of financial and management accounting as a source of value creation
- Linkage between Business Strategy and Financial Statements (maximising operating profit)
- Overview of the Financial Accounting Process (Cycles) and expected outcomes

### Module 2 — Accounting Principles and Financial Reporting Framework
- Fundamental principles and assumptions in financial accounting
- **Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP)**
- **International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) and key IAS/IFRS standards**:
  - **IFRS S1 & S2** (Sustainability and Climate Disclosures)
  - **IFRS 5, 7, 8, 13**
  - **IFRS 15** (Revenue), **IAS 2** (Inventory), **IAS 16** (PPE)
- **Comparison of US GAAP vs IFRS**, especially on **R&D accounting**
- **Identifying accounting red flags**: bias and noise due to rule rigidity
- Where financial reporting falls short

### Module 3 — Accounting Mechanics and Transaction Flow
- Categories of accounts: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Income, Expenses
- Accounting Equation and Dual-Entry System
- Recording in Journal → Posting to Ledger → Trial Balance → Financial Statements

### Module 4 — Understanding Financial Statements
- Income Statement (P&L): structure & purpose
- Balance Sheet: structure & purpose
- Cash Flow Statement: Operating / Investing / Financing activities
- Preparation and interconnection of financial statements

### Module 5 — Integrating Financial Statements: A Holistic View
- Integrating P&L, B/S and Cash Flow for performance analysis
- Using real-world financial statements for business evaluation

### Module 6 — Ratio Analysis: Concepts and Classifications
- Liquidity / Solvency / Profitability / Efficiency-Activity ratios
- Definitions, formulae and usage of key ratios

### Module 7 — Ratio Analysis: Analysis and Interpretation
- Trend analysis and comparison with industry benchmarks
- Case-based evaluation of financial ratios
- Using real-life company financials

### Module 8 — Cost Concepts and CVP Analysis
- Types of Costs: Fixed, Variable, Direct, Indirect; Prime & Conversion costs
- Break-even Point: calculation and interpretation
- Contribution Margin & Margin of Safety
- CVP analysis in short-term decision making

### Module 9 — Managerial Decisions Using Cost Accounting Information
- **Make or Buy** decisions
- **Special order** acceptance
- Cost considerations in **pricing strategy** and profit planning

### Module 10 — Cost Allocation, Inventory Valuation & Budgeting
- **Inventory Valuation Methods**: FIFO, LIFO, Weighted Average — impact on financial statements
- **Budgets**: Master, Operating, Capital, Zero-Based
- **Variance Analysis** — importance in performance monitoring

## Experiential Learning Component (ELC)

> Each participant selects an organisation (with annual report). If unavailable, choose any similar organisation. Collect **5 years of annual reports** with full financial statements.

**What you will do:**
- Apply class concepts to do FS analysis & interpretation of the chosen org
  1. Trend analysis (5 yrs)
  2. Comparative analysis (vs competitors, 5 yrs)
  3. Industry comparative analysis (vs industry avg, 5 yrs)

**How:** Pull data from Bloomberg or open sources; analyse:
1. Cost structure risk → CVP analysis
2. Liquidity → ability to meet current liabilities + asset utilisation
3. Leverage → operating leverage (EBIT sensitivity to revenue) and financial leverage (EPS sensitivity to EBIT); ability to meet interest obligations
4. Profitability → Economic, Commercial, Owners' profitability

**Group sizes** (1 / 2 / 4 / 6 / 8 students) — bigger groups must analyse more orgs.

**Marks distribution:**
- 5 marks for calculating appropriate ratios and parameters
- 10 marks for critical analysis and interpretations
