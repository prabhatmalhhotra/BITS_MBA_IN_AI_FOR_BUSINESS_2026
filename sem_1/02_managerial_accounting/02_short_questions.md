# Managerial Accounting — Short Questions (handout-aligned)

> Aligned to `MBACC ZG502` 16-session plan. Mid-Sem (closed book) draws from S1-S8 (intro → IFRS → ratios part 1). Comprehensive (open book) covers S1-S16.
> Each Q has a 1-3 line ideal answer.

## Session 1 — Introduction to Accounting
1. **What is managerial accounting?** Internal decision-support discipline that uses cost & financial data to plan, control, and evaluate; flexible, forward-looking, not bound by GAAP.
2. **Distinguish financial vs management accounting.** Financial = external, statutory, historical, GAAP/Ind-AS-compliant. Management = internal, no statutory format, forward-looking.
3. **List four branches of accounting.** Financial, Management/Cost, Tax, Sustainability (ESG/IFRS S1-S2).
4. **State the steps in the financial accounting cycle.** Identify transaction → Journal → Ledger → Trial Balance → Adjustments → Financial Statements.
5. **Why is accounting a "value-creation lens"?** It links business strategy to outcomes (margin, ROCE, cash) used by management to allocate capital, not just for compliance.

## Session 2 — GAAP & Accounting Principles
6. **State the dual-aspect concept.** Every transaction has equal Dr and Cr effects; foundation of double-entry bookkeeping.
7. **Define going concern.** Assumption that the entity will continue indefinitely; justifies historical cost over liquidation valuation.
8. **State the matching principle.** Expenses are recognised in the same period as the revenues they help generate.
9. **Explain conservatism (prudence).** Anticipate losses but never gains; gives a downward bias to assets and profits.
10. **Difference between concept and convention.** Concepts = fundamental assumptions ("why"); conventions = practical guidelines ("how"): consistency, conservatism, full disclosure, materiality.

## Session 3-4 — Journal, Ledger, Trial Balance, Financial Statements
11. **State the accounting equation.** Assets = Liabilities + Equity (always balances).
12. **Rules of debit & credit (modern).** Asset/Expense ↑ Dr, ↓ Cr. Liability/Income/Equity ↑ Cr, ↓ Dr.
13. **Pass entry: Cash sale ₹50,000.** Cash A/c Dr 50,000 / To Sales A/c 50,000.
14. **Pass entry: Purchased goods on credit ₹20,000 from Ravi.** Purchases A/c Dr 20,000 / To Ravi A/c 20,000.
15. **What does Trial Balance not detect?** Errors of omission, commission (right amount wrong account, same side), principle, and compensating errors.
16. **Why prepare adjusting entries before final accounts?** To recognise accruals, prepayments, depreciation, bad debts and apply matching/accrual principles.
17. **Why "Cash is King"?** A profitable firm can collapse if cash flow timing is mismanaged; profit is opinion, cash is fact.
18. **Indirect-method CFO formula.** Start with PAT → +/- non-cash items (depreciation, deferred tax) → +/- working capital changes → less tax paid = CFO.

## Session 5 — IFRS / IAS & Quality of Financial Reporting
19. **What does IFRS S1 cover?** General sustainability disclosures — material sustainability risks/opportunities affecting future cash flows.
20. **What does IFRS S2 cover?** Climate-related disclosures — physical/transition risks, GHG metrics, transition plans.
21. **State the 5-step IFRS 15 revenue model.** (1) Identify contract → (2) Identify performance obligations → (3) Determine TP → (4) Allocate → (5) Recognise as obligations satisfied.
22. **Inventory valuation under IAS 2.** Lower of cost and Net Realisable Value (NRV); LIFO is **prohibited** under IFRS.
23. **PPE measurement under IAS 16.** Cost or revaluation model; capitalise components and depreciate each over its useful life.
24. **Difference: US GAAP vs IFRS on R&D.** US GAAP expenses all R&D. IFRS (IAS 38) expenses research; capitalises **development** if 6 PIRATE criteria met.
25. **Three-level fair value hierarchy (IFRS 13).** Level 1 = quoted in active market; Level 2 = observable inputs; Level 3 = unobservable.
26. **List four red flags in financial reporting.** Aggressive revenue recognition, capitalising operating expenses, recurring "one-time" items, off-balance-sheet financing.
27. **What is "big bath" accounting?** Recording large losses in a bad year so subsequent years look better; usually with restructuring/impairment charges.
28. **Why does conservatism create downward bias today?** Modern asset-light firms have intangibles (brand, R&D, customer relationships) that aren't capitalised, understating their economic worth.

## Session 6-7 — Understanding & Analysing Financial Statements
29. **Three components of the cash flow statement.** Operating, Investing, Financing.
30. **Direct vs indirect method of CFO.** Direct: list cash receipts/payments. Indirect: reconcile from PAT. Indirect more common in India.
31. **Schedule III India presentation order.** Equity & Liabilities first; then Assets. Within each, non-current then current.
32. **A red flag: PAT growing but OCF flat — what does it mean?** Earnings quality concern — likely receivables bloat or aggressive recognition; profit not converting to cash.
33. **Define EBITDA.** Earnings before Interest, Tax, Depreciation & Amortisation; proxy for operating cash generation.

## Session 8 — Ratio Analysis (intro)
34. **Current ratio formula and norm.** CA / CL; ideally 1.5-2.0.
35. **Quick ratio formula.** (CA − Inventory − Prepaid) / CL; ideally ≥ 1.0.
36. **State Debt-Equity ratio.** Long-term Debt / Equity; lower = lower financial risk.
37. **Define Interest Coverage Ratio.** EBIT / Interest expense; ≥ 3 considered comfortable.
38. **What is ROCE?** EBIT / Capital Employed (Equity + LT Debt); pre-tax measure of capital productivity.

## Session 9-10 — Ratio Analysis (deep dive)
39. **DuPont 3-step ROE.** Net Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier.
40. **DuPont 5-step ROE.** Tax Burden × Interest Burden × EBIT Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier.
41. **Cash Conversion Cycle formula.** DSI + DSO − DPO; lower is better (faster cash recovery).
42. **What does negative working capital indicate?** Either operational excellence (collect first, pay later — e.g., FMCG, e-commerce) or liquidity stress (depending on context).
43. **Why must ratios be benchmarked?** Single ratio in isolation is meaningless; compare against industry peers, historical trend, and strategic targets.
44. **Tell-tale ratios for banking sector.** Very high equity multiplier (12-15×), thin net interest margin, NIM 2-4 %, NPA ratios.
45. **Tell-tale ratios for FMCG sector.** Low margin, high asset turnover, low DSO, modest debt, high inventory turnover.
46. **Limitations of ratio analysis.** Historical data, inflation distortion, window-dressing, accounting policy differences across firms, single ratio misleading without context.

## Session 11-13 — Empirical / Case Study FS Analysis
47. **What is common-size statement?** P&L items as % of revenue; B/S items as % of total assets — enables size-independent comparison.
48. **What is comparative statement?** Side-by-side YoY view with absolute and % changes — reveals trends and abnormalities.
49. **5-year CAGR formula.** (End Value / Start Value)^(1/n) − 1, where n = number of years.
50. **Operating leverage formula.** % Δ EBIT / % Δ Revenue; high fixed-cost firms have high operating leverage.
51. **Financial leverage formula.** % Δ EPS / % Δ EBIT; debt amplifies EPS sensitivity.
52. **Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF).** EBIT(1−t) + D&A − Capex − ΔWC.
53. **Quality of earnings ratio.** OCF / PAT; ≥ 1 indicates earnings backed by cash.
54. **State three sources of company financials.** Annual Report, Bloomberg/Capitaline/screener.in/CRISIL, BSE/NSE filings.

## Session 14-15 — Cost Accounting and CVP
55. **Difference: Fixed vs Variable cost (one example each).** Fixed = independent of activity within relevant range (rent). Variable = proportional to output (raw material).
56. **Define Prime Cost.** Direct Material + Direct Labour + Direct Expenses.
57. **Define Conversion Cost.** Direct Labour + Manufacturing Overhead.
58. **Cost-of-Goods-Sold formula.** Opening Stock + Production Cost − Closing Stock.
59. **Define Contribution Margin.** Sales − Variable Cost; available to cover fixed cost & profit.
60. **P/V Ratio formula.** (Contribution / Sales) × 100; portion of every ₹1 sales contributing to fixed cost & profit.
61. **Break-even point (units).** Fixed Cost / CM per unit. **Break-even (₹)** = Fixed Cost / P/V Ratio.
62. **Margin of Safety.** Actual Sales − Break-even Sales; or % = MoS / Actual Sales.
63. **DOL formula.** Contribution / EBIT; sensitivity of EBIT to revenue change.
64. **When accept a special order?** When price ≥ relevant variable cost, spare capacity exists, and accepting won't damage regular customers.
65. **When make-or-buy in favour of buying?** When relevant cost of making (variable + avoidable fixed) > buying price, AND no strategic loss (quality/IP/control).
66. **Limiting factor / Key factor — definition.** Constraint preventing higher output (scarce material/machine hours); rank products by CM per unit of limiting factor.
67. **Difference: Sunk vs Relevant cost.** Sunk = past, irreversible, irrelevant for decisions. Relevant = future, differential, avoidable; only relevant costs matter.

## Session 16 — Budgeting and Variance Analysis
68. **What is master budget?** Consolidated set of all sub-budgets producing projected P&L, B/S, Cash Flow.
69. **Define Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB).** Every expense justified from scratch each period (vs incremental); used to challenge entrenched costs.
70. **Difference: Fixed vs Flexible budget.** Fixed = single activity level. Flexible = adjusted for actual activity; better for variance analysis.
71. **Material Cost Variance formula.** (SQ × SP) − (AQ × AP). Splits into Price (AQ × (SP−AP)) and Usage (SP × (SQ−AQ)).
72. **Labour Efficiency Variance formula.** SR × (SH − AH); SR = standard rate, SH/AH = standard/actual hours for actual output.
73. **What is "management by exception" in variance analysis?** Investigate only material variances (favourable/adverse beyond a threshold); ignore noise.
74. **What is the "Beyond Budgeting" idea?** Replace static annual budgets with rolling forecasts and decentralised decision rights — addresses budget gaming.

## Bonus one-liners

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| Goodwill — type of asset? | Intangible non-current asset |
| Drawings deducted from? | Owner's capital |
| Bank overdraft — type of liability? | Current liability |
| Depreciation is which type of expense? | Non-cash period expense |
| Closing stock appears in? | Trading A/c (credit) and Balance Sheet (current asset) |
| Net working capital? | CA − CL |
| Operating cycle? | Days from raw material purchase to cash collection from sale |
| EBIT − Interest − Tax = | Net Profit |
| Provision vs Reserve? | Provision = charge against profit (known liability); Reserve = appropriation (strengthen position) |
| Ind-AS 2 inventory rule? | Lower of cost and NRV |
