# Managerial Accounting — Short Questions (with Answers)

> 35 short answers, 2–5 marks each. Drill these for EC-1 quizzes and the short-section of mid/comprehensive.

---

## Set A — Fundamentals

**Q1. Differentiate financial accounting and management accounting.**
Financial accounting = external reporting per regulations (audited, standardised, historical). Management accounting = internal decision support (forward-looking, flexible, no statutory format).

**Q2. State the accounting equation.**
Assets = Liabilities + Equity (Owner's funds).

**Q3. Define accrual basis of accounting.**
Revenues recognised when earned, expenses when incurred — irrespective of when cash flows.

**Q4. List any 4 GAAP concepts.**
Business entity, going concern, money measurement, dual aspect (also: matching, accrual, periodicity, cost, realisation).

**Q5. Distinguish capital expenditure and revenue expenditure.**
Capex = creates a long-term asset / extends life (capitalised, depreciated). Revex = consumed within the period (expensed).

## Set B — Accounting cycle

**Q6. State the rules of debit and credit using "DEAD CLIC".**
DEBIT increases: Expenses, Assets, Drawings. CREDIT increases: Liabilities, Income, Capital.

**Q7. What is a trial balance? What does it not detect?**
A list of ledger balances used to verify arithmetic accuracy. It does not detect errors of omission, errors of commission (right amount wrong account), errors of principle, or compensating errors.

**Q8. Pass journal entry for: Purchased goods worth ₹20,000 on credit from Ravi.**
Purchases A/c Dr 20,000 / To Ravi A/c 20,000. (Credit purchase.)

**Q9. Pass journal entry for: Paid salary ₹50,000 by cheque.**
Salary A/c Dr 50,000 / To Bank A/c 50,000.

**Q10. What is a contra entry in the cash book?**
An entry affecting both cash and bank columns simultaneously (e.g., cash deposited into bank); marked "C" — no separate posting needed.

## Set C — Financial statements

**Q11. State 3 differences between trading account and P&L account.**
(i) Trading shows gross profit; P&L shows net profit. (ii) Trading deals with direct expenses/revenue; P&L deals with indirect/operating. (iii) Trading is the first step; P&L starts with gross profit.

**Q12. Where does outstanding salary appear in financial statements?**
Debit to P&L (added to salary expense); shown as a current liability on balance sheet.

**Q13. State the indirect method for cash flow from operating activities.**
Start with PBT → add back non-cash items (depreciation, provisions) → adjust for working capital changes → less tax paid = CFO.

**Q14. What is the difference between provision for doubtful debts and bad debts written off?**
Bad debts written off = actual non-recoverable; charged to P&L, removed from debtors. Provision for doubtful debts = estimated future loss; charged to P&L, deducted from debtors as a contra.

**Q15. State two qualitative characteristics of useful financial information.**
Relevance and faithful representation (also: comparability, verifiability, timeliness, understandability).

## Set D — Depreciation and inventory

**Q16. Differentiate SLM and WDV depreciation.**
SLM: equal annual depreciation. WDV: depreciation as % of book value, higher in early years. SLM matches even-usage assets; WDV matches assets used more in early years.

**Q17. Asset cost ₹5,00,000, scrap ₹50,000, life 5 years. Annual depreciation under SLM?**
(5,00,000 − 50,000) / 5 = ₹90,000/year.

**Q18. Why is LIFO disallowed under Ind AS?**
LIFO can produce balance sheet inventory values far below current replacement cost and can be manipulated to manage taxable income.

**Q19. State the principle of valuing inventory under Ind AS 2.**
Lower of cost and net realisable value (NRV) — applied item-by-item.

**Q20. Distinguish provision and reserve.**
Provision = charge against profit for known liabilities/expenses with uncertain amount/timing. Reserve = appropriation of profit to strengthen financial position.

## Set E — Ratio analysis

**Q21. State the formula and ideal value of the current ratio.**
Current ratio = Current assets / Current liabilities. Ideal ≈ 1.5 to 2.

**Q22. Define ROE and ROCE.**
ROE = Net profit / Shareholders' equity × 100. ROCE = EBIT / (Equity + Long-term debt) × 100.

**Q23. State the Du Pont identity for ROE.**
ROE = Net margin × Asset turnover × Equity multiplier = (NP/Sales) × (Sales/Assets) × (Assets/Equity).

**Q24. What does a falling debtor turnover ratio indicate?**
Slower collection of receivables → tighter cash flow, possibly weaker credit control.

**Q25. Compute interest coverage ratio if EBIT = ₹2.5 cr, interest = ₹50 lakh.**
2.5 / 0.5 = 5 times. Comfortable cushion to service debt.

## Set F — Cost accounting & CVP

**Q26. Differentiate fixed and variable costs with one example each.**
Fixed cost — independent of activity within a relevant range (rent). Variable cost — varies directly with activity (raw material).

**Q27. Define contribution and P/V ratio.**
Contribution = Sales − Variable cost. P/V ratio = Contribution / Sales × 100. Tells how much each rupee of sales contributes to fixed cost & profit.

**Q28. Selling price ₹50, variable cost ₹30, fixed cost ₹2,00,000. Compute BEP in units.**
Contribution per unit = 20. BEP = 2,00,000 / 20 = 10,000 units.

**Q29. Define margin of safety.**
Excess of actual (or budgeted) sales over break-even sales. High MoS = cushion against demand shocks.

**Q30. Define operating leverage.**
DOL = Contribution / Operating profit. Measures sensitivity of profit to change in sales — higher fixed cost structures have higher DOL.

## Set G — Marginal vs absorption + decisions

**Q31. State two key differences between marginal and absorption costing.**
(i) Treatment of fixed manufacturing overhead — period expense (marginal) vs product cost (absorption). (ii) Inventory valuation differs → reported profits differ when production ≠ sales.

**Q32. When should a special order be accepted?**
When the price ≥ variable cost (positive contribution), spare capacity exists, and accepting won't impair regular customers' pricing or relationships.

**Q33. Define limiting factor / key factor.**
The constraint that prevents the firm from achieving unlimited output (e.g., scarce raw material, machine hours). Decisions ranked by contribution per unit of limiting factor.

---

## Set H — Budgeting & variances

**Q34. Differentiate fixed and flexible budgets.**
Fixed budget: prepared for one activity level. Flexible: adjusts for various activity levels — better for performance comparison and variance analysis.

**Q35. State material price and usage variance formulas.**
Price Variance = AQ × (SP − AP). Usage Variance = SP × (SQ − AQ). Cost = Price + Usage.
