Cash Flow Statement & Integrating Financial Statements

The Cash Flow Statement strips away accrual assumptions to show where cash actually came from and went. When combined with P&L and Balance Sheet, it completes your analytical toolkit.

T1 Chapters 14-15 (Maheswari)Mid-Sem Syllabus~50 min read
Class Notes AI Notes
Real-World Problem

Byju's reported profits but ran out of cash. How?

Byju's (Think & Learn Pvt Ltd) showed aggressive revenue growth and even accounting profits in some periods. Yet by 2023, it could not pay salaries, defaulted on loans, and faced insolvency. The Cash Flow Statement would have told the story: massive cash burn on acquisitions (CFI), funded by debt and equity (CFF), while operating cash flow (CFO) remained deeply negative. The P&L alone was misleading.

The Cash Flow Statement is the "lie detector" of financial reporting. It strips away accrual assumptions and shows where cash actually came from and went. This session completes your toolkit for financial statement analysis.

1 Cash Flow Statement: The Three Buckets Beginner

Every cash movement falls into one of three categories:

Operating (CFO)
Cash from core business
Sales receipts, supplier payments, salaries, taxes
Investing (CFI)
Cash for long-term assets
Capex, acquisitions, investments bought/sold
Financing (CFF)
Cash from/to capital providers
Debt raised/repaid, equity issued, dividends
Change in Cash = CFO + CFI + CFF
Closing Cash (B/S) = Opening Cash + CFO + CFI + CFF
Daily Life Example

Your personal cash flow this month:
Operating: Salary received ₹80,000, grocery/rent/bills paid ₹45,000 = CFO +₹35,000
Investing: Bought mutual funds ₹20,000 = CFI -₹20,000
Financing: Paid car EMI (principal portion) ₹10,000 = CFF -₹10,000
Net change: +₹5,000 (your bank balance grew by ₹5K)

2 Indirect Method for CFO Intermediate

Most companies (and exam questions) use the Indirect Method to compute operating cash flow:

Start: Net Profit (PAT)
From the Income Statement
▼ Add back
Non-Cash Charges
+ Depreciation, + Amortisation, + Provision for bad debts
▼ Adjust
Non-Operating Items
Remove interest expense (goes to CFF), investment income (goes to CFI)
▼ Adjust
Working Capital Changes
Increase in receivables/inventory = cash USE
Increase in payables = cash SOURCE
▼
Cash Flow from Operations (CFO)

Working Capital Changes: The Key to CFO

ChangeEffect on CashWhy?
Receivables increaseCash decreasesYou sold goods but haven't collected cash yet
Inventory increaseCash decreasesYou bought more goods and they're sitting unsold
Payables increaseCash increasesYou're delaying payments to suppliers (keeping cash longer)
Prepaid expenses increaseCash decreasesYou paid in advance
Exam Tip: The indirect method is heavily tested. Remember: increase in current ASSET = cash outflow (subtract); increase in current LIABILITY = cash inflow (add). This is counter-intuitive at first — practice until it clicks.

3 Integrating the Three Statements Advanced

The three financial statements are deeply interconnected. Here is how a single transaction flows through all three:

TransactionP&L ImpactB/S ImpactCF Impact
Buy machinery ₹10L cashNo immediate impact (depreciation later)PPE +10L, Cash -10LCFI: -10L (investing outflow)
Record depreciation ₹1LExpense +1L, Profit -1LPPE -1L, Reserves -1LCFO: Added back (non-cash)
Issue shares ₹50LNo P&L impactCash +50L, Share Capital +50LCFF: +50L (financing inflow)
Credit sale ₹5LRevenue +5LReceivables +5LCFO: Subtracted (no cash received)
Receive payment on credit saleNo P&L impactCash +5L, Receivables -5LCFO: Added (cash received)
Articulation Check: Closing Cash on B/S - Opening Cash = CFO + CFI + CFF
Trending Example (2024)

Asian Paints FY24 provides a textbook example of statement integration: PAT was ₹5,558 crore, but CFO was ₹4,198 crore (lower than PAT). Why? Working capital ate up cash — inventory and receivables increased as the company expanded. Meanwhile, CFI was negative ₹2,100 crore (capex on new plants), and CFF was negative ₹3,200 crore (dividends paid). The balance sheet showed healthy reserves growing year-on-year. All three statements told a consistent story of a profitable company investing for growth while rewarding shareholders.

Session 7 — Key Takeaways

  • Cash Flow Statement has three sections: Operating, Investing, Financing
  • Indirect method for CFO: PAT + non-cash items +/- working capital changes
  • Working capital rule: Current asset increase = cash outflow; Current liability increase = cash inflow
  • Three statements are interconnected: Net profit flows to B/S equity; Depreciation hits P&L and B/S; Cash changes link B/S and CF
  • Articulation check: Closing Cash - Opening Cash = CFO + CFI + CFF
  • CFO > PAT = high quality earnings; CFO < PAT = investigate working capital or accrual issues
