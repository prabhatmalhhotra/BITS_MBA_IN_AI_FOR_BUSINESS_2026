Real-World Problem

Maruti Suzuki vs Tata Motors: A competitor comparison case study

Maruti dominates passenger cars with 40%+ market share and consistent profitability. Tata Motors made a dramatic turnaround with EVs and JLR. How do you compare these two systematically? This session walks through a competitor analysis framework used in the ELC project.

The ELC project carries 15 marks (5 for ratios, 10 for analysis). This session equips you with the analytical framework to score full marks on the interpretation component.

1 ELC Project Framework Intermediate

1. Industry Context
Size, growth rate, key players, regulatory environment, cyclicality
▼
2. Company Background
Business model, segments, geography, competitive advantages
▼
3. Financial Highlights (5 Years)
Revenue, PAT, Total Assets, Key ratios in a summary table
▼
4. Profitability Deep-Dive
DuPont 5-step decomposition, margin trends
▼
5. Liquidity & Working Capital
Current ratio, CCC trend, working capital adequacy
▼
6. Leverage & Risk
D/E, ICR, Operating & Financial leverage
▼
7. Cash Flow Analysis
CFO/PAT ratio, FCFF, capex trends
▼
8. Comparative/Industry Analysis
Peer ratios, industry averages, competitive positioning
▼
9. Red Flag Scan
Auditor qualifications, related party transactions, aggressive accounting
▼
10. Recommendations
Investment thesis, management action items, risk alerts

2 Key Analysis Phrases for Your Report Advanced

Use professional financial language in your ELC report:

ObservationProfessional Phrasing
Margins improved"EBITDA margin expanded 220 bps over FY21-25, driven by operating leverage as revenue grew at an 18% CAGR"
Working capital worsened"Cash conversion cycle deteriorated from 32 days to 51 days, suggesting weakening collection discipline"
Good asset utilisation"ROCE of 22% is 600 bps above the industry median of 16%, reflecting superior capital allocation"
Quality of earnings concern"OCF/PAT ratio declined from 1.2x to 0.7x over 3 years, warranting scrutiny of accrual quality"
Operating Leverage = % Change in EBIT / % Change in Revenue
Financial Leverage = % Change in EPS / % Change in EBIT
FCFF = EBIT(1-t) + Depreciation - Capex - Change in Working Capital
Exam Tip: In the ELC, 10 of 15 marks are for interpretation. Do not just present a table of ratios. Every ratio needs a sentence explaining what it means for the business. Use the phrases above as templates.

Session 12 — Key Takeaways

  • 10-step ELC framework: Industry → Company → Financials → Profitability → Liquidity → Leverage → Cash Flow → Comparison → Red Flags → Recommendations
  • Interpretation > Computation: 10/15 marks are for analysis, not calculation
  • Use professional financial language and quantify observations with basis points and CAGR
  • Data sources: screener.in, company annual reports, BSE/NSE filings
