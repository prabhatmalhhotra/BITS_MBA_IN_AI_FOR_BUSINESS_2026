Real-World Problem

Completing Your Financial Analysis: Cross-Checks and Red Flags

You have computed 20+ ratios and written interpretations. But how do you know if the financial statements themselves are reliable? Session 13 teaches you the final quality checks that separate a good analysis from a great one.

A complete financial analysis must include a red flag scan and cross-checks. Without them, you may be building sophisticated analysis on unreliable data.

1 Cross-Checks for Case Work Advanced

CheckWhat to DoWhat to Look For
CFO vs PATCompute CFO/PAT ratio over 5 yearsRatio declining = deteriorating earnings quality
FCFFEBIT(1-t) + D&A - Capex - Delta WCPersistent negative FCFF = value destruction
Working Capital TrendTrack DSI, DSO, DPO and CCCLengthening CCC = cash being trapped in operations
Receivable ConcentrationTop 5 debtors as % of total receivablesHigh concentration = credit risk
Auditor ReportRead auditor qualifications and KAMsEmphasis of Matter paragraphs, going concern doubts
Notes to AccountsRead contingent liabilities, related party transactionsLarge undisclosed risks or non-arm's-length deals

2 Altman Z-Score: Predicting Bankruptcy Advanced

Z = 1.2(WC/TA) + 1.4(RE/TA) + 3.3(EBIT/TA) + 0.6(MVE/BVD) + 1.0(Sales/TA)
Z-Score RangeInterpretation
Z > 2.99Safe Zone — low probability of bankruptcy
1.81 < Z < 2.99Grey Zone — moderate risk, monitor closely
Z < 1.81Distress Zone — high probability of bankruptcy

Where: WC = Working Capital, TA = Total Assets, RE = Retained Earnings, MVE = Market Value of Equity, BVD = Book Value of Total Debt

Trending Example (2023-24)

Vodafone Idea consistently scored in the distress zone (Z < 1.0) with negative working capital, accumulated losses exceeding equity, and thin EBIT margins. The Z-score correctly flagged the ongoing financial distress that traditional revenue analysis alone would not capture.

Session 13 — Key Takeaways

  • Quality checks: CFO/PAT trend, FCFF, WC trend, receivable concentration, auditor report
  • Altman Z-Score predicts bankruptcy risk using 5 B/S and P&L ratios
  • Always read the Notes to Accounts and Auditor's Report
  • Your ELC report should end with a red flag section and actionable recommendations
