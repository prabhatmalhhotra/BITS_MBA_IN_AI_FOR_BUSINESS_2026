Budgeting & Variance Analysis

Planning with budgets and controlling with variances — the management cycle that turns accounting data into business action.

T1 Chapter 25 (Maheswari)Comprehensive Exam~50 min read
Real-World Problem

Why did IndiGo's actual fuel costs differ from budget by ₹800 crore in one quarter?

IndiGo budgets fuel costs based on expected ATF (Aviation Turbine Fuel) prices and planned flight hours. When crude oil spiked due to geopolitical tensions, actual fuel costs far exceeded the budget. But was this a price variance (fuel became expensive) or a usage variance (they flew more than planned)? Variance analysis separates these two effects — giving management precise information on what went wrong and who is responsible.

Budgeting sets the plan. Variance analysis tells you what deviated and why. Together, they form the control cycle that drives performance in every well-run company.

Fixed Budget

  • Set for one activity level
  • Does not adjust if volume changes
  • Simpler but less useful for variance analysis

Flexible Budget

  • Adjusts for actual activity level
  • Separates volume variance from cost variance
  • More accurate for performance evaluation

4 Variance Analysis Advanced

Variance = Standard (Budgeted) - Actual. Favourable (F) when actual is better; Adverse (A) when worse.

Material Variances

Material Cost Variance (MCV) = (SQ x SP) - (AQ x AP)
Material Price Variance (MPV) = AQ x (SP - AP)
Material Usage Variance (MUV) = SP x (SQ - AQ)

Where: SQ = Standard Quantity, SP = Standard Price, AQ = Actual Quantity, AP = Actual Price

Labour Variances

Labour Cost Variance = (SH x SR) - (AH x AR)
Labour Rate Variance = AH x (SR - AR)
Labour Efficiency Variance = SR x (SH - AH)

Where: SH = Standard Hours, SR = Standard Rate, AH = Actual Hours, AR = Actual Rate

Worked Example

Scenario: A company produces 1,000 units. Standard: 2 kg material per unit at ₹50/kg. Actual: used 2,100 kg at ₹48/kg.

VarianceCalculationAmountF/A
MCV(2,000 x 50) - (2,100 x 48) = 1,00,000 - 1,00,800₹800Adverse
MPV2,100 x (50 - 48) = 2,100 x 2₹4,200Favourable (bought cheaper)
MUV50 x (2,000 - 2,100) = 50 x (-100)₹5,000Adverse (used more)
CheckMPV + MUV = 4,200 F + 5,000 A= ₹800 A = MCV✓

Interpretation: The purchasing department saved money (₹4,200 F on price), but the production floor wasted material (₹5,000 A on usage). Could the cheaper material have caused more wastage? This is the kind of root-cause analysis management must perform.

Exam Tip: Variance analysis problems are guaranteed in the comprehensive exam. Always: (1) Compute each sub-variance separately, (2) Verify that sub-variances add up to total variance, (3) Label each F or A, (4) Provide one-line interpretation. The verification step alone earns marks for methodology.

5 Modern Budgeting Trends Intermediate

Traditional Annual Budget

  • Set once a year, rigid
  • Often based on last year + increment
  • Gaming: "spend it or lose it"
  • Outdated by month 3

Beyond Budgeting / Rolling Forecasts

  • Updated quarterly or monthly
  • Driver-based (link to business KPIs)
  • AI/ML for demand sensing
  • Used by Unilever, Handelsbanken, etc.
Trending Example (2025)

Indian IT companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) have moved away from static annual budgets to rolling 4-quarter forecasts updated every quarter. They use AI-driven demand sensing to predict deal pipeline and adjust hiring, bench utilisation, and subcontracting budgets dynamically. This is modern FP&A (Financial Planning & Analysis) in action — the future of budgeting.

Session 16 — Key Takeaways

  • Budgets serve 4 purposes: Plan, Coordinate, Motivate, Control
  • Budget types: Master, Operating, Capital, Financial, Zero-Based, Fixed vs Flexible
  • Cash budget: Opening + Receipts - Payments = Closing
  • Material Variance: MCV = MPV + MUV; Labour Variance: LCV = LRV + LEV
  • Favourable (F) = actual better than standard; Adverse (A) = actual worse
  • Always verify: sub-variances must add up to total variance
  • Management by exception: investigate only material variances
  • Modern trend: Rolling forecasts + AI-driven FP&A replacing static annual budgets
