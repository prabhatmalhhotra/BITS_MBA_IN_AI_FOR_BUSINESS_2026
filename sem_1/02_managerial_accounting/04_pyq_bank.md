## Mid-Semester papers

### Mid-Sem Paper A — Closed book, 2 hours, 60 marks

**Section A — Short answer (5 × 4 = 20 marks)**
1. State and explain any four GAAP concepts.
2. Differentiate between provision and reserve. Give one example of each.
3. Explain the rules of debit and credit using "DEAD CLIC".
4. State three differences between FIFO and LIFO methods of inventory valuation.
5. What does a high current ratio (e.g., 4) indicate? Is it always desirable?

**Section B — Numerical (3 × 8 = 24 marks)**
6. From the following, prepare Trading and P&L A/c and Balance Sheet of M/s Sharma Traders for the year ended 31 March 2025. (Trial balance + 4 adjustments provided.)

7. The cost of an asset is ₹6,00,000, scrap ₹60,000, life 8 years. Compute annual depreciation and book value at the end of years 1, 4 and 8 under (a) SLM, (b) WDV at 25%.

8. M/s ABC's financials show: Sales ₹40 lakh, COGS ₹28 lakh, Operating expenses ₹6 lakh, Interest ₹1 lakh, Tax @ 30%. Compute GP margin, NP margin, and operating margin.

**Section C — Long answer (1 × 16 = 16 marks)**
9. Discuss in detail the role of management accounting in modern corporations. Compare and contrast it with financial accounting on at least 6 dimensions.

### Mid-Sem Paper B — 50 marks

1. (5 m) Define accrual basis. Why is it considered superior to cash basis for periodic profit measurement?
2. (5 m) Pass journal entries for the following: (a) Started business with cash ₹5,00,000 (b) Bought goods on credit ₹1,20,000 (c) Sold half the goods at 25% profit, ₹50,000 cash + balance on credit (d) Salary paid ₹30,000 (e) Outstanding rent ₹10,000.
3. (8 m) From the trial balance below, prepare a trial balance check, then journal entries for given adjustments. (Specifics given.)
4. (10 m) Compute current ratio, quick ratio, debt-equity ratio, interest coverage and ROCE from the given balance sheet of Beta Ltd.
5. (10 m) Compute opening stock, closing stock, COGS, GP from the data: Sales ₹15 lakh, GP margin 30%, inventory turnover 6 times, opening stock = closing stock + ₹50,000.
6. (12 m) From the data: Trial balance + 5 adjustments — prepare full set of accounts.

## Comprehensive papers

### Comprehensive Paper A — Closed book, 2 hours, 100 marks

**Section A — Short answer (5 × 4 = 20 marks)**
1. Differentiate marginal and absorption costing.
2. Define operating leverage and degree of operating leverage.
3. State the formula for material price variance and material usage variance.
4. What is the difference between zero-based and traditional budgeting?
5. State four limitations of ratio analysis.

**Section B — Cost & CVP numericals (3 × 10 = 30 marks)**
6. SP = ₹250, VC = ₹150, FC = ₹15,00,000. Compute (i) BEP, (ii) MoS at sales of 25,000 units, (iii) Sales required for ₹10 lakh profit, (iv) New BEP if VC rises by 10%.

7. Standard cost: Material 4 kg @ ₹50 = ₹200; Labour 3 hr @ ₹60 = ₹180. Production = 1,500 units. Actuals: Material 6,200 kg @ ₹52, Labour 4,400 hrs @ ₹65. Compute all material and labour variances.

8. Optimal product mix problem with 2 products and a limited resource (machine hours) — compute optimal allocation and total contribution.

**Section C — Financial statement analysis (1 × 20 = 20 marks)**
9. Comparative ratios of two firms over 3 years. Diagnose strengths/weaknesses, recommend strategic actions.

**Section D — Decision case (1 × 15 = 15 marks)**
10. Special-order decision OR make-vs-buy decision OR sell-or-process-further. Compute incremental impact, recommend with reasoning.

**Section E — Theory long answer (1 × 15 = 15 marks)**
11. Discuss the Balanced Scorecard. Provide 12 KPIs across all 4 perspectives for a fintech start-up.

### Comprehensive Paper B — 80 marks

1. (10 m) Cash flow statement preparation (direct or indirect method) — given data.
2. (10 m) Capital budgeting: 4 projects with cash flows; compute NPV, IRR, payback. Recommend.
3. (10 m) Variance analysis (materials + labour + variable OH).
4. (10 m) Budget preparation: cash budget for 3 months from given data.
5. (10 m) Inventory valuation problem: FIFO vs Weighted Average — compute and contrast.
6. (10 m) Marginal vs absorption costing reconciliation when production ≠ sales.
7. (10 m) Theory: Discuss responsibility accounting and ROI vs RI vs EVA.
8. (10 m) Strategic mini-case requiring use of multiple ratios + recommendation.

## Common mistakes to avoid

1. Mis-classifying expense (capital vs revenue).
2. Forgetting closing stock as a credit in trading account AND a current asset on BS.
3. In ratios, mixing average and year-end figures inconsistently.
4. CVP — confusing contribution per unit with contribution margin ratio.
5. Variance signs — denoting "Adverse" (A) vs "Favourable" (F) clearly.
6. Capital budgeting — using cost of capital wrong year (year 0 vs year 1).
