## Mid-Sem papers (closed book, Sessions 1-8)

### Mid-Sem Paper A — 2 hours, 60 marks

**Section 1 — Short answer (4 × 4 = 16 marks)**

1. Distinguish financial accounting from management accounting with two examples each.
2. State the dual-aspect concept and explain why it forms the foundation of double-entry bookkeeping.
3. List four red flags that indicate poor quality of financial reporting.
4. State the 5-step revenue recognition model under IFRS 15.

**Section 2 — Numerical / Journalising (3 × 8 = 24 marks)**

5. Pass journal entries for the following transactions of M/s Karan Traders, May 2025: (a) Started business with cash ₹5,00,000. (b) Purchased machinery for ₹2,00,000 by cheque. (c) Sold goods costing ₹50,000 for ₹70,000 on credit to Suresh. (d) Received ₹40,000 from Suresh in full settlement of his account. (e) Paid salary ₹30,000.

6. From the following Trial Balance, prepare Trading & Profit & Loss Account and Balance Sheet of XYZ as on 31-Mar-2025. Closing stock ₹35,000. Depreciate furniture @ 10 %.

| Particulars | Dr (₹) | Cr (₹) |
|-------------|-------:|-------:|
| Capital | | 2,00,000 |
| Sales / Purchases | 1,50,000 | 2,80,000 |
| Stock (1-Apr) | 30,000 | |
| Wages | 25,000 | |
| Salaries | 40,000 | |
| Furniture | 80,000 | |
| Debtors / Creditors | 70,000 | 50,000 |
| Cash | 1,35,000 | |
| **Total** | **5,30,000** | **5,30,000** |

7. From P&L A/c data, compute Cash Flow from Operating Activities under the indirect method. PAT ₹2,50,000; Depreciation ₹40,000; Increase in debtors ₹30,000; Decrease in inventory ₹15,000; Increase in creditors ₹20,000; Tax paid ₹50,000.

**Section 3 — Long answer (1 × 20 = 20 marks)**

8. Explain in detail the IFRS framework. Discuss the impact of (a) IFRS 15 on revenue recognition, (b) IAS 2 on inventory valuation, (c) IAS 16 on PPE, and (d) US GAAP vs IFRS treatment of R&D. Give one Indian Ind-AS equivalent for each.

### Mid-Sem Paper B — 2 hours, 50 marks

1. (5 m) Distinguish capital expenditure from revenue expenditure. Give two examples each.
2. (5 m) State four limitations of historical-cost accounting in modern asset-light businesses.
3. (8 m) From the data: PAT ₹4,00,000; Depreciation ₹60,000; Decrease in debtors ₹20,000; Increase in inventory ₹35,000; Decrease in creditors ₹15,000; Tax paid ₹70,000. Compute CFO.
4. (10 m) ABC Ltd's standalone financials show Revenue ₹500 Cr, COGS ₹300 Cr, Op Exp ₹120 Cr, Interest ₹20 Cr, Tax ₹15 Cr; Equity ₹400 Cr, LT Debt ₹150 Cr, Total Assets ₹700 Cr; Current Assets ₹250 Cr (Inventory ₹80 Cr), Current Liabilities ₹180 Cr. Compute Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, D/E, ICR, ROA, ROE, Net Margin. Interpret.
5. (10 m) Discuss the major IFRS sustainability standards (IFRS S1 and IFRS S2). What kind of disclosures are required? How do they affect the analysis of asset-heavy vs asset-light firms?
6. (12 m) "Profit is opinion, cash is fact." Critically evaluate this statement using the relationship between accrual accounting and cash flow. Explain why a profitable firm can still face liquidity issues.

### Mid-Sem Paper C — Practice paper aligned to S2-25

1. (4 m) Define accrual basis and matching principle with one illustration.
2. (5 m) State and illustrate the Cost (historical) and Going Concern concepts.
3. (8 m) Pass journal entries for: (a) Withdrew goods worth ₹5,000 for personal use. (b) Bad debts written off ₹3,000. (c) Provision for doubtful debts created at 5 % on debtors ₹2,00,000. (d) Outstanding salary ₹8,000.
4. (10 m) Reconstruct the Cash Flow Statement (operating section, indirect method) given changes in working capital and net income.
5. (12 m) Discuss IFRS 13 fair value hierarchy. For each of three illustrative items (listed equity, unlisted bond, custom-built plant), categorise into Level 1/2/3 and explain.
6. (12 m) Discuss four red flags in financial reporting with real-world examples. How can an analyst use cash flow patterns to detect them?

## Comprehensive papers (open book, Sessions 1-16)

### Comprehensive Paper A — 2.5 hours, 100 marks

**Section A — Short answer (5 × 4 = 20 marks)**
1. State the DuPont 5-step decomposition of ROE.
2. Define and contrast operating leverage and financial leverage with formulas.
3. Define cash conversion cycle. State three ways to reduce it.
4. List the four kinds of budgets per the handout (Master, Operating, Capital, ZBB).
5. State material price variance and usage variance formulas.

**Section B — Numericals (4 × 10 = 40 marks)**

6. Compute and interpret all the following ratios for a manufacturing company: Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, Inventory Turnover, DSO, DPO, CCC, D/E, ICR, ROCE, Net Margin, Asset Turnover, ROE. Apply 5-step DuPont. (Data table provided in question.)

7. CVP problem: Selling price ₹250, variable cost ₹150, fixed cost ₹15,00,000. (a) BEP units & revenue. (b) Margin of Safety at 25,000 units sold. (c) Sales required for ₹6,00,000 profit. (d) New BEP if SP drops 5 % and VC rises 4 %.

8. Make-or-Buy: Component currently made at variable cost ₹80, fixed overhead ₹40 per unit (50 % avoidable on outsourcing); offered by vendor at ₹110. Annual demand 10,000 units. (a) Should outsource? (b) What if vendor offers ₹95?

9. Variance analysis: Standard 1 unit needs 2 kg material @ ₹40/kg + 3 hrs labour @ ₹50/hr. Actual production 4,000 units; actual material 8,500 kg @ ₹42/kg; actual labour 11,500 hrs @ ₹52/hr. Compute Material Price, Material Usage, Labour Rate, Labour Efficiency variances. Comment.

**Section C — Long answer / case (2 × 15 = 30 marks)**

10. **Case.** A pharmaceutical company's 5-year financials show: revenue CAGR 12 %, EBITDA margin contracting from 24 % to 19 %, R&D capitalised under IFRS rising from 8 % to 14 % of revenue, debt-equity rising from 0.5 to 0.9. (a) Diagnose the company's financial trajectory. (b) Discuss IFRS-related quality concerns (IFRS S1/S2 disclosures, IAS 38 R&D capitalisation). (c) Apply DuPont decomposition to explain ROE trend.

11. Discuss the key components of an effective budgetary control system. Compare incremental, flexible, and zero-based budgeting. Discuss limitations of traditional annual budgeting and the rise of "Beyond Budgeting" / driver-based forecasts.

**Section D — Application (1 × 10 = 10 marks)**

12. A consumer electronics firm is launching a new product. Help management decide pricing using CVP and cost-plus approaches. Discuss strategic considerations: penetration vs skimming pricing, competitor benchmarking, cost-plus floor.

### Comprehensive Paper B — 2.5 hours, 80 marks

1. (10 m) Discuss the IFRS framework with focus on S1, S2, 15, IAS 2, IAS 16. Explain the comparability advantage for a multinational investor.

2. (10 m) Common-size and comparative statements: From P&L data of two firms in the same sector (provided), prepare common-size and comparative statements; compute year-on-year growth rates. Interpret.

3. (12 m) Cash Flow Statement preparation (indirect method): from balance sheets of two consecutive years and the P&L of the current year (provided), prepare the Cash Flow Statement and reconcile cash & cash equivalents.

4. (12 m) Special order decision: A factory at 80 % capacity (16,000 units; total capacity 20,000) is offered an export order for 5,000 units at ₹90/unit. Standard SP ₹130, VC ₹70, FC ₹4,00,000. (a) Accept order? (b) What if accepting forces displacement of 1,500 domestic units? (c) Strategic factors beyond cost.

5. (12 m) Budgeting: Prepare a cash budget for 3 months (Jan-Mar 2026) with given sales, collections (50 % cash, 50 % next month), purchases, expenses, capex, dividend payments. Identify months requiring overdraft.

6. (14 m) Mini-case: A startup pivoting from B2C to B2B SaaS. Discuss how its (a) revenue recognition, (b) inventory/COGS, (c) cost structure (CVP), (d) cash flow profile change. Recommend appropriate IFRS standards and KPIs to track.

7. (10 m) Discuss the limitations of ratio analysis. How does an analyst overcome these in practice? Give examples for cyclical (steel) vs defensive (FMCG) industries.

---

### Comprehensive Paper C — Practice paper aligned to S2-25

1. (8 m) Compute and interpret 5-step DuPont for two firms with identical ROE but different drivers. Which is better quality and why?
2. (10 m) Discuss the appropriate accounting treatment under IFRS for a software company that capitalises development costs. Reconcile to the US GAAP view. Impact on EBITDA, net income, and asset base.
3. (12 m) Mini-case: Reliance Industries 5-year ratios provided. Discuss segment-wise (Oil-to-Chemicals, Retail, Jio) profitability, leverage, and capital allocation. Comment on the Jio investment cycle and its impact on consolidated ratios.
4. (12 m) CVP + product mix: Two products with given SP, VC, machine-hour requirements; total available machine hours and demand limits given. Determine optimal production mix and total profit.
5. (14 m) Variance analysis comprehensive: Material, Labour, Variable OH, Fixed OH, Sales (price + volume) variances. Interpret all and recommend management actions.
6. (14 m) Open-ended FS-analysis case: Pick any listed Indian firm; outline the structure of your 5-year analysis, key ratios, peer benchmark, red flags, and IFRS S1-S2 disclosures.
