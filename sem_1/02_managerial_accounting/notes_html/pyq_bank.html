Mid-Sem Paper A — 2 hours, 60 marks Mid-Sem / Closed Book

Section 1 — Short Answer (4 × 4 = 16 marks)

  1. 1 Distinguish financial accounting from management accounting with two examples each. 4 marks
  2. 2 State the dual-aspect concept and explain why it forms the foundation of double-entry bookkeeping. 4 marks
  3. 3 List four red flags that indicate poor quality of financial reporting. 4 marks
  4. 4 State the 5-step revenue recognition model under IFRS 15. 4 marks

Mid-Sem Paper B — 2 hours, 50 marks Mid-Sem / Closed Book

  1. 1 Distinguish capital expenditure from revenue expenditure. Give two examples each. 5 marks
  2. 2 State four limitations of historical-cost accounting in modern asset-light businesses. 5 marks
  3. 3 From the data: PAT ₹4,00,000; Depreciation ₹60,000; Decrease in debtors ₹20,000; Increase in inventory ₹35,000; Decrease in creditors ₹15,000; Tax paid ₹70,000. Compute CFO. 8 marks
  4. 4 ABC Ltd's standalone financials show Revenue ₹500 Cr, COGS ₹300 Cr, Op Exp ₹120 Cr, Interest ₹20 Cr, Tax ₹15 Cr; Equity ₹400 Cr, LT Debt ₹150 Cr, Total Assets ₹700 Cr; Current Assets ₹250 Cr (Inventory ₹80 Cr), Current Liabilities ₹180 Cr. Compute Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, D/E, ICR, ROA, ROE, Net Margin. Interpret. 10 marks
  5. 5 Discuss the major IFRS sustainability standards (IFRS S1 and IFRS S2). What kind of disclosures are required? How do they affect the analysis of asset-heavy vs asset-light firms? 10 marks
  6. 6 "Profit is opinion, cash is fact." Critically evaluate this statement using the relationship between accrual accounting and cash flow. Explain why a profitable firm can still face liquidity issues. 12 marks

Mid-Sem Paper C — Practice Paper (S2-25 aligned) Mid-Sem / Closed Book

  1. 1 Define accrual basis and matching principle with one illustration. 4 marks
  2. 2 State and illustrate the Cost (historical) and Going Concern concepts. 5 marks
  3. 3 Pass journal entries for: (a) Withdrew goods worth ₹5,000 for personal use. (b) Bad debts written off ₹3,000. (c) Provision for doubtful debts created at 5% on debtors ₹2,00,000. (d) Outstanding salary ₹8,000. 8 marks
  4. 4 Reconstruct the Cash Flow Statement (operating section, indirect method) given changes in working capital and net income. 10 marks
  5. 5 Discuss IFRS 13 fair value hierarchy. For each of three illustrative items (listed equity, unlisted bond, custom-built plant), categorise into Level 1/2/3 and explain. 12 marks
  6. 6 Discuss four red flags in financial reporting with real-world examples. How can an analyst use cash flow patterns to detect them? 12 marks

Comprehensive Paper A — 2.5 hours, 100 marks Comprehensive / Open Book

Section A — Short Answer (5 × 4 = 20 marks)

  1. 1 State the DuPont 5-step decomposition of ROE. 4 marks
  2. 2 Define and contrast operating leverage and financial leverage with formulas. 4 marks
  3. 3 Define cash conversion cycle. State three ways to reduce it. 4 marks
  4. 4 List the four kinds of budgets per the handout (Master, Operating, Capital, ZBB). 4 marks
  5. 5 State material price variance and usage variance formulas. 4 marks

Section B — Numericals (4 × 10 = 40 marks)

  1. 6 Compute and interpret all the following ratios for a manufacturing company: Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, Inventory Turnover, DSO, DPO, CCC, D/E, ICR, ROCE, Net Margin, Asset Turnover, ROE. Apply 5-step DuPont. (Data table provided in question.) 10 marks
  2. 7 CVP problem: Selling price ₹250, variable cost ₹150, fixed cost ₹15,00,000. (a) BEP units & revenue. (b) Margin of Safety at 25,000 units sold. (c) Sales required for ₹6,00,000 profit. (d) New BEP if SP drops 5% and VC rises 4%. 10 marks
  3. 8 Make-or-Buy: Component currently made at variable cost ₹80, fixed overhead ₹40 per unit (50% avoidable on outsourcing); offered by vendor at ₹110. Annual demand 10,000 units. (a) Should outsource? (b) What if vendor offers ₹95? 10 marks
  4. 9 Variance analysis: Standard 1 unit needs 2 kg material @ ₹40/kg + 3 hrs labour @ ₹50/hr. Actual production 4,000 units; actual material 8,500 kg @ ₹42/kg; actual labour 11,500 hrs @ ₹52/hr. Compute Material Price, Material Usage, Labour Rate, Labour Efficiency variances. Comment. 10 marks

Section C — Long Answer / Case (2 × 15 = 30 marks)

  1. 10 Case. A pharmaceutical company's 5-year financials show: revenue CAGR 12%, EBITDA margin contracting from 24% to 19%, R&D capitalised under IFRS rising from 8% to 14% of revenue, debt-equity rising from 0.5 to 0.9. (a) Diagnose the company's financial trajectory. (b) Discuss IFRS-related quality concerns (IFRS S1/S2 disclosures, IAS 38 R&D capitalisation). (c) Apply DuPont decomposition to explain ROE trend. 15 marks
  2. 11 Discuss the key components of an effective budgetary control system. Compare incremental, flexible, and zero-based budgeting. Discuss limitations of traditional annual budgeting and the rise of "Beyond Budgeting" / driver-based forecasts. 15 marks

Section D — Application (1 × 10 = 10 marks)

  1. 12 A consumer electronics firm is launching a new product. Help management decide pricing using CVP and cost-plus approaches. Discuss strategic considerations: penetration vs skimming pricing, competitor benchmarking, cost-plus floor. 10 marks

Comprehensive Paper B — 2.5 hours, 80 marks Comprehensive / Open Book

  1. 1 Discuss the IFRS framework with focus on S1, S2, 15, IAS 2, IAS 16. Explain the comparability advantage for a multinational investor. 10 marks
  2. 2 Common-size and comparative statements: From P&L data of two firms in the same sector (provided), prepare common-size and comparative statements; compute year-on-year growth rates. Interpret. 10 marks
  3. 3 Cash Flow Statement preparation (indirect method): from balance sheets of two consecutive years and the P&L of the current year (provided), prepare the Cash Flow Statement and reconcile cash & cash equivalents. 12 marks
  4. 4 Special order decision: A factory at 80% capacity (16,000 units; total capacity 20,000) is offered an export order for 5,000 units at ₹90/unit. Standard SP ₹130, VC ₹70, FC ₹4,00,000. (a) Accept order? (b) What if accepting forces displacement of 1,500 domestic units? (c) Strategic factors beyond cost. 12 marks
  5. 5 Budgeting: Prepare a cash budget for 3 months (Jan-Mar 2026) with given sales, collections (50% cash, 50% next month), purchases, expenses, capex, dividend payments. Identify months requiring overdraft. 12 marks
  6. 6 Mini-case: A startup pivoting from B2C to B2B SaaS. Discuss how its (a) revenue recognition, (b) inventory/COGS, (c) cost structure (CVP), (d) cash flow profile change. Recommend appropriate IFRS standards and KPIs to track. 14 marks
  7. 7 Discuss the limitations of ratio analysis. How does an analyst overcome these in practice? Give examples for cyclical (steel) vs defensive (FMCG) industries. 10 marks

Comprehensive Paper C — Practice Paper (S2-25 aligned) Comprehensive / Open Book

  1. 1 Compute and interpret 5-step DuPont for two firms with identical ROE but different drivers. Which is better quality and why? 8 marks
  2. 2 Discuss the appropriate accounting treatment under IFRS for a software company that capitalises development costs. Reconcile to the US GAAP view. Impact on EBITDA, net income, and asset base. 10 marks
  3. 3 Mini-case: Reliance Industries 5-year ratios provided. Discuss segment-wise (Oil-to-Chemicals, Retail, Jio) profitability, leverage, and capital allocation. Comment on the Jio investment cycle and its impact on consolidated ratios. 12 marks
  4. 4 CVP + product mix: Two products with given SP, VC, machine-hour requirements; total available machine hours and demand limits given. Determine optimal production mix and total profit. 12 marks
  5. 5 Variance analysis comprehensive: Material, Labour, Variable OH, Fixed OH, Sales (price + volume) variances. Interpret all and recommend management actions. 14 marks
  6. 6 Open-ended FS-analysis case: Pick any listed Indian firm; outline the structure of your 5-year analysis, key ratios, peer benchmark, red flags, and IFRS S1-S2 disclosures. 14 marks
