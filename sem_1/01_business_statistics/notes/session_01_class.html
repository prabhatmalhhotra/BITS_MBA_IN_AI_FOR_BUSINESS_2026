4 Types of Datasets Beginner

Unstructured vs Structured

Unstructured

Text, images, videos, social media posts — no fixed format

Structured

Rows and columns, databases, spreadsheets — organised format

Population vs Sample

Population

The entire group you want to study.

  • All 10 IPL teams = Population

Sample

A subset selected for study.

  • 3 IPL teams selected = Sample
Professor's IPL Example

Population: All 10 IPL teams (CSK, MI, RCB, KKR, DC, SRH, PBKS, RR, GT, LSG).
Sample: If you pick just 3 teams (say CSK, MI, RCB) for analysis, that is your sample.
Measurements on the population are Parameters; measurements on the sample are Statistics.

Time Series vs Cross-Sectional

Time Series

Same variable measured over time

  • Monthly sales for 2025
  • Daily stock prices

Cross-Sectional

Multiple variables measured at one point in time

  • Survey of 500 customers today
  • Census data snapshot

5 Measurement Scales — NOIR Intermediate

Professor spent significant time on the four measurement scales. He defined each with clear criteria and Indian examples:

ScaleKey PropertyProfessor's Examples
Nominal (N) Can be categorised but NOT ranked Gender, City, IPL team name, Brand
Ordinal (O) Can be ranked but gaps are not measurable Army ranks, Satisfaction ratings (Poor/Good/Excellent), T-shirt sizes (S/M/L/XL)
Interval (I) Measured with equal gaps but NO true zero Temperature in °C/°F, Calendars (year 0 is arbitrary), Shoe sizes (UK vs US), Product ratings (1-5)
Ratio (R) Measured with equal gaps AND true zero Weight, Height, Income, Sales, BSE/NSE index values

Operations & Techniques by Scale

Professor showed a detailed table of which arithmetic operations and statistical techniques apply to each scale:

Operation / TechniqueNominalOrdinalIntervalRatio
Categorise / CountYesYesYesYes
Rank / OrderNoYesYesYes
Add / Subtract (equal intervals)NoNoYesYes
Multiply / Divide (true zero)NoNoNoYes
ModeYesYesYesYes
MedianNoYesYesYes
MeanNoNoYesYes
Std DeviationNoNoYesYes
Ratios ("twice as much")NoNoNoYes
Exam Tip: Professor emphasised NOIR extensively. Be prepared to classify variables from any given dataset. The key distinction: Interval has no true zero (0°C does not mean "no temperature"), while Ratio has a true zero (₹0 = no income).

6 Applied: Classifying Dataset Columns Intermediate

Professor applied NOIR to real dataset columns from the Goods Sold Invoice data, marking each column as N, O, I, or R:

ColumnScaleReasoning
Invoice NumberN (Nominal)Just an identifier, cannot be ranked meaningfully
Customer NameN (Nominal)Label/category, no inherent order
Product CategoryN (Nominal)Categories like Electronics, Clothing — no natural rank
QuantityR (Ratio)True zero exists (0 = nothing sold), can say "twice as many"
Unit PriceR (Ratio)True zero (₹0 = free), ratios are meaningful
Total AmountR (Ratio)Same reasoning as unit price
DateI (Interval)Equal gaps between days but no "true zero" date
Payment MethodN (Nominal)UPI/Card/Cash — categories, no rank
Classroom Exercise

Professor asked students to practice this classification on the Properties Sold and Amazon TV Feedback datasets as well. For example: Star rating on Amazon = Ordinal (1-5 stars, ranked but gaps not guaranteed equal); Price = Ratio; Brand = Nominal.

Session 1 Class Notes — Key Takeaways

  • Statistics = Descriptive (Summarise, Ch 1-2) + Inferential (Estimation, Hypothesis, Correlation, Regression, Ch 3-12)
  • Statistics is the foundation for business (13+ applications listed), other fields, and AI/ML techniques
  • Professor showed 3 real datasets: Properties Sold, Amazon TV Feedback, Goods Sold Invoices
  • Dataset types: Unstructured vs Structured; Population vs Sample (IPL teams example); Time Series vs Cross-Sectional
  • NOIR scales: Nominal (categorise) < Ordinal (rank) < Interval (measure, no true zero) < Ratio (true zero)
  • Each scale enables progressively more arithmetic operations and statistical techniques
  • Practice: classify every column in a dataset as N, O, I, or R
  • Sampling methods deferred to Session 7 (Chapter 7: Sampling Distributions)
