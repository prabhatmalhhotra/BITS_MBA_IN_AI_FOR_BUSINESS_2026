Two-Sample Tests and ANOVA

Comparing means across two or more groups — from A/B tests to multi-city performance benchmarks.

T1 Chapter 10 (Levine)Comprehensive Exam~55 min read
Real-World Problem

Zomato A/B test: Does the new app UI increase order value?

Zomato rolls out a redesigned checkout page to 5,000 randomly selected users (Group B) while 5,000 others keep the old design (Group A). After two weeks, Group A has mean order value ₹380 and Group B has ₹395. Is this ₹15 difference real or just noise?

This is a two-sample t-test. If they also tested a third design variant, that would need ANOVA (comparing 3+ groups simultaneously).

1 Two Independent Means Intermediate

Pooled-Variance t-Test (Equal Variances Assumed)

t = (X̄₁ - X̄₂) / √[Sᵩ²(1/n₁ + 1/n₂)]

where Sᵩ² = [(n₁-1)S₁² + (n₂-1)S₂²] / (n₁ + n₂ - 2)

df = n₁ + n₂ - 2

Welch t-Test (Unequal Variances)

t = (X̄₁ - X̄₂) / √[S₁²/n₁ + S₂²/n₂]

df calculated via Welch-Satterthwaite formula (use software)

Equal Variances (Pooled)

  • Use F-test to check (Section 4)
  • Pool both sample variances
  • df = n₁ + n₂ - 2
  • More powerful if assumption holds

Unequal Variances (Welch)

  • Safer default choice
  • Separate variance estimates
  • df from Satterthwaite approximation
  • Robust, always valid

2 Two Related (Paired) Means Intermediate

When the same subjects are measured twice (before/after, left/right, husband/wife), use a paired t-test.

Dᵢ = X₁ᵢ - X₂ᵢ   (compute differences)

t = D̄ / (S_D / √n)     df = n - 1
Daily Life Example

Weight loss programme: 20 people weigh themselves before and after a 3-month programme. The paired t-test checks whether the mean difference (before - after) is significantly greater than 0. You compute each person's individual weight change (D) and test if D̄ is significantly different from 0.

3 Two Independent Proportions Intermediate

Z = (p̂₁ - p̂₂) / √[p̄(1-p̄)(1/n₁ + 1/n₂)]

where p̄ = (X₁ + X₂) / (n₁ + n₂)   (pooled proportion)

4 F-Test for Two Variances Advanced

F = S₁² / S₂²    (larger variance in numerator)

df₁ = n₁ - 1,   df₂ = n₂ - 1

H₀: σ₁² = σ₂². Reject if F > F-critical. This test is sensitive to non-normality, so use with caution.

5 One-Way ANOVA Advanced

Tests whether 3+ group means are equal: H₀: μ₁ = μ₂ = ... = μₖ.

F = MS_Between / MS_Within = (SSB/(k-1)) / (SSW/(n-k))

Assumptions: Independence, Normality within each group, Equal variances (homoscedasticity).

Post-Hoc: Tukey HSD

If ANOVA rejects H₀, it only says "at least one pair differs." Tukey's Honestly Significant Difference test identifies which specific pairs are different.

Trending Example (2024)

Swiggy comparing delivery times across 4 cities: Using one-way ANOVA, Swiggy tested whether mean delivery time differs among Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, and Hyderabad. The F-test was significant (p < 0.001), and Tukey HSD revealed Bangalore was significantly slower than the other three, leading to targeted operational improvements.

Exam Tip: Know how to construct and interpret the ANOVA table. A common question gives you SSB, SSW, k, and n, and asks you to compute F and decide. Also, know the difference between independent and paired designs — the exam often gives an ambiguous scenario where you must identify which test to use.

Session 10 — Key Takeaways

  • Independent means: Pooled t (equal var) or Welch t (unequal var); df differs
  • Paired means: Compute differences D, then one-sample t on D̄
  • Two proportions: Z-test with pooled proportion p̄
  • F-test: compares two variances; larger S² in numerator
  • ANOVA: F = MSB/MSW; tests k means simultaneously; SST = SSB + SSW
  • Tukey HSD: post-hoc test to find which pairs differ after significant ANOVA
  • Assumptions: independence, normality, equal variances
