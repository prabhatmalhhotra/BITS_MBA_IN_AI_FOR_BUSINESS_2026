Confidence Interval Estimation

Moving from point estimates to ranges that quantify our uncertainty. "The average is 32 minutes" becomes "we are 95% confident the average is between 30.5 and 33.5 minutes."

T1 Chapter 8 (Levine) Mid-Sem Syllabus (Last Topic) ~50 min read
Real-World Problem

Groww wants to know: What is the average monthly SIP amount of our users?

Groww has 8 crore registered users. They sample 500 users and find the average monthly SIP is ₹4,200 with SD = ₹1,800. The board asks: "What is the true average for all users, and how confident are you?"

A confidence interval answers: "We are 95% confident the true average monthly SIP is between ₹4,042 and ₹4,358." This is more honest and useful than just saying "₹4,200."

1 CI for the Mean (σ Unknown) Intermediate

In practice, we almost never know the population standard deviation σ. We estimate it with the sample standard deviation S, and use the t-distribution instead of Z.

CI for μ:   X̄ ± t(α/2, n-1) × S / √n

The t-Distribution

  • Similar to normal but with heavier tails (more uncertainty from estimating σ)
  • Defined by degrees of freedom (df = n - 1)
  • As df increases, t approaches Z (for df > 30, very similar to Z)
  • t-critical is always larger than z-critical, giving wider CIs (more honest about uncertainty)

Worked Example

Sample: n = 50, X̄ = ₹4,200, S = ₹1,800. For 95% CI:

StepValue
Degrees of freedomdf = 50 - 1 = 49
t-critical (95%, df=49)t = 2.010 (from t-table)
Standard errorSE = 1800 / √50 = 254.56
Margin of errorE = 2.010 × 254.56 = 511.66
95% CI₹3,688.34 to ₹4,711.66
Daily Life Example

If you time your morning commute for 30 days and get mean = 35 min, SD = 8 min, the 95% CI is 35 ± 2.045 × 8/√30 = 35 ± 2.99 = 32.01 to 37.99 minutes. You can tell your boss: "I am 95% confident my commute takes between 32 and 38 minutes."

2 CI for the Proportion Intermediate

CI for π:   p̂ ± z(α/2) × √[p̂(1-p̂)/n]

Note: we use Z (not t) for proportions, because proportions follow a different sampling distribution.

Worked Example

Survey of n = 400 Flipkart users: 280 say they prefer free delivery over discounts. p̂ = 280/400 = 0.70. For 95% CI:

StepValue
z-critical (95%)z = 1.96
Standard errorSE = √(0.70 × 0.30 / 400) = 0.0229
Margin of errorE = 1.96 × 0.0229 = 0.0449
95% CI0.655 to 0.745 (65.5% to 74.5%)

3 Determining Sample Size Advanced

For Estimating a Mean

n = (z(α/2) × σ / e)²

where e = desired margin of error. If σ is unknown, use a pilot study estimate or prior knowledge.

For Estimating a Proportion

n = z(α/2)² × π(1-π) / e²

If π is unknown, use π = 0.5 (maximises n, giving the most conservative sample size).

Example

Want to estimate mean delivery time within ±2 minutes (e = 2), with 95% confidence, and σ estimated at 12 minutes:

n = (1.96 × 12 / 2)² = (11.76)² = 138.3 → round up to n = 139
Exam Tip: Always round UP sample size to the next whole number (138.3 becomes 139, not 138). Also remember: CI formula for mean uses t (sigma unknown), CI for proportion uses Z. The most common error is using Z for the mean when sigma is unknown.
Trending Example (2024)

TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India) determines sample sizes for its quarterly QoS (Quality of Service) audits of Jio, Airtel, and Vodafone-Idea. For call drop rates (a proportion), they use the sample size formula with π = 0.5 and e = 2%, requiring approximately 2,400 test calls per operator per circle — a direct application of the formula above.

4 Common Interpretation Pitfalls Beginner

CORRECT Interpretation

"If we repeated this sampling process many times, 95% of the resulting intervals would contain the true μ."

The confidence is in the procedure, not in any single interval.

WRONG Interpretation

"There is a 95% probability that μ is in this interval."

μ is a fixed number — it is either in the interval or not. There is no probability about it.

Daily Life Example

Think of it like a ring-toss game. The ring (interval) is thrown at the peg (μ). We say "95% of my throws will land around the peg." After one throw, the ring is either on the peg or not — you cannot say "there is a 95% chance the peg is inside this ring."

Session 8 — Key Takeaways (Last Mid-Sem Topic!)

  • CI for mean (σ unknown): X̄ ± t(α/2, n-1) × S/√n — uses t-distribution
  • CI for proportion: p̂ ± z(α/2) × √[p̂(1-p̂)/n] — uses Z
  • t-distribution: heavier tails than normal; approaches Z as df increases
  • Sample size for mean: n = (zσ/e)²; for proportion: n = z²π(1-π)/e²
  • Always round up sample size
  • Use π = 0.5 when proportion is unknown (conservative)
  • Correct interpretation: 95% of intervals from repeated samples contain μ
