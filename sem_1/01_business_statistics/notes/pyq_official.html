Question 3 5 marks

In a large IT company, every applicant for the entry-level jobs undergoes two rounds of the selection process — a written test and an interview round. Historical data shows:

  • P(passes written test) = 50%
  • P(passes interview round) = 60%
  • P(passes both written test and interview round) = 40%
  1. If a candidate has passed the interview round, what is the probability that he also passed the written test?
  2. Are the written test and interview round independent — justify your answer.
Important: Show every calculation/working.

Question 5 5 marks

A passenger car manufacturer buys car batteries from two suppliers, Supplier-A and Supplier-B.

  • Supplier-A supplies 65% of the batteries; Supplier-B supplies the remaining requirement.
  • Supplier-A: 99% Good quality, 2% Bad quality (as stated in paper).
  • Supplier-B: 95% Good quality, 5% Bad quality.

The batteries from both suppliers are stocked in the same store. If a battery is selected at random:

  1. What is the probability that it is of Good quality?
  2. What is the probability that the battery has been supplied by Supplier-A given that it is of Good quality?
  3. What is the probability that the battery has been supplied by Supplier-B given that it is of Bad quality?
Important: Show all calculations/procedure/approach/formula. (Bayes' Theorem)
