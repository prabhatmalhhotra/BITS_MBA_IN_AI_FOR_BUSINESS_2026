Non-Parametric Tests

When data is ordinal, sample sizes are small, or normality assumptions fail — non-parametric tests come to the rescue.

Class NotesComprehensive Exam~50 min read
Class Notes AI Notes
Real-World Problem

MakeMyTrip: Do hotel ratings differ between North and South India?

Hotel ratings are ordinal (1-5 stars, not truly interval). With 15 hotels in each region, can you use a t-test? Not safely — the data is ordinal and sample is small. The Mann-Whitney U test is your non-parametric alternative.

Non-parametric tests make fewer assumptions about data distribution, making them robust for ordinal data, small samples, and non-normal distributions.

1 When to Use Non-Parametric Tests Beginner

  • Data is ordinal (rankings, ratings, Likert scales)
  • Normality assumptions are violated and sample is too small for CLT
  • Data has extreme outliers that would distort parametric tests
  • You want a test that is robust regardless of distribution

Trade-off: Non-parametric tests are less powerful than parametric ones when parametric assumptions are actually met. They use ranks instead of actual values, losing some information.

2 Sign Test Intermediate

Tests whether the median of a population equals a hypothesised value. For paired data, tests if the median difference is zero.

Count positive differences (above median) and negative differences (below median).
Under H₀: number of positives ~ Binomial(n, 0.5)
Ties are excluded from n.
Daily Life Example

A restaurant claims median wait time is 10 minutes. You visit 20 times: 14 times you waited more than 10 min, 6 times less. Is 14/20 significantly different from 50%? The sign test answers this using the binomial distribution with p = 0.5.

3 Wilcoxon Rank-Sum / Mann-Whitney U Test Intermediate

Non-parametric alternative to the two-sample t-test for independent groups.

1. Combine both samples and rank all observations (1 = smallest)
2. Sum ranks for each group: T₁ and T₂
3. Compare to critical values or compute U statistic

U₁ = n₁n₂ + n₁(n₁+1)/2 - T₁

H₀: The two populations have the same distribution (same median). Reject if rank sum is unusually high or low.

4 Kruskal-Wallis Test Advanced

Non-parametric alternative to one-way ANOVA for 3+ independent groups.

H = [12 / n(n+1)] × Σ (Tᵢ² / nᵢ) - 3(n+1)

Under H₀: H ~ χ² with k - 1 df

Where Tᵢ = sum of ranks in group i, nᵢ = size of group i, n = total sample size, k = number of groups.

5 Spearman Rank Correlation Intermediate

rᵧ = 1 - 6Σdᵢ² / [n(n² - 1)]

where dᵢ = rank(Xᵢ) - rank(Yᵢ)

Measures monotonic (not just linear) association. Works on ordinal data. Robust to outliers. Range: [-1, +1].

Trending Example (2024)

Glassdoor India company rankings: Researchers studying whether Glassdoor rating (ordinal, 1-5) correlates with employee attrition rate used Spearman rank correlation. Pearson r would be inappropriate since ratings are ordinal. Spearman rᵧ = -0.68, confirming a strong negative monotonic relationship: higher-rated companies have lower attrition.

Session 12 — Key Takeaways

  • Use non-parametric when data is ordinal, non-normal, or has small samples
  • Sign test: one-sample/paired median test using binomial with p = 0.5
  • Mann-Whitney U: two independent samples, uses combined ranks
  • Kruskal-Wallis: 3+ groups, H ~ χ² with k-1 df
  • Spearman rᵧ = 1 - 6Σd²/[n(n²-1)]; measures monotonic association on ranks
  • Non-parametric tests are less powerful but more robust
  • Python: scipy.stats.mannwhitneyu, wilcoxon, kruskal, spearmanr
