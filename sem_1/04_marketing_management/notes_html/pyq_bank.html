Mid-Sem Paper A — 2 hours, 60 marks Mid-Sem / Closed Book

Section 1 — Short Answer (5 × 4 = 20 marks)

  1. 1 Define marketing per Kotler. State the difference between marketing concept and selling concept. 4 marks
  2. 2 List the 4 Ps and the additional 3 Ps for services. Give one example of each. 4 marks
  3. 3 State the 5 stages of consumer decision-making process. 4 marks
  4. 4 Explain effective segmentation criteria with one example each. 4 marks
  5. 5 Define Customer Value Proposition (CVP). State its components. 4 marks

Section 2 — Application / Short Essay (3 × 8 = 24 marks)

  1. 6 Discuss how Maslow's need hierarchy can be used to design marketing strategy for (a) bottled water, (b) electric two-wheelers, (c) family planning services. 8 marks
  2. 7 Explain Porter's 5 forces. Apply to the Indian premium smartphone market. 8 marks
  3. 8 Discuss the 4 distinctive characteristics of services. Use SERVQUAL (RATER) to analyse a hospital chain in your city. 8 marks

Section 3 — Long Answer / Mini-Case (1 × 16 = 16 marks)

  1. 9 Mini-case — Mahindra XYLO (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 9). Discuss STP decisions in launching XYLO. What worked, what could have been improved? Recommend a refreshed positioning. 16 marks

Mid-Sem Paper B — 2 hours, 50 marks Mid-Sem / Closed Book

  1. 1 Distinguish needs, wants, and demand with one example each. State three demand states (Kotler). 5 marks
  2. 2 Discuss 5-stage consumer buying decision process. Explain cognitive dissonance with one example. 5 marks
  3. 3 Compare consumer (B2C) and industrial (B2B) marketing on five dimensions. 8 marks
  4. 4 Discuss bases for consumer market segmentation. Apply to Indian quick-service restaurant (QSR) industry. 8 marks
  5. 5 Explain the 5 product levels (Kotler). Apply to a smartwatch. 10 marks
  6. 6 Mini-case — Kellogg's Cornflakes in India (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 10). Critically analyse Kellogg's positioning challenges. Apply POP/POD. Recommend a refined positioning statement. 14 marks

Mid-Sem Paper C — Practice Paper (S2-25 aligned) Mid-Sem / Closed Book

  1. 1 Define market demand and demand forecasting. List three forecasting methods. 4 marks
  2. 2 Personas: define and explain why they help marketers. Cite the HSTalk by Dr. Annmarie Hanlon. 5 marks
  3. 3 Discuss the EKB model of consumer behaviour. Tie to Pearson Indian Cases Case 6 (Consumer Behaviour: Wide Facts and Deep Implications). 8 marks
  4. 4 Discuss Value Proposition Canvas with components. Build one for a fintech BNPL service for Tier-2 cities. 8 marks
  5. 5 Apply Product Life Cycle to Indian two-wheeler market. Map current products of Hero, Bajaj, TVS. 10 marks
  6. 6 Mini-case — MTR Foods: A Marketing Marvel (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 1). Discuss services vs goods marketing-mix transition. Apply 7Ps. 15 marks

Comprehensive Paper A — 2.5 hours, 100 marks Comprehensive / Open Book

Section A — Short Answer (5 × 4 = 20 marks)

  1. 1 Define marketing communications mix (Kotler's 8 modes). 4 marks
  2. 2 State 5 Ms of advertising. 4 marks
  3. 3 Distinguish penetration vs skimming pricing. 4 marks
  4. 4 List 4 channel functions and define VMS types. 4 marks
  5. 5 State Indian marketing regulators (any 4) and one role each. 4 marks

Section B — Numericals / Application (4 × 10 = 40 marks)

  1. 6 Develop a pricing decision for a smart-home robot vacuum launching in India. Apply Kotler's 6-step process. Recommend penetration or skimming with rationale. Discuss likely competitor response. 10 marks
  2. 7 Develop an IMC plan for a Tier-2 restaurant chain entering Tier-1 cities. Cover target audience, objectives, message, channels mix, budget approach, and measurement. 10 marks
  3. 8 Design a multi-channel strategy for a D2C beauty brand expanding to offline. Address omnichannel, channel conflict, and conflict-resolution. Reference HSTalk on Omni-channel marketing. 10 marks
  4. 9 Apply Webster & Wind's buying-centre concept to a B2B SaaS sale to an Indian bank. Identify all roles. Recommend an account-based marketing plan. 10 marks

Section C — Long Answer / Case (2 × 15 = 30 marks)

  1. 10 Case — Oreo in India (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 17). Critically analyse how Oreo built the brand using IMC. Apply 5 Ms of advertising and discuss IMC integration across channels. 15 marks
  2. 11 Case — Amazon India (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 21). Discuss how Amazon adapted its global retail playbook. Apply Ansoff matrix, BCG matrix, and STP. What lessons for global brands entering India? 15 marks

Section D — Application (1 × 10 = 10 marks)

  1. 12 Reflect on your Cesim Marketing Simulation experience (1 practice + 3 actual rounds). Discuss your team's strategy, decisions that worked, lessons applicable to real marketing. Tie to Kotler frameworks. 10 marks

Comprehensive Paper B — 2.5 hours, 80 marks Comprehensive / Open Book

  1. 1 Discuss Kotler's 8-mode communication mix. For an FMCG launch, design a mix and justify. 10 marks
  2. 2 Compare AIDA and Hierarchy-of-effects models. Discuss budgeting methods (affordable, % of sales, competitive parity, objective-and-task) — recommend the best with reasoning. 10 marks
  3. 3 Channel design: A premium audio brand wants to enter India. Recommend channel structure (own retail + online + modern trade + speciality). Address conflict and omnichannel integration. 12 marks
  4. 4 Discuss e-commerce, m-commerce, and quick-commerce evolution in India. Implications for traditional retail. 12 marks
  5. 5 Discuss ASCI and CCPA roles in regulating Indian marketing. Highlight three recent areas (greenwashing, influencer disclosure, AI-generated content). Recommend a brand self-regulation framework. 12 marks
  6. 6 Industrial (B2B) marketing — discuss 5 unique characteristics. Apply buying-centre concept to a heavy-equipment sale (CNC machine to MSME). 12 marks
  7. 7 Strategic Marketing Plan — for an Indian D2C jewellery brand. Cover SWOT, STP, marketing mix, action programs, budget, control. Apply Ansoff matrix. 12 marks

Exam Tactics

Comprehensive (open book, 2.5 hrs):
• Tab Kotler 16e by chapter (Ch 1, 2, 3, 6, 7, 8, 11, 12, 13, 15, 16).
• Carry: this revision sheet + summaries of all 5 mandatory case discussions + Cesim notes.
• For HSTalks tie-in questions, mention specific HSTalk title.
• Time = 1.5 min/mark; case answers should follow Background → Diagnosis (using framework) → Recommendations → Implementation steps.
• Always cite Indian + global examples for variety.

Mid-Sem (closed book, 2 hrs):
• Memorise: Kotler's marketing definition, 4Ps + 7Ps, consumer behaviour stages, STP framework, segmentation criteria, CVP components, POP/POD, PLC stages, SERVQUAL.
• For each mandatory case (XYLO, Kellogg's, MTR), prep a 1-pager: context → marketing problem → decisions → outcomes → lessons.
