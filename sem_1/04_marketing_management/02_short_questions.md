# Marketing Management — Short Questions (with Answers)

> 35 short answers, 2–5 marks each.

---

## Set A — Core concepts

**Q1. Differentiate need, want and demand.**
Need = basic human requirement. Want = need shaped by culture/personality. Demand = want backed by ability and willingness to pay.

**Q2. Define marketing in one sentence.**
Marketing is the art and science of choosing target markets and getting, keeping and growing customers through creating, delivering and communicating superior customer value (Kotler).

**Q3. Differentiate selling and marketing.**
Selling is product-centric, inside-out, short-term, transaction-driven. Marketing is customer-centric, outside-in, long-term, relationship-driven.

**Q4. State four dimensions of holistic marketing.**
Relationship, Integrated, Internal, Performance.

**Q5. Define value (in marketing).**
Value = Total benefits ÷ Total costs (functional, emotional, social, monetary, time, effort).

## Set B — Environment

**Q6. Expand PESTEL.**
Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal.

**Q7. List Porter's Five Forces.**
Threat of new entrants, supplier power, buyer power, threat of substitutes, industry rivalry.

**Q8. State three macro-environmental forces affecting Indian e-commerce in 2025.**
DPDP Act 2023 (data privacy regulation), UPI penetration, GenAI-driven personalisation.

---

## Set C — Research and consumer behaviour

**Q9. Differentiate qualitative and quantitative research.**
Qualitative: depth, why behind behaviour (FGDs, IDIs). Quantitative: scale, generalisability (surveys, A/B tests).

**Q10. State the five stages of buyer decision process.**
Need recognition → Information search → Evaluation of alternatives → Purchase decision → Post-purchase behaviour.

**Q11. List four influences on consumer behaviour.**
Cultural (culture, sub-culture, social class), Social (reference groups, family, role/status), Personal (age, occupation, lifestyle), Psychological (motivation, perception, learning, attitudes).

**Q12. Define ZMOT.**
Zero Moment of Truth (Google) — the pre-shopping research phase, mostly on mobile, where customers form intent.

**Q13. State the Theory of Planned Behaviour equation.**
Behavioural intention = Attitude + Subjective Norms + Perceived Behavioural Control. Predicts adoption of new behaviours.

---

## Set D — STP

**Q14. State five bases of segmentation.**
Demographic, Geographic, Psychographic, Behavioural, Technographic (B2B).

**Q15. List five criteria for effective segmentation.**
Measurable, Accessible, Substantial, Differentiable, Actionable (MASDA).

**Q16. Differentiate undifferentiated and differentiated targeting.**
Undifferentiated = one offering for all (mass marketing). Differentiated = different offerings for different segments.

**Q17. Define perceptual map.**
A 2D visualisation of customer perception of brands across two attributes (e.g., price × quality). Used to identify positioning gaps.

**Q18. Write a positioning statement template.**
"For [target customer], [brand] is the [category] that [single most important benefit] because [reason to believe]."

---

## Set E — Product

**Q19. State the five product levels (Kotler).**
Core benefit, Basic, Expected, Augmented, Potential.

**Q20. Differentiate convenience, shopping and speciality goods.**
Convenience: bought frequently, low effort (toothpaste). Shopping: comparison effort (electronics). Speciality: brand loyalty, willing to travel/wait (Apple, Hermès).

**Q21. Define product mix width, length, depth.**
Width = number of product lines. Length = total items. Depth = variants per item (e.g., flavours, sizes).

**Q22. State four stages of PLC and corresponding strategies.**
Introduction (awareness), Growth (brand-building), Maturity (differentiate), Decline (harvest/reposition/divest).

**Q23. List the eight stages of NPD.**
Idea generation → Screening → Concept testing → Marketing strategy → Business analysis → Product development → Test marketing → Commercialisation.

**Q24. State Aaker's five dimensions of brand equity.**
Brand awareness, Perceived quality, Brand associations, Brand loyalty, Other proprietary assets.

**Q25. Describe Keller's CBBE pyramid.**
Bottom-up: Brand salience → Brand performance + imagery → Brand judgements + feelings → Brand resonance.

---

## Set F — Pricing & Place

**Q26. Differentiate skimming and penetration pricing.**
Skimming: high initial price, lower later (iPhone). Penetration: low launch price for mass adoption (Jio).

**Q27. Write the formula for price elasticity.**
PED = %ΔQ / %ΔP. >1 elastic, <1 inelastic.

**Q28. Define three intensities of distribution.**
Intensive (max outlets — FMCG), Selective (moderate — apparel), Exclusive (single per area — luxury).

**Q29. List three vertical-marketing systems.**
Corporate (single ownership across stages), Contractual (franchising, voluntary chains), Administered (channel leader coordinates without ownership).

**Q30. Define D2C marketing.**
Direct-to-Consumer — brand sells through its own channels (web/app/store) bypassing wholesalers and retailers. Higher margins, direct data, control over experience.

---

## Set G — Promotion / Services / Strategy

**Q31. Expand AIDA.**
Attention → Interest → Desire → Action.

**Q32. Define GRP and CPM.**
GRP = Reach × Frequency. CPM = cost per thousand impressions.

**Q33. State the IIVP characteristics of services.**
Intangibility, Inseparability, Variability, Perishability.

**Q34. List the seven Ps of services marketing.**
Product, Price, Place, Promotion, People, Process, Physical evidence.

**Q35. Define CLV (Customer Lifetime Value).**
Net present value of total cash flows from a customer over their relationship lifetime, minus acquisition cost.
