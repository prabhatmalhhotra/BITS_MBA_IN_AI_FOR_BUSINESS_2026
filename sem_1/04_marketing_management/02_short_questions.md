# Marketing Management — Short Questions (handout-aligned)

> Aligned to `MBACC ZG503` 16-session plan. Mid-Sem (closed book, 2 hrs, 30 %): Sessions 1-8. Comprehensive (open book, 2.5 hrs, 40 %): all 16.
> Each Q has a 1-3 line ideal answer. Primary text: Kotler, Keller, Chernev, Sheth & Shaineesh, *Marketing Management*, Pearson 16/e (2022).

## Session 1 — Introduction to Marketing
1. **Define Marketing (Kotler).** Activity, set of institutions, and processes for creating, communicating, delivering and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners and society at large.
2. **Distinguish marketing concept vs selling concept.** Selling = inside-out, push existing products. Marketing = outside-in, identify customer needs and design offerings around them.
3. **State the 4 Ps and 7 Ps of marketing.** 4 Ps: Product, Price, Place, Promotion. 7 Ps add: People, Process, Physical evidence (for services).
4. **What are the "New Realities" Kotler highlights?** Technology, globalisation, social responsibility shifting marketing toward digital, holistic, sustainable, customer-centric.
5. **Difference: B2C vs B2B marketing.** B2C = mass market, emotional/branded, short cycle. B2B = derived demand, fewer/larger buyers, rational, longer cycle, multi-stakeholder.
6. **Define Customer Value Proposition (CVP).** Bundle of benefits and costs the firm offers to a target segment that differentiates it from competitors.

## Session 2 — Need, Wants, Demand
7. **Distinguish needs, wants, demand.** Need = basic requirement (food). Want = need shaped by culture/personality (pizza). Demand = want backed by ability to pay.
8. **State Maslow's 5 needs hierarchy.** Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-actualisation; used to identify product/service positioning.
9. **State 5 demand states (Kotler).** Negative, No demand, Latent, Declining, Irregular, Full, Overfull, Unwholesome.
10. **Define market demand and demand forecasting.** Total volume of a product/service that would be bought by a defined customer group, in a defined area, time period, marketing environment, marketing program. Forecasting = expected demand at specified marketing effort.
11. **List demand-estimation methods.** Survey of buyers' intentions, expert opinion, sales-force composite, time-series analysis, regression, market test.

## Session 3 — Consumer Behaviour & Competitive Behaviour
12. **State 4 factors influencing consumer behaviour.** Cultural, Social (reference groups, family, roles), Personal (age, occupation, lifestyle), Psychological (motivation, perception, learning, beliefs).
13. **Stages in consumer buying decision process.** Need recognition → Information search → Evaluation of alternatives → Purchase decision → Post-purchase behaviour.
14. **What is Cognitive Dissonance in post-purchase?** Buyer's anxiety about whether purchase was right; firms reduce via reassurance communication, return policy.
15. **Define Engel-Kollat-Blackwell (EKB) model.** Comprehensive 5-stage decision-process model integrating environmental + individual + decision-process variables.
16. **Define Porter's 5 forces.** Industry rivalry, threat of new entrants, threat of substitutes, bargaining power of suppliers, bargaining power of buyers.
17. **Strategic groups vs competitors.** Strategic group = firms following similar strategy on key dimensions. Competitor = direct rivals targeting same buyers with similar product.

## Session 4 — Segmentation & Targeting
18. **STP framework.** Segmentation → Targeting → Positioning.
19. **Bases for consumer segmentation.** Geographic, Demographic, Psychographic, Behavioural.
20. **Bases for B2B segmentation.** Demographic (industry, size), Operating variables, Purchasing approach, Situational factors, Personal characteristics.
21. **Requirements for effective segmentation.** Measurable, Substantial, Accessible, Differentiable, Actionable.
22. **3 targeting strategies.** Undifferentiated (mass), Differentiated (multi-segment), Concentrated (niche), Micromarketing (local/individual).
23. **Define personas.** Detailed semi-fictional profile of ideal customer in a segment based on research; humanises segmentation.

## Session 5 — Customer Value Proposition
24. **Components of a CVP statement.** For (target customer), who (need/problem), our (product/service) is a (category) that (key benefit), unlike (competitor), our offering (differentiator).
25. **Define Value Proposition Canvas.** Tool linking customer profile (jobs, pains, gains) with value map (products/services, pain relievers, gain creators).
26. **Customer-perceived value.** Difference between total customer benefits (product, services, personal, image) and total customer costs (monetary, time, energy, psychic).

## Session 6 — Positioning
27. **Define brand positioning.** Act of designing the company's offering and image to occupy a distinctive place in the minds of the target market.
28. **Components of a positioning statement.** Target segment, Frame of reference (category), Point of difference (USP), Reason to believe.
29. **Difference: POP vs POD.** POP = Points of Parity (must-haves to be considered); POD = Points of Difference (unique advantages).
30. **State 4 positioning errors (Kotler).** Underpositioning, Overpositioning, Confused positioning, Doubtful positioning.
31. **Perceptual map.** Visual diagram of consumer perceptions of brands on key attributes; reveals gaps and competitive clustering.

## Session 7 — Product Management & New Product Introduction
32. **Define product (Kotler).** Anything offered to a market for attention, acquisition, use, consumption that satisfies a want or need.
33. **Five product levels (Kotler).** Core benefit → Basic product → Expected → Augmented → Potential.
34. **Product Life Cycle (PLC) stages.** Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Decline. Each has distinct sales, profit, competition, marketing strategy.
35. **State steps in NPD process.** Idea generation → Idea screening → Concept development & testing → Marketing strategy → Business analysis → Product development → Test marketing → Commercialisation.
36. **Define differentiation.** Designing a set of meaningful differences to distinguish the firm's offering from competitors'; can be product, service, personnel, channel, image.

## Session 8 — Services Marketing & Marketing Mix
37. **Distinctive characteristics of services.** Intangibility, Inseparability, Variability (heterogeneity), Perishability.
38. **Service quality model — SERVQUAL 5 dimensions.** Reliability, Assurance, Tangibles, Empathy, Responsiveness (RATER).
39. **Define service blueprint.** Visual map of customer journey and the front-stage/back-stage processes that deliver the service.
40. **Service-Profit Chain.** Internal service quality → Employee satisfaction → Productivity → Customer value → Customer satisfaction & loyalty → Revenue and profit.

## Session 9 — Pricing Strategies
41. **6 steps in setting price (Kotler).** Pricing objective → Demand → Cost → Competitor analysis → Pricing method → Final price.
42. **Pricing methods (any 4).** Markup pricing, Target-return pricing, Perceived-value pricing, Value pricing (EDLP), Going-rate pricing, Auction-type, Market-based.
43. **Penetration vs Skimming pricing.** Penetration = low price to gain volume/share fast (price-sensitive market). Skimming = high initial price to skim premium segment, lower over time (innovative product, low elasticity).
44. **Price elasticity of demand formula.** % Δ Quantity / % Δ Price; |E| > 1 elastic, < 1 inelastic.
45. **Define EDLP and High-Low pricing.** EDLP = consistently low (Walmart). High-Low = higher base with frequent promotions (department stores).

## Session 10 — Pricing Tactics & Competitive Response
46. **Initiating a price cut — when?** Excess capacity, price war defence, drive volume.
47. **Initiating a price increase — when?** Cost inflation, over-demand, repositioning premium.
48. **State competitor response options to a price challenge.** Maintain, Counter-cut, Improve perceived value, Launch fighter brand.
49. **Define geographic pricing.** Different prices for different geographies; FOB origin, uniform delivered, zone pricing, freight-absorption.
50. **Define discount and allowance pricing.** Cash, Quantity, Functional (trade), Seasonal, Trade-in allowances, Promotional allowances.

## Session 11 — Marketing Communications
51. **Marketing communications mix (8 modes per Kotler).** Advertising, Sales Promotion, Events & Experiences, PR & Publicity, Direct & Database Marketing, Online & Social Media, Mobile, Personal Selling.
52. **Macro-model of communication.** Sender → Encoding → Message/Media → Decoding → Receiver → Response → Feedback (Noise affects all stages).
53. **AIDA model.** Awareness → Interest → Desire → Action.
54. **Hierarchy of effects model.** Awareness → Knowledge → Liking → Preference → Conviction → Purchase.
55. **State steps in developing effective communication.** Identify target audience → Set objectives → Design message → Choose channels → Set budget → Decide mix → Measure results → Manage IMC.
56. **Major budgeting methods.** Affordable, % of sales, Competitive parity, Objective-and-task.

## Session 12 — Advertising & Sales Promotion
57. **5 Ms of advertising (Kotler).** Mission, Money, Message, Media, Measurement.
58. **State three categories of advertising objectives.** Inform, Persuade, Remind (also Reinforce).
59. **Reach vs Frequency vs Impact.** Reach = % audience exposed at least once; Frequency = avg times exposed; Impact = qualitative value of exposure.
60. **Sales promotion tools — consumer (any 4).** Coupons, Samples, Cash refunds, Price packs, Premiums, Loyalty programs, Contests, POP displays.
61. **Sales promotion tools — trade (any 3).** Allowances, Free goods, Co-op advertising, Sales contests.
62. **Define IMC (Integrated Marketing Communication).** Coordinating all communication channels to deliver a clear, consistent, compelling message about the organisation and its products.

## Session 13 — Channels of Distribution
63. **Define marketing channel.** Set of interdependent organisations involved in making a product or service available for use or consumption.
64. **Functions of channel members.** Information, Promotion, Negotiation, Ordering, Financing, Risk-taking, Physical possession, Payment, Title transfer.
65. **Levels of channels.** 0 (direct), 1 (one intermediary), 2, 3 levels.
66. **Channel design decisions.** Analyse customer needs → Set channel objectives → Identify alternatives → Evaluate alternatives.
67. **VMS (Vertical Marketing System) types.** Corporate, Administered, Contractual (incl. Franchise).
68. **Channel conflict types.** Vertical (between levels), Horizontal (same level), Multichannel.

## Session 14 — Channel Trends & E/M-Commerce
69. **Define omni-channel.** Seamless integrated customer experience across all channels (online, offline, mobile, social) with consistent brand and inventory.
70. **Disintermediation vs Reintermediation.** Disintermediation = bypassing intermediaries (Dell direct). Reintermediation = new intermediaries replace old (e.g., Amazon vs traditional bookstore).
71. **E-commerce vs M-commerce.** E-commerce = transactions via internet on any device. M-commerce = subset, transactions via mobile devices/apps.
72. **Last-mile delivery models.** Hub-and-spoke, dark stores, locker pickup, crowdsourced (Dunzo, Swiggy Genie).
73. **D2C (Direct-to-Consumer) — define.** Brand sells directly to end consumer bypassing retail intermediaries; via own e-commerce + social media.

## Session 15 — Marketing Regulation & Market Research / B2B
74. **Indian marketing regulators (any 3).** ASCI (advertising), CCPA (consumer protection), TRAI (telecom), FSSAI (food), CCI (competition), IRDAI (insurance), SEBI (securities).
75. **Consumer Protection Act 2019 — what's new?** CCPA, product liability, e-commerce rules, misleading advertisement penalties, mediation as ADR.
76. **Steps in marketing research.** Define problem → Develop research plan → Collect data → Analyse → Present findings → Make decision.
77. **Primary vs Secondary data.** Primary = collected first-hand for the research (survey, observation, experiment). Secondary = pre-existing (govt, internal, syndicated).
78. **Industrial (B2B) marketing characteristics.** Derived demand, fewer larger buyers, geographic concentration, multiple buying influences, professional buyers, longer sales cycles.
79. **Buying centre roles (Webster & Wind).** Initiator, User, Influencer, Decider, Approver, Buyer, Gatekeeper.

## Session 16 — Marketing Strategy & Plan
80. **Components of a marketing plan.** Executive summary → Situation analysis (SWOT/PESTLE) → Marketing strategy (STP, 4Ps) → Action programs → Budgets → Controls.
81. **Define BCG matrix.** Portfolio analysis on Market Growth × Relative Market Share → Star, Cash Cow, Question Mark, Dog.
82. **Define GE / McKinsey matrix.** 3×3 grid: Industry Attractiveness × Business Strength → Invest/Grow, Selectivity, Harvest/Divest.
83. **Ansoff Growth Matrix.** Market Penetration, Market Development, Product Development, Diversification.
84. **Define Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).** Net present value of profit stream a customer is expected to generate over the lifetime of the relationship.
