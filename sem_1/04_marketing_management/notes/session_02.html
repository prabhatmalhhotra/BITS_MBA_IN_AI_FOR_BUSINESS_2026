Real-World Problem

Why did Paper Boat succeed where dozens of packaged drinks failed?

India had no shortage of soft drinks — Coke, Pepsi, Frooti, Maaza dominated. Yet Hector Beverages launched Paper Boat in 2013 with aam panna, jaljeera, kokum — flavours your grandmother makes. Nobody "asked" for packaged aam panna. But Paper Boat tapped a deep latent need: nostalgia for homemade Indian beverages that urbanites had lost access to.

Paper Boat did not create a new need — it uncovered a latent need and converted it into a want with beautiful packaging and storytelling. That is the power of understanding demand states.

Daily Life Example

Your gym membership shows irregular demand: packed in January (New Year resolutions), empty by March. Gyms use synchromarketing — off-peak discounts, summer-body challenges. Cult.fit does this with dynamic class pricing and seasonal campaigns.

Exam Tip: This table is an exam favourite. You will get a scenario and need to identify the demand state + appropriate marketing task. Memorise all 8 with one example each.

2 Demand Estimation Intermediate

Total Market Demand Formula

Q = N × q × p
N = number of buyers | q = quantity per buyer | p = price per unit

Alternatively, use the Chain Ratio Method: start with total population, multiply by successive ratios narrowing to your actual market.

Daily Life Example

Estimating premium coffee demand in Pune:
Pune population: 70 lakh × 40% drink coffee × 15% willing to pay Rs 200+ × 3 cups/week × 52 weeks = annual demand. This chain-ratio thinking is how Starbucks decides whether to open a new store.

We see inputs (stimuli) and outputs (purchase decisions), but what happens inside the "black box" is what marketers try to decode.

4 The Buyer Decision Process Intermediate

1. Need Recognition
"I'm hungry" or "My phone is slow" — internal or external trigger
▼
2. Information Search
Google reviews, ask friends, visit stores, YouTube comparisons
▼
3. Evaluation of Alternatives
Compare brands on price, features, brand image
▼
4. Purchase Decision
Brand, dealer, timing, payment. Can be derailed by opinions
▼
5. Post-Purchase Behaviour
Satisfaction or cognitive dissonance? Repeat or regret?
Daily Life Example

Buying a new smartphone: (1) Phone lags — need recognized. (2) Search YouTube "best phone under 20K", read Flipkart reviews. (3) Shortlist Samsung M35, Redmi Note 14, Realme 13 Pro. (4) Friend says "Samsung display is best" — buy during Big Billion Days. (5) Happy after a week — recommend it (post-purchase advocacy).

Trending Example (2024-25)

How Blinkit changed the decision process: Traditional grocery buying had all 5 steps. Blinkit's 10-minute delivery collapses steps 2-4 into seconds. Need ("I need milk") → open app → tap → delivered. Quick commerce disrupts by removing friction from the decision process.

5 Levels of Consumer Involvement Intermediate

High Involvement

Expensive, risky, infrequent, self-expressive purchases.

  • Car, house, home loan, MBA admission
  • Extensive search, many brands compared
  • Brand differences matter a lot
  • Intense post-purchase evaluation

Low Involvement

Cheap, low-risk, habitual purchases.

  • Salt, toothpaste, cooking oil, pens
  • Little or no search
  • Brand-switching for variety or deals
  • Minimal post-purchase thought

Marketers must adjust strategy: high-involvement needs detailed information (specs, testimonials); low-involvement needs visibility, availability, and habit formation.

Exam Tip: Expect a question linking involvement level to marketing strategy. High involvement = education, content, trial. Low involvement = shelf visibility, in-store displays, repetitive advertising.

Session 2 — Key Takeaways Checklist

  • 8 demand states: negative, non-existent, latent, declining, irregular, full, overfull, unwholesome — each needs different marketing
  • Total market demand = N × q × p; also use chain-ratio method
  • Forecasting methods: buyer intentions, sales-force composite, Delphi, market test, time-series
  • Stimulus-Response (Black Box) model: stimuli → consumer mind → purchase response
  • 5-step buyer decision process: Need recognition → Info search → Evaluate → Purchase → Post-purchase
  • High involvement = extensive search, brand loyalty; Low involvement = habitual, availability matters
  • Latent demand is a goldmine — Paper Boat, boAt, Nykaa all spotted unmet latent needs
