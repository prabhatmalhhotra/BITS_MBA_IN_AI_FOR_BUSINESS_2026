Real-World Problem

How did Amul become India's most loved brand — without a big advertising budget?

Amul spends barely 1% of revenue on advertising. Yet its "Utterly Butterly" girl is among the longest-running ad campaigns in the world (since 1966). Amul sells everything from butter to ice cream to chocolate — across 50+ product categories in India and 40+ countries.

  • Product: Affordable, quality dairy for every Indian household
  • Price: Kept intentionally low — "the taste of India" for the masses
  • Place: 10 lakh+ retail outlets, from kiranas to supermarkets
  • Promotion: Topical billboard ads that become viral cultural commentary
Amul's success is textbook marketing: understanding needs, creating value, reaching the right place, at the right price, with the right message. That is what Session 1 teaches.

1 What is Marketing? Beginner

Most people think marketing = advertising or selling. But marketing is far bigger:

AMA Definition: "Marketing is the activity, set of institutions, and processes for creating, communicating, delivering, and exchanging offerings that have value for customers, clients, partners, and society at large."

Breaking it down:

  • Creating — Designing products/services that solve real problems (Zomato solving "what to eat tonight?")
  • Communicating — Telling people about it (Swiggy's "Why is this a Swiggy ad?")
  • Delivering — Getting it to the customer (Amazon's next-day delivery)
  • Exchanging — Both sides gain value (you get food, Zomato gets money)
Daily Life Example

When you buy chai from a roadside stall, all 4 elements work: The chaiwala created a recipe, the aroma and signboard communicate it, the stall location delivers convenience, and your Rs 10 is the exchange. Even a chai stall does marketing!

2 Core Marketing Concepts Beginner

Need → Want → Demand

The most fundamental chain in marketing. Every purchase starts with a need.

NEED
Basic human requirement (hunger, safety, belonging)
→
WANT
Need shaped by culture & personality (pizza, not roti)
→
DEMAND
Want + Ability & willingness to pay
Daily Life Example

Need: You are thirsty. Want: You want a cold Coca-Cola (not just water — branding shaped that). Demand: You have Rs 40 and are willing to spend it. Now Coke has a customer!

Other Core Concepts

ConceptDefinitionExample
OfferingCombination of products, services, info, experiencesZomato = food delivery + reviews + Gold membership
ValuePerceived benefits minus perceived costsIndiGo: low price + on-time = high value for business travelers
SatisfactionPerformance vs expectationsExpecting 30-min delivery, getting in 20 = delight
ExchangeGiving something to get something desiredRs 599/month for Netflix = hundreds of hours of content
MarketSet of all potential buyersEveryone in India who drinks tea = tea market
Exam Tip: BITS loves asking "differentiate need, want, and demand with examples." Always use a 3-step chain with a single scenario — it shows you understand the progression, not just definitions.

3 The Marketing Mix — 4Ps Framework Beginner

The Marketing Mix is the set of controllable tools a marketer uses to produce the desired response from the target market. The classic framework has 4Ps:

Product
What you sell — features, quality, design, brand, packaging
Price
What you charge — list price, discounts, payment terms
Place
Where you sell — channels, locations, delivery, coverage
Promotion
How you communicate — ads, PR, sales promo, digital
Trending Example (2024-25)

boAt's 4Ps masterclass: Product — affordable, stylish audio wearables for Indian youth. Price — Rs 499-5,000 (undercutting JBL/Sony). Place — Amazon, Flipkart first (D2C), then offline. Promotion — Hardik Pandya, Kiara Advani, IPL sponsorships, heavy social media. Result: India's #1 earwear brand.

For Services: 4Ps + 3 more = 7Ps

People
Staff who deliver the service
+
Process
How the service is delivered
+
Physical Evidence
Tangible cues (uniforms, decor, website)

Holistic Marketing
Relationship + Integrated + Internal + Performance (Kotler 16e)

Holistic Marketing — The 4 Pillars

Relationship Marketing

Deep, lasting bonds with customers, employees, partners.

  • Amazon Prime loyalty ecosystem
  • Tata Neu super-app rewards

Integrated Marketing

All channels deliver consistent message.

  • Apple's website, store, ads — same minimalist feel
  • Nykaa's blog + app + stores = unified experience

Internal Marketing

Everyone inside thinks like a marketer.

  • Zappos: every employee empowered to delight
  • Taj Hotels: "guest is god" culture

Performance Marketing

Accountability for financial + non-financial returns.

  • Tracking ROMI (Return on Marketing Investment)
  • Measuring CLV (Customer Lifetime Value)

6 Consumer Markets vs Business Markets Intermediate

Consumer Markets (B2C)

  • Large number of buyers
  • Low value per transaction
  • Emotional + rational decisions
  • Individual/family decides
  • Mass communication works
  • Short sales cycle

Example: Parle-G sells to 100 crore Indians at Rs 5-10

Business Markets (B2B)

  • Fewer, larger buyers
  • High value per transaction
  • Rational, specification-driven
  • Buying centre (DMU) decides
  • Personal selling dominates
  • Long sales cycle

Example: TCS selling IT services — multi-crore, multi-year contracts

Key B2B Characteristics

  • Derived demand — demand for steel depends on demand for cars
  • Inelastic demand — small price changes don't affect B2B volumes much
  • Multi-person buying centre — Initiator, Influencer, Decider, Buyer, User, Gatekeeper
  • Close relationships — Maruti and tier-1 vendors work closely for years
Daily Life Example

When your college buys 500 laptops for a lab, it is B2B. IT head (influencer) recommends Dell, admin (buyer) issues PO, director (decider) approves, students (users) use them. No single person decides — unlike buying your own laptop (B2C).

7 Marketing's Role Within the Firm Beginner

Marketing is the revenue engine connecting with every function:

  • MARKETING The customer voice inside the organisation
    • FinanceRevenue forecasts, justifies spend via ROMI
    • OperationsDemand forecasting for right inventory
    • R&D / ProductMarket insights feed new product development
    • SalesMarketing generates leads, sales converts
    • HREmployer branding, internal marketing culture
Exam Tip: A common question: "Explain marketing's cross-functional role." Structure around 3-4 functions with one concrete example per function. Avoid vague statements.

Session 1 — Key Takeaways Checklist

  • Marketing = creating, communicating, delivering, and exchanging value (AMA definition)
  • Need → Want → Demand is the foundational chain
  • Value = perceived benefits minus perceived costs
  • Marketing Mix = 4Ps (Product, Price, Place, Promotion); services add People, Process, Physical Evidence = 7Ps
  • Orientations evolved: Production → Product → Selling → Marketing → Holistic
  • Holistic marketing = Relationship + Integrated + Internal + Performance
  • New realities: technology, globalisation, consumer empowerment, disintermediation
  • B2C = many buyers, emotional; B2B = fewer buyers, rational, buying centres
  • Marketing is the cross-functional revenue engine and brand custodian
