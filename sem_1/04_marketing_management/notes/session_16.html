Marketing Strategy & Plan

The capstone session: how everything connects into a strategic marketing plan. BCG Matrix, Ansoff Matrix, SWOT, and the complete marketing plan structure.

R1 Case 21 (Amazon India)HST2: McDonald's IndiaComprehensive Exam
Real-World Problem

How did Amazon invest Rs 50,000+ crore to crack the Indian market?

Amazon entered India in 2013 via the marketplace model (FDI rules prohibited inventory). They adapted everything: Cash on Delivery (60%+ Indian orders were COD), vernacular interfaces in Hindi/Tamil/Telugu, Easy Ship logistics for remote areas, and "I Have Space" partnerships with kiranas for last-mile delivery. Despite massive losses, Amazon played a long game — building logistics infrastructure, seller ecosystem, and Prime membership base.

Global brands need India-specific marketing strategies. The STP, 4Ps, and marketing plan must be redesigned for local context. Amazon's India story is a masterclass in strategic adaptation.

2 Key Strategic Frameworks Intermediate

BCG Matrix

Stars (High Growth, High Share)

Invest heavily. Example: Jio in 2020

Cash Cows (Low Growth, High Share)

Milk profits. Example: Maruti Alto

Question Marks (High Growth, Low Share)

Invest or divest? Example: Tata EV business in 2021

Dogs (Low Growth, Low Share)

Divest or harvest. Example: Declining feature phone line

Ansoff Matrix (Growth Strategies)

Market Penetration

Existing product, existing market. Increase usage/share.

Coca-Cola: more consumption occasions

Market Development

Existing product, NEW market.

Patanjali expanding from North to South India

Product Development

NEW product, existing market.

Apple launching Apple Watch for iPhone users

Diversification

NEW product, NEW market. Highest risk.

Reliance entering telecom (Jio) from petrochemicals

Porter's Generic Strategies

Cost Leadership
Lowest cost producer. DMart, IndiGo, Xiaomi
|
Differentiation
Unique value. Apple, Titan, Asian Paints
|
Focus
Narrow segment (cost or diff). Royal Enfield, Forest Essentials

5 Case Study: Amazon India Advanced

R1 Case 21: Amazon.com adopts its retail business in India

  • Entry (2013): Junglee.com → Amazon.in marketplace model (FDI-compliant)
  • India adaptations: COD, vernacular interfaces, kirana partnerships (I Have Space), Easy Ship
  • Investment: Rs 50,000+ crore committed to compete with Flipkart and JioMart
  • Challenges: FDI rules limiting inventory model, COD profitability, data protection regulations
  • Key lesson: Global STP and 4Ps must be completely redesigned for India
Exam Tip (Comprehensive): The comprehensive exam is open book. You will likely get a case-based question requiring you to write a mini marketing plan or strategic analysis. Practice structuring your answer as: Situation Analysis → SWOT → STP → Marketing Mix → Implementation. Use frameworks (BCG, Ansoff, Porter) to structure your thinking.
