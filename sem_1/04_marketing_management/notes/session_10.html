Setting Initial Prices

How to set the first price for a new product, adjust it over time, and respond when competitors change their prices.

T1 Chapter 11 (Kotler 16e)Comprehensive Exam
Real-World Problem

When OnePlus launched at Rs 21,999, everyone said "too cheap for premium, too costly for budget" — and they were wrong

OnePlus entered India in 2014 with flagship specs at half the price of Samsung/Apple. Invite-only sales created scarcity. Over time, they raised prices: OnePlus 1 (Rs 22K) → OnePlus 12 (Rs 65K). This is a classic penetration-to-premium price migration — enter low, build brand, then climb.

Initial pricing sets the trajectory for the entire brand. Get it wrong, and repositioning is nearly impossible (see Tata Nano).

1 Skimming vs Penetration Beginner

Market Skimming

Start HIGH, lower over time

  • Demand high enough at high price
  • Small volume costs manageable
  • High price signals quality
  • Discourages early competitors

iPhone, Samsung Galaxy Fold, Tesla Model S

Market Penetration

Start LOW, grab share fast

  • Market is highly price-sensitive
  • Costs fall with experience/scale
  • Low price discourages competitors
  • Works when network effects exist

Jio, Xiaomi, IKEA India entry

2 Price Adjustments Intermediate

Adjustment TypeMethodExample
DiscountsCash, quantity, seasonal, trade-inFlipkart Big Billion Days, exchange offers on smartphones

3 Responding to Competitor Price Changes Advanced

When a competitor cuts price, you have 5 options:

Maintain Price
If you have strong brand loyalty
Add Value
Keep price but add features/service
Reduce Price
Match competitor (price war risk)
Raise Price + Quality
Move upmarket
Launch Fighter Brand
New low-cost brand to compete
Daily Life Example

When Jio slashed prices, Airtel did not just cut prices (that would bleed money). It added value (Airtel Thanks, free OTT apps), maintained premium position (best network quality), and launched a fighter brand (Airtel sub-brands for budget users). Multi-pronged response, not panic discounting.

Exam Tip: In the comprehensive exam (open book), expect scenario-based pricing questions. You might get a situation where a competitor cuts price by 20% and be asked to recommend a response with justification. Use the 5-option framework above.

Session 10 — Key Takeaways Checklist

  • Skimming: high-to-low, for innovative/premium products
  • Penetration: low-to-grow, for price-sensitive mass markets
  • Price adjustments: discounts, differentiated pricing, promotional pricing
  • 5 responses to competitor price cuts: maintain, add value, reduce, increase+quality, fighter brand
  • Price-quality matrix: Premium, High-value, Super-value, Economy, etc.
  • OnePlus demonstrates penetration-to-premium migration over time
