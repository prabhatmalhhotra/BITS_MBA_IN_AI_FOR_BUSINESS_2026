Marketing Channels

How products travel from factory to your doorstep. Channel design, management, conflict, and the rise of vertical marketing systems.

T1 Chapter 15 (Kotler 16e)HST7: Omni-channelComprehensive Exam
Real-World Problem

How does Hindustan Unilever reach 9 million retail outlets across India?

HUL's distribution network is legendary: 9 million outlets, from Mumbai supermarkets to village kiranas. Their system: factory → C&F agents (30+) → redistributors (7,000+) → retailers (9 million). In rural India, they use Project Shakti — women entrepreneurs (Shakti Ammas) sell HUL products door-to-door in villages with no retail. This is a 3-level channel system, customised for India's vast geography.

Distribution is often the hardest P to replicate. HUL's 9-million-outlet network took decades to build and is their most defensible competitive advantage.

3 Channel Conflict & Power Advanced

Types of Conflict

  • Vertical: Manufacturer vs retailer (Apple vs Amazon pricing)
  • Horizontal: Same-level intermediaries competing
  • Multi-channel: Online vs offline cannibalization

Channel Power (French & Raven)

  • Coercive: Threat of withdrawal
  • Reward: Better margins, exclusives
  • Legitimate: Contractual authority
  • Expert: Superior knowledge
  • Referent: Brand prestige

Vertical Marketing Systems (VMS)
