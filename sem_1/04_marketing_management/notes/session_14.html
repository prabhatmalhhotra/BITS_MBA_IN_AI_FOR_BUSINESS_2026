Intermediaries, E-commerce & M-commerce

From kirana stores to Amazon to 10-minute delivery — the retail revolution reshaping how India shops.

T1 Chapters 15 & 16HST7: Omni-channelComprehensive Exam
Real-World Problem

How is Blinkit delivering groceries in 10 minutes — and why does it matter for marketing?

Blinkit (formerly Grofers) operates 500+ dark stores across Indian cities. Each dark store stocks ~5,000 SKUs within a 2-3 km radius. When you order, a picker grabs items in 2 minutes, a rider delivers in 8. This "quick commerce" model is fundamentally changing the Place (distribution) P. Brands now compete for dark-store shelf space the way they once competed for supermarket shelf space. If your brand is not on Blinkit, Zepto, or Instamart, you are invisible to urban India's impulse buyers.

E-commerce and quick commerce have not just added a channel — they have redefined what "Place" means in the marketing mix.

Trending Example (2024-25)

ONDC (Open Network for Digital Commerce): India's government-backed initiative to democratise e-commerce. Like UPI did for payments, ONDC aims to create an interoperable network where any buyer app can discover any seller app. Implications: kiranas can compete with Amazon, and no single platform monopolises digital commerce.

3 M-Commerce & Digital Payments Intermediate

India is a mobile-first market with 800M+ smartphone users. UPI processed 13+ billion transactions/month in 2024.

  • App-based aggregators: Swiggy, Zomato, Ola, Urban Company, PharmEasy
  • Mobile wallets/payments: PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm
  • Quick commerce: Blinkit, Zepto, Swiggy Instamart (10-min delivery)
  • Live commerce: Flipkart Live, Meesho Live — shopping + entertainment

Indian E-Commerce Regulations

  • FDI policy: 100% in marketplace model; inventory model not allowed for foreign platforms
  • Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules 2020: Mandatory return/refund policies
  • DPDP Act 2023: Data protection for digital consumers
