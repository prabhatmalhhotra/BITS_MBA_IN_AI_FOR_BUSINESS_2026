Services & Marketing Mix

Services are different from products — you cannot touch, store, or standardise them. Learn the 7Ps framework and SERVQUAL model that govern service marketing.

T1 Chapter 8 (Kotler 16e)Case: MTR Foods (R1 Case 1)Mid-Sem Syllabus
Real-World Problem

Why does Zomato's rating for the same restaurant vary from 3.5 to 4.5 depending on when you order?

You ordered biryani from Meghana Foods on a Saturday night and it was cold, late, and the raita was missing. Rating: 3 stars. Your friend ordered on a Tuesday afternoon — hot, fast, complete. Rating: 5 stars. Same restaurant, wildly different experience. This is the core challenge of services: heterogeneity. Quality varies with provider, time, and place. Unlike a Parle-G biscuit (which tastes the same every time), services are inherently inconsistent.

Services have unique characteristics (IHIP) that require a different marketing approach. The 7Ps framework extends the 4Ps to handle these challenges.

1 Service Characteristics: IHIP Beginner

Intangibility
Cannot see, touch, taste before purchase. A haircut, a flight, a consulting session.
Heterogeneity
Quality varies with provider, time, place. Same barber, different day = different cut.
Inseparability
Produced and consumed simultaneously. Doctor and patient must both be present.
Perishability
Cannot be stored. Empty airline seat = lost revenue forever.
Daily Life Example

Think about a movie theatre seat. Intangible (the experience of watching, not the seat itself). Heterogeneous (different audience = different vibe). Inseparable (you must be there). Perishable (an unsold Friday night seat cannot be sold on Saturday).

3 SERVQUAL — Measuring Service Quality Intermediate

The SERVQUAL model (Parasuraman, Zeithaml, Berry) measures service quality across 5 dimensions — remember RATER:

Reliability
Deliver promised service dependably (MOST important)
Assurance
Knowledge, courtesy, trust of staff
Tangibles
Physical facilities, equipment, appearance
Empathy
Caring, individualised attention
Responsiveness
Willingness to help promptly

The 5 Gaps Model

Service failures occur due to gaps: (1) Knowledge gap (not knowing expectations), (2) Design gap (wrong standards), (3) Performance gap (not meeting standards), (4) Communication gap (promising more than delivered), (5) Customer gap (expected vs perceived).

4 Service Recovery & Modern Frameworks Advanced

When service fails, recovery matters more than prevention:

Acknowledge
Recognise the failure immediately
▼
Empathise
Show genuine concern
▼
Fix
Resolve the problem
▼
Compensate
Offer something extra (refund, upgrade)
▼
Learn
Prevent recurrence — "Recovery Paradox" can build higher loyalty

4Cs and SAVE Frameworks

4Cs (Lauterborn)

  • Customer value (not Product)
  • Cost to customer (not Price)
  • Convenience (not Place)
  • Communication (not Promotion)

SAVE (B2B)

  • Solution (not Product)
  • Access (not Place)
  • Value (not Price)
  • Education (not Promotion)

5 Case Study: MTR Foods Advanced

R1 Case 1: MTR Foods — A Marketing Marvel

  • Heritage Bangalore restaurant brand (est. 1924) expanded to packaged ready-to-eat and ready-to-cook products
  • Product: Authentic South Indian recipes (rava idli mix, bisibelebath) — premium quality
  • Price: Premium but justified by quality and heritage
  • Place: Modern retail + traditional kirana + growing online presence
  • Promotion: Indian sentiment-led advertising, trust-based positioning
  • Acquired by Norwegian Orkla in 2007; merged with Eastern Condiments
Exam Tip: MTR Foods is a great example of a service brand (restaurant) extending into products. Be ready to discuss how they managed the 7Ps transition. Also re-watch HST1 on the marketing mix for services context. Mid-Sem scope ends after this session (S1-S8).
