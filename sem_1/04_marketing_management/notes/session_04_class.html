Segmentation & Targeting

Why segmentation is needed, the four bases of segmentation (Geographic, Demographic, Psychographic, Behavioral), business market segmentation, effective segmentation criteria, and targeting strategies.

Lecture 4-5 (L4_5) — First Half Mid-Sem Syllabus

1 Learning Objectives

  1. In what ways can a company divide the consumer market into segments?
  2. How should business markets be segmented?
  3. How should a company choose the most attractive target markets?
  4. What are the requirements for effective segmentation?
  5. What are the different levels of market segmentation?

2 Why is Segmentation Needed?

It is nearly impossible for a company to connect with all customers in large, broad, or diverse markets. They need to identify the market segments they can serve effectively. India alone has 145 crore people — you cannot target everyone.

Market segment: A group of customers who share a similar set of needs and wants.

3 Four Bases of Consumer Market Segmentation

Geographic
Where they live
Demographic
Who they are
Psychographic
How they think
Behavioral
How they act

a) Geographic Segmentation

  • Geographical units: Nations, states, regions, counties, cities, or neighborhoods
  • Nielsen Claritas' PRIZM: US-based rating of 500,000 neighborhoods into 16 groups and 66 lifestyle segments based on education/affluence, family life cycle, urbanization, race/ethnicity, mobility
  • PRIZM Clusters: Young Digerati (tech-savvy, affluent urban singles), Beltway Boomers (college-educated, upper-middle-class), The Cosmopolitans (educated, midscale, multiethnic)
Indian Examples

Nariman Point, Cuffe Parade, Peddar Road (all Mumbai) and Panchsheel Park, Amrita Shergill Marg (all Delhi) may have similar characteristics. A retail chain or school would find such geographic segmentation useful.

b) Demographic Segmentation

Variables: Age, Life stage, Gender, Income, Generation, Race & Culture.

  • Age & Life-cycle: Childhood, Adolescence, Adulthood, Middle Age, Old — wants and abilities change with age
  • Life stage: Major concerns like job, marriage, children's education
  • Gender: Men and women have different attitudes and behave differently (Gillette Venus for women)
  • Income: Long-standing practice for segmentation
  • Generation: Millennials/Gen Y (1977-1994), Gen X (1964-1978), Baby Boomers (1946-64), Silent Generation (1925-1945) — each uses different platforms (Facebook/WhatsApp vs Snapchat/Instagram)
  • Race & Culture: In India — Linguistic Groups (ads, cuisines, dress, festivals) and Religious Groups (ethnic wear, cuisines)
Slide Examples

Kinder Joy: Demographic segmentation for children. Right Shift Jaggery Ragi Cookies (ITC): Health-conscious middle-aged adults. Horlicks: Age-based segmentation — Growth Plus (kids), Protein Plus (adults), Mother's Plus (pregnancy), Women's Plus, Diabetes Plus, Strength Plus (elderly). One brand, six demographic segments.

c) Psychographic Segmentation

Buyers divided on the basis of psychological/personality traits, lifestyle, or values. (Adventure Freaks, Young-at-heart, Geeks)

Slide Example

Honda Shine 125 vs Pulsar 125: Same segment (125cc bike), but targeting completely different psychographic profiles. Honda Shine = reliable commuter. Pulsar = sporty, exciting personality.

d) Behavioral Segmentation

Divide buyers on the basis of their knowledge of, attitude toward, use of, or response to a product.

  • Needs and benefits
  • Decision roles: Initiator, Influencer, Decider, Buyer, User
  • User & Usage variables: Occasions, User status, Usage rate, Loyalty status, Buyer-readiness stage, Attitude
Caselet: Coaching Centre (Rishi)

Initiator: Mother → Marketing should target parents. Influencer: Sister (academic excellence) → Use good students as influencers. Decider: Rishi (child) → Demo classes must be excellent quality. Buyer: Parents → Offer financing options. User: Rishi/friend → Design product for user needs.

User & Usage Variables (Detail)

  • Occasion: Stay in hotel for business (Taj Mahal), leisure (Taj Holiday Village), experience (Oberoi Vilas — fort). Bicycle?
  • User Status: Non-user, Ex-user, Potential User, First-time user, Regular user. 5-star hotels offer memberships to non-users. Portals run campaigns for ex-users (LinkedIn).
  • Usage Rate: Light, Medium, Heavy users
  • Buyer Readiness Stage: Unaware, Aware, Informed, Interested, Intend to buy
  • Loyalty Status: Hard core loyals, Split Loyals, Shifting Loyals, Switcher
  • Attitude: Enthusiastic, Positive, Indifferent, Negative, Hostile
Slide Example

Cadbury's attempt to build around existing habits: "Kuch accha ho jaye, kuch meetha ho jaye" — linking chocolate to Indian celebration/sweetness culture. Cadbury Celebrations for Rakhi/festivals — occasion-based behavioral segmentation.

4 Marketing Funnel

The funnel shows conversion at each stage: Aware → Ever Tried → Recent Trial → Occasional User → Regular User → Most Often Used.

Brand A vs Brand B

Brand A: 96 Aware → 63 Ever Tried (65%) → 29 Recent Trial (46%) → 18 Occasional (62%) → 12 Regular (67%) → 6 Most Often (50%). Brand B: 97 Aware → 74 Ever Tried (76%) → 45 Recent Trial (61%) → 32 Occasional (71%) → 24 Regular (75%) → 15 Most Often (62%). Brand B has better conversion at every stage.

6 Market Targeting & Effective Segmentation

7 Steps in the Segmentation Process

  1. Needs-Based Segmentation: Group customers based on similar needs and benefits
  2. Segment Identification: Determine demographics, lifestyles, usage behaviors making the segment distinct and identifiable
  3. Segment Attractiveness: Market growth, competitive intensity, market access
  4. Segment Profitability: Determine profitability
  5. Segment Positioning: Create value proposition and product-price positioning strategy
  6. Segment "Acid Test": Create segment storyboard to test attractiveness
  7. Marketing-Mix Strategy: Expand to include all 4Ps

Effective Segmentation Criteria

  • Measurable — Can you measure the segment size?
  • Substantial — Is the segment large enough to be profitable?
  • Accessible — Can you effectively reach the segment?
  • Differentiable — Is the segment conceptually distinguishable?
  • Actionable — Can you formulate effective programs for the segment?

Porter's 5 Forces (Segment Attractiveness)

  • Threat of Rivalry: Too much competition
  • Threat of New Entrants: Low entry barrier
  • Threat of Substitutes: Many alternative products
  • Threat of Buyer Bargaining Power: Less number of buyers / big buyers
  • Threat of Supplier Bargaining Power: Too few suppliers, controlling vital resources

7 Levels of Segmentation & Targeting

A spectrum from Mass Market (Full Market Coverage) → Multiple Segments → Single Segments → Individuals as Segments (Customization).

Targeting Slide Examples

Dove: Targeted to women who value real beauty and moisturization. Tide: Gets its point effortlessly — mass market targeting. Thums Up: "Taste the Thunder" positioning for young, macho, adventurous males. Pulsar "Definitely Male": You should know whom NOT to target — Pulsar explicitly excludes women from its positioning.

Session 4 Class Notes — Key Takeaways

  • Segmentation needed because 145 Cr Indians cannot all be targeted
  • 4 Bases: Geographic (PRIZM clusters, Indian neighborhoods), Demographic (age, gender, income, generation, race/culture), Psychographic (VALS framework, personality traits), Behavioral (decision roles, occasions, user status, loyalty, attitude)
  • Horlicks: 6 demographic segments from one brand
  • Honda Shine vs Pulsar: same category, different psychographic targets
  • VALS: 8 segments from Innovators to Survivors
  • Decision Roles: Initiator, Influencer, Decider, Buyer, User (coaching centre caselet)
  • Marketing Funnel: Aware → Tried → Trial → Occasional → Regular → Most Often Used
  • Business segmentation: Demographic, Operating, Purchasing, Situational, Personal variables
  • 7-Step Segmentation Process ending with Marketing-Mix Strategy
  • Effective criteria: Measurable, Substantial, Accessible, Differentiable, Actionable
  • Porter's 5 Forces for segment attractiveness
  • Spectrum: Mass Market → Customization
