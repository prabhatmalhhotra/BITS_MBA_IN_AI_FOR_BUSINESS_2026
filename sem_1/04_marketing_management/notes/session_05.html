Customer Value Proposition & Positioning Intro

Why should anyone choose YOUR brand over a hundred alternatives? The CVP answers that question. Positioning plants that answer firmly in the customer's mind.

Real-World Problem

Why do people pay Rs 300 for a Starbucks coffee when chai costs Rs 10?

Starbucks does not sell coffee. It sells an experience — AC comfort, Wi-Fi, Instagram-worthy cups, a "third place" between home and office. Its CVP: "For urban professionals who want a premium coffee experience, Starbucks is the coffeehouse brand that offers consistently excellent coffee in a welcoming atmosphere, because we source the world's finest beans and train every barista extensively."

The Customer Value Proposition is the reason customers choose you. It must clearly articulate WHO you serve, WHAT you offer, and WHY you are different.

1 What is a Customer Value Proposition? Beginner

A CVP is a clear statement of what the firm offers, to whom, and why it is better than alternatives.

Components of a CVP

Target Customer
WHO are you serving?
Customer Needs
What jobs/pains do they have?
Offering
Your product/service
Benefits
Functional + Emotional + Social
Differentiation
Why better than competitors?

2 Value Proposition Canvas Intermediate

The Strategyzer Value Proposition Canvas helps you design a CVP that truly fits:

Customer Profile (Right side)

  • Jobs — What tasks are they trying to accomplish?
  • Pains — What frustrates or blocks them?
  • Gains — What outcomes do they desire?

Value Map (Left side)

  • Products & Services — What you offer
  • Pain Relievers — How you eliminate pains
  • Gain Creators — How you create gains

FIT is achieved when your value map addresses the customer profile — pain relievers match pains, gain creators match gains.

Daily Life Example

Zepto's Value Proposition Canvas: Customer jobs = buy groceries quickly. Pains = traffic, parking, long queues, out-of-stock. Gains = convenience, instant gratification. Zepto's value map: 10-minute delivery (pain reliever for traffic/queues), curated selection (gain creator for convenience). Perfect fit — that is why it works.

3 Value Disciplines (Treacy & Wiersema) Intermediate

Every successful company excels in ONE of three value disciplines:

Operational Excellence
Best total cost — reliable, low price, hassle-free. IndiGo, Walmart, DMart, Decathlon
Product Leadership
Best product — innovation, performance, design. Apple, Tesla, Dyson, OnePlus
Customer Intimacy
Best total solution — customised, deep relationships. Asian Paints, IBM, Nordstrom
Exam Tip: You may be asked to identify which value discipline a company follows. The key is: you must be excellent in ONE and adequate in the other two. No company can lead in all three — trade-offs are necessary.

4 Introduction to Positioning Intermediate

Positioning = "the act of designing the company's offer and image so that it occupies a distinctive place in the minds of the target customers." — Kotler

Positioning Statement Template

"For [target segment], our [brand] is the [category] that [point of difference], because [reason to believe]."

Key Positioning Concepts

ConceptDefinitionExample (Volvo)
Frame of Reference (FoR)The competitive set / categoryPremium automobiles
Points of Parity (POP)Must-haves to belong to the categoryPerformance, luxury, design (matches BMW/Audi)
Points of Difference (POD)What makes you unique and preferableSAFETY — Volvo owns this word
Trending Example (2024-25)

CRED's positioning challenge: "For premium credit card holders (target), CRED is the rewards app (category) that makes bill payment feel exclusive and rewarding (POD), because only the top 5% of credit scores qualify (reason to believe)." Love it or hate it, CRED carved a distinctive position in a crowded fintech space through exclusivity.

Session 5 — Key Takeaways Checklist

  • CVP = Target + Needs + Offering + Benefits + Differentiation
  • Value Proposition Canvas: Customer Profile (Jobs, Pains, Gains) must FIT Value Map (Products, Pain Relievers, Gain Creators)
  • Three value disciplines: Operational Excellence, Product Leadership, Customer Intimacy — excel in ONE
  • Positioning = occupying a distinctive place in target customer's mind
  • Positioning statement: For [target], [brand] is the [category] that [POD], because [reason to believe]
  • FoR + POP + POD are the building blocks of positioning
  • Positioning is completed in Session 6 — this is the foundation
