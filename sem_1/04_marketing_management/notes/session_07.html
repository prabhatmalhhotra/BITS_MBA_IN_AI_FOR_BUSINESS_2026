Product Management & NPD

From the core benefit to the augmented product, and from idea to commercialisation — how companies design, manage, and launch new products.

T1 Chapter 7 (Kotler 16e)R1 Case 10Mid-Sem Syllabus
Real-World Problem

How did Tata Motors develop the Nexon EV — India's best-selling electric car?

In 2020, EVs were a tiny niche in India. Tata saw a latent need: Indians wanted affordable, practical electric cars — not just golf carts or luxury imports. The Nexon EV was built on an existing platform (cost control), priced at Rs 14 lakh (accessible), with 300+ km range (practical). By 2024, it held 60%+ of India's EV market. Tata followed the NPD process textbook-style: idea generation from market research, concept testing with fleet operators, business analysis, development, limited market testing in metro cities, then full commercialisation.

Great products are not accidents. They follow a structured New Product Development (NPD) process from idea to launch.

1 Product Levels (Kotler's 5 Levels) Beginner

Every product has layers, like an onion. Marketers add value at each layer:

1. Core Benefit
Fundamental need: "I need transportation"
▼
2. Basic Product
A car with engine, wheels, seats
▼
3. Expected Product
AC, music system, power steering — what buyers normally expect
▼
4. Augmented Product
Extended warranty, roadside assistance, free servicing — exceeds expectations
▼
5. Potential Product
Self-driving, solar roof, flying car — future possibilities
Daily Life Example

Hotel room: Core = sleep. Basic = bed + bathroom. Expected = clean sheets, Wi-Fi, AC. Augmented = welcome drink, late checkout, spa discount. Potential = AI concierge, holographic room tours. Competition happens at the augmented level.

4 New Product Development (NPD) Process Advanced

1. Idea Generation
Employees, customers, competitors, R&D, distributors
▼
2. Idea Screening
Cost, feasibility, strategic fit — kill bad ideas early
▼
3. Concept Development & Testing
Describe product idea to target consumers, gauge reactions
▼
4. Marketing Strategy
Target market, positioning, pricing, distribution plan
▼
5. Business Analysis
Revenue, cost, profit projections — go/no-go
▼
6. Product Development
Build prototype, engineer for manufacturing
▼
7. Market Testing
Test in limited geography/audience before full launch
▼
8. Commercialisation
Full launch: when, where, to whom, how
Trending Example (2024-25)

NPD failures as lessons: Patanjali Atta Noodles tried to ride the Maggi ban but failed (poor taste, weak distribution). Tata Nano was engineered brilliantly but positioned as "cheapest car" — nobody wants to be seen in the cheapest car. Both failed at concept testing and positioning stages.

Exam Tip: NPD's 8 steps are a popular exam topic. Be ready to apply them to a given scenario. Also know reasons for NPD failure: overestimated market, poor positioning, high cost, weak marketing support, bad timing.

Session 7 — Key Takeaways Checklist

  • 5 product levels: Core → Basic → Expected → Augmented → Potential
  • Product mix: Width, Length, Depth, Consistency
  • PLC stages: Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline (different marketing mix per stage)
  • 8-step NPD: Idea generation → Screening → Concept test → Strategy → Business analysis → Development → Market test → Commercialisation
  • NPD failure reasons: overestimate market, poor positioning, high cost, bad timing
  • Innovation types: incremental vs disruptive (Christensen)
