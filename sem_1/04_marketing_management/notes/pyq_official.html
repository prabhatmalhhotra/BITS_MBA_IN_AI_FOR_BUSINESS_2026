Case Study — Ruffles Luxury Chocolates

It was 25th December 2016, Vishal was in a peculiar situation. The luxury chocolate ‘Riva’ that he launched in August of 2015 was a huge hit and was giving him regular income. This chocolate was a unique product with three different components: milk, coco and coffee. The product was something that Indian market had not seen before. Reports have suggested that coffee and chocolate as a combination was never taken well by the Indian market before.

However, Vishal wanted to accelerate the growth of the company. This would give him much more flexibility to move things faster, and also repay the loans that he had taken ahead of schedule.

Background

Vishal was a graduate from a reputed management institution of India. He was hired by a leading global coco sourcing company in 2005. For the first years, he was posted in Ghana, West Africa. During his stint, he thoroughly understood the nuances of chocolate sourcing and processing, and established cordial relationships with coco farmers and traders. In 2010, Vishal was also asked to head the coffee sourcing vertical. He established relationships with coffee farmers and traders as well.

Vishal’s clients were senior procurement managers of leading chocolate and coffee companies worldwide. He understood that South Asia had significant market potential for chocolates and confectionaries, especially with increasing disposable income. He also noted that India was one of the largest producers of dairy products.

After 10 years in corporate, Vishal moved back to India in 2015 and bought a 10-acre land in Gurgaon for a chocolate processing unit. He invested 25 Million rupees for processing machinery and set aside 10 Million for hiring: five sales people, two production managers, two accounting managers, two experienced chefs, and ten manufacturing staff. The company became closely held, with Vishal handling product and brand management.

He established the brand Ruffles in January 2015, headquartered in Gurgaon.

Value Offerings

Vishal leveraged his contacts for the finest raw materials at low prices. He wanted to manufacture luxury chocolates for Indian consumers, who were not exposed to many brands. Vineville from Dadbury’s was one product catering to this segment — other than that, there was no big player. The total addressable market for Ruffles stood at 20 Million USD.

Vishal wanted to first test the market with one product, then launch more in subsequent years. His first chocolate had equal amounts of coco, milk and coffee. The closest competitor, Dadbury’s, relied on dark chocolate — not banking on taste but on the experience of possessing it. Vineville was often used as a gifting option.

Competition & Distribution

Dadbury’s operated at multiple price points, making the brand synonymous with all consumers. Pestle and Rotte also enjoyed strong recall and recognition due to financial muscle, long business history, and strong distribution networks covering every last mile.

Vishal took a loan of 1 billion rupees to hire 20 more sales staff reporting to the two existing sales leaders (total: 22 in sales and distribution). The plan was to set up operations in Haryana, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh and Uttarakhand.

Vishal wanted to differentiate Ruffles by pricing it slightly lower than Vineville — mulling the option of Rs. 99/- (vs Vineville’s Rs. 100/-), creating the perception of being comparatively cheaper.

Marketing & Growth

Vishal chose Social Media for promotion (no budget for ATL activities like print, radio, TV). He believed superior taste would generate positive word of mouth. He built an aesthetically appealing website with e-commerce capability. He wanted Ruffles to be an eco-friendly brand, attracting green consumers who use products publicly.

Manufacturing started 1st August 2015: over 2,000 chocolates/day. E-commerce launched the same day with at least one order daily (avg ticket size: Rs. 5,000). Monthly revenue from retailers in Haryana, Punjab, Delhi, UP and Uttarakhand: Rs. 20,00,000.

By February 2016, Ruffles made revenue of one crore rupees.

By December 2016 (market research): 20% of target audience aware of Ruffles. Of that 20%, 50% willing to try, 25% interested in repeat purchases. Expected 2,000 average repeat purchases/month for the next year.

One year in, Vishal wanted to step up. He was ready with another luxury product. Channel partners were excited (Vishal gave huge margins). However, the brand was still not popular enough. The processing unit was at capacity. Banks offered 50 Million rupees loan; Vishal conservatively took 35 Million.

Case Study — Ravi’s Entertainment Platform

It was 2nd March, 2020. Ravi, after working with Netflix for five years, quit his job with an intention to come back to India. Prior to Netflix, Ravi worked for Facebook for five years.

In the last ten years, after his grad education, Ravi found himself in good stead both in terms of financial competence and technical competence. He wanted to gain entrepreneurial experience and do something on his own in India.

Ravi originally hailed from Karimnagar, a popular town in Telangana. He went back by 10th March, 2020. By 22nd March, 2020, the entire country went under lockdown for 21 days.

During this period, Ravi understood there was a strong need for a local entertainment platform like Netflix. His research found that consumers would find any additional subscription fee a burden, as most Telugu-speaking people under 25 were already subscribers of HotStar, Netflix and Amazon Prime.

He only wanted to target the under-25 group as they were vulnerable to advertisements and known to build emotional relationships with brands.

Come up with possible positioning for Ravi’s new platform.

Question 5 5 marks
