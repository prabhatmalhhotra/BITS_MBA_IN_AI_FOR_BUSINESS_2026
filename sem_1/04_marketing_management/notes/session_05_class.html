1 Evaluating & Selecting Market Segments

Possible levels of segmentation form a spectrum:

  • Full Market Coverage (Mass Market) — target everyone
  • Multiple Segments — serve several distinct segments
  • Single Segment — niche focus
  • Individuals as Segments (Customization) — one-to-one marketing

2 One-to-One Marketing (Customization)

Example: A salon often practices one-to-one marketing. Hotels also do this.

3 Behavioral Segmentation Breakdown

A detailed tree structure for behavioral segmentation of any target market:

  • Target Market splits into:
    • Unaware
    • Aware
      • Not Tried: Negative opinion, Neutral, Favorable opinion
      • Tried:
        • Rejector
        • Not yet repeated → Loyal to other brand, Switcher
        • Repeated → Loyal to brand → Light user, Regular user, Heavy user

This tree helps marketers understand exactly where potential customers are in the adoption journey and design targeted interventions for each node.

4 Cadbury: Building Around Existing Habits

Slide Example

Cadbury Dairy Milk: "Kuch accha ho jaye, kuch meetha ho jaye" — replacing traditional Indian sweets (mithai) with chocolate for celebrations. Cadbury Celebrations: Designed for Rakhi, Diwali, and other festivals — an occasion-based segmentation strategy that builds on the existing Indian habit of gifting sweets, substituting chocolate boxes for mithai boxes.

5 Legal & Ethical Issues in Segmentation

Marketers must avoid consumer backlash:

  • Labeling consumers: Avoid offensive labels like "Old", "non-performer", "spinsters", "lonely"
  • Vulnerable groups: Be careful targeting children (soft drinks, unhealthy food)
  • Disadvantaged groups: Avoid making fun of disabled persons; Racist toys
  • Potentially harmful products: Marketing cigarettes (Ms Special Filters), alcohol to specific groups
Prof. Sagar's Point

The same ethical issues appear in both L3 (Consumer Behaviour) and L4-5 (Segmentation) slides — this is a recurring exam theme. Marketers have a social responsibility not to exploit vulnerable segments, even if those segments are profitable.
