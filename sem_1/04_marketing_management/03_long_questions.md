## Mid-Sem scope (Sessions 1-8)

### LQ-1 (Session 1) — Marketing's role in the new realities
**Prompt.** "In a world of digital disruption, social activism, and AI personalisation, marketing must evolve from 4Ps to a holistic, customer-centric, sustainable function." Discuss with reference to Kotler's "marketing for the new realities". Use one Indian and one global brand example.

**Skeleton**: Selling vs marketing concept evolution → 4Ps → 7Ps for services → "new realities": digital + social + sustainability. Indian: Tata Tea Jaago Re (cause + brand); ITC sustainability (e-Choupal, ESG-led marketing). Global: Patagonia (anti-consumption messaging), Nike (Colin Kaepernick stand). Implications: holistic marketing (internal, integrated, performance, relationship); customer-centricity, brand purpose, digital-first omnichannel.

### LQ-2 (Session 2) — Need-want-demand framework
**Prompt.** Distinguish needs, wants, demand, and demand states. Apply Maslow's hierarchy and demand-state typology to design marketing strategy for (a) bottled water, (b) electric two-wheelers, (c) family planning services in rural India.

**Skeleton**: Definitions → Maslow framework. Bottled water: physiological need; full demand in urban (drive premiumisation Bisleri Limited Edition); latent in tier 3 (build distribution). EV two-wheelers: safety + esteem + self-actualisation; latent demand (TCO economics, environmental positioning); subsidies, financing. Family planning rural: demand could be negative or no-demand → social marketing (PSI/Jago Grahak), incentives, ASHA workers; convert no-demand to latent.

### LQ-3 (Session 3) — Consumer behaviour & competitive dynamics
**Prompt.** Discuss the model of consumer behaviour. Apply the 5-stage decision process and Porter's 5 forces to analyse the Indian premium smartphone market. Tie to **Case Discussion: Consumer Behaviour — Its wide Facts and Deep Implications** (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 6).

**Skeleton**: Black-box model → 4 sets of factors (cultural/social/personal/psychological). 5-stage process for premium smartphone: Need recognition (older device, status); Information search (YouTube reviews, peer influence); Evaluation (camera, brand, ecosystem, price); Purchase (online vs offline, financing); Post-purchase (cognitive dissonance, AppleCare/Samsung Care). Porter: high rivalry (Apple, Samsung, OnePlus, Xiaomi); medium new entrants (Nothing, Realme); high substitutes (refurbished, lower segments); medium supplier power (Qualcomm, foundries); high buyer power (price-comparison, switching).

### LQ-4 (Session 4) — STP for Mahindra XYLO (mandatory case)
**Prompt.** Using the **Mahindra XYLO case** (Pearson Indian Cases), explain how Mahindra's segmentation, targeting, and positioning decisions evolved. What did they get right and what could improve? Apply effective-segmentation criteria.

**Skeleton**: Segments — large family travellers, intercity commercial users (taxi operators), upgraders from sedan; needs space, comfort, fuel economy. Targeting: differentiated (consumer + commercial trims). Positioning: "premium MPV" vs Toyota Innova. Effective segmentation criteria: measurable (yes), substantial (yes — large family segment), accessible (dealer network strong), differentiable, actionable. What worked: tapping commercial cab segment. Improve: stronger urban brand, refresh vs Innova Crysta. Conclude with personas (lecturer Dr. Annmarie Hanlon HSTalk).

### LQ-5 (Session 5) — CVP for a fintech startup
**Prompt.** Design a Customer Value Proposition for a UPI-based small-business credit fintech targeting kirana stores. Use Value Proposition Canvas (jobs/pains/gains × pain relievers/gain creators).

**Skeleton**: Customer profile — Jobs: manage daily working capital, pay suppliers, receive UPI payments. Pains: cash crunch, slow bank loans, paperwork, high interest from informal sources. Gains: instant credit, transparent rates, dignity, growth. Value Map: products — instant ₹10K-2L credit on UPI history; pain relievers: 0 paperwork, 30-sec approval, transparent rate, no collateral; gain creators: build credit score, accept QR + reconcile, integrate accounting. CVP statement: "For kiranas needing flexible working capital, our UPI-data-driven instant credit is faster than banks and cheaper than informal lenders." Differentiator: data + speed.

### LQ-6 (Session 6) — Positioning Kellogg's Cornflakes (mandatory case)
**Prompt.** Using the **Kellogg's Cornflakes in India case** (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 9), discuss Kellogg's positioning challenges in India. Apply POP/POD analysis. Recommend a refined positioning statement.

**Skeleton**: Initial mistake — positioned as breakfast cereal, but Indian breakfast = hot, savoury, filling (paratha, idli, poha). POP: cereal category convenience; POD initially failed (cold, sweet, milk-based — alien). Adaptations: Chocos (kids), millet variants, fortified with vitamins, hot-prep options. Repositioning statement: "For health-conscious urban Indian families wanting a quick, nutritious breakfast, Kellogg's Cornflakes is a fortified-grain option that complements (not replaces) traditional breakfasts." Lessons: cultural sensitivity, distinguishing POP vs POD, perceptual mapping.

### LQ-7 (Session 7) — Product life cycle & differentiation
**Prompt.** Apply the Product Life Cycle (PLC) to the Indian two-wheeler market. Map current positions of Bajaj Chetak (EV), Hero Splendor, TVS Apache. Discuss differentiation strategies for each.

**Skeleton**: PLC stages — Introduction (Chetak EV), Growth (premium 150-200cc Apache, FZ), Maturity (Splendor — cash cow), Decline (legacy Vespa). Strategies: Chetak — build awareness, R&D, partner with charging infra, skim pricing. Apache — differentiate on performance (RTR, racing DNA), aggressive marketing, lifestyle. Splendor — milk profits, line extensions (Splendor Plus, iSmart), defend share, EDLP. Discuss differentiation: product (engine, design), service (warranty, EMI), channel (rural reach), image (brand persona), personnel (Hero ki ghodaagiri).

### LQ-8 (Session 8) — Services marketing & MTR Foods (mandatory case)
**Prompt.** Discuss the unique characteristics of services using SERVQUAL. Then, using the **MTR Foods case** (Pearson Indian Cases), explain how MTR managed the goods-services continuum (restaurant → packaged food). Apply marketing-mix (4Ps + 3Ps) for services.

**Skeleton**: 4 service characteristics (IHIP). SERVQUAL 5 dims (RATER). MTR pivot: restaurant (high service variability) → packaged ready-to-eat (productisation; reduce variability). Marketing mix transition: Product (RTE, masala), Price (premium with brand equity), Place (modern trade + traditional + e-commerce), Promotion (heritage + nostalgia), People (vendor partnerships), Process (cold chain), Physical evidence (packaging, branding). Lesson: service brand equity transferable to packaged goods if execution holds.

## Comprehensive scope (Sessions 9-16)

### LQ-9 (Sessions 9-10) — Pricing strategy for new product
**Prompt.** A consumer electronics firm is launching a smart-home robot vacuum in India. Discuss the pricing decision: penetration vs skimming. Cover the 6 steps of pricing (Kotler), elasticity considerations, and competitive response. Recommend a pricing strategy with rationale.

**Skeleton**: 6 steps — Pricing objective (skim premium segment first, then penetrate), Demand (high-income urban tech-savvy ~5M households; price-elastic in mid-tier), Cost (high R&D + import duty), Competitor analysis (iRobot Roomba, Eureka Forbes, Mi), Method (perceived-value + competitive parity), Final price. Recommend skimming launch (₹35-45K) for early adopters → progressive cuts as competition increases / scale builds (penetration phase). Counter to competitor cuts: maintain price, improve features, fighter brand at sub-₹25K. Discuss EDLP not viable at launch.

### LQ-10 (Session 11) — IMC plan
**Prompt.** Develop an IMC plan for a Tier-2 city restaurant chain entering Tier-1 cities. Cover: target audience, communication objectives, message strategy, channels mix, budget approach, and measurement.

**Skeleton**: Target — urban millennial + gen-Z, foodies, family diners. Objectives (AIDA): build awareness in Tier-1, convert to trial, build loyalty. Message: regional authenticity + fresh ingredients + experience. Channels mix: social (Instagram/YouTube/influencer), OOH (high-traffic), digital ads (Swiggy/Zomato banners), PR (food critics), in-restaurant experience (events, loyalty), email/SMS for repeat. Budget: objective-and-task method (vs %-of-sales). Measure: brand recall, footfall, online orders, NPS, repeat rate, CAC/LTV.

### LQ-11 (Session 12) — Oreo India IMC (mandatory case)
**Prompt.** Using the **Oreo in India — Launching and Establishing a global brand using IMC effectively case** (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 17), critically analyse how Oreo built a brand in India. Apply the 5 Ms of advertising and discuss IMC integration.

**Skeleton**: Background — Cadbury-Mondelez launched Oreo in India 2011. Insight: Indian families need shared family rituals; Oreo's "twist-lick-dunk" = ritual creator. 5 Ms: Mission — build category + market share; Money — heavy launch budget; Message — "Togetherness in every bite", father-child ritual; Media — TV (heavy launch), digital social (UGC twist-lick-dunk videos), POS in modern trade; Measurement — brand awareness, market share, repeat purchase. IMC integration: TV + Digital + Mom-blogger PR + retail visibility + sales promotion (combo packs). Lessons: cultural insight + emotional positioning + ritual = sustainable brand.

### LQ-12 (Sessions 13-14) — Channel strategy for D2C startup
**Prompt.** A D2C beauty brand wants to expand from its e-commerce-only model to include offline. Design a multi-channel strategy. Discuss channel design, conflict management, and omnichannel integration. Reference the HSTalk on Omni-channel marketing.

**Skeleton**: Channel design steps — analyse customer needs (touch + feel for cosmetics), channel objectives (reach + experience + brand-building), alternatives: own retail flagship + Sephora/Nykaa/Tata Cliq partnerships + modern trade (DMart Beauty, Reliance Trends) + kiosks. Evaluate via 3 criteria (economic, control, adaptive). Conflict management: clear pricing parity (no online discount sabotage), exclusive SKUs per channel, transparent allocation, channel-specific service. Omnichannel: unified inventory, BOPIS (buy-online-pickup-in-store), endless aisle in stores, social commerce, app loyalty. KPI: blended CAC, channel margin contribution, NPS by channel.

### LQ-13 (Session 14) — E-commerce & M-commerce trends
**Prompt.** Discuss the evolution of e-commerce and m-commerce in India. Discuss disintermediation, reintermediation, last-mile innovations, and quick commerce (Blinkit/Zepto/Instamart). Implications for traditional retail.

**Skeleton**: 2008-15: e-commerce growth (Flipkart, Amazon); cash-on-delivery, RTO. 2015-22: M-commerce dominance (UPI, smartphone penetration); social commerce (Meesho). 2022-26: Quick commerce 10-min delivery (Blinkit, Zepto, Instamart) — dark stores, hub-and-spoke. Disintermediation: D2C brands bypassing retail. Reintermediation: aggregators (Nykaa, Pharmeasy). Implications for traditional retail: digital store-fronts, hyperlocal, omnichannel, focus on assortment + experience that ecommerce can't replicate. Cite HSTalk on Omni-channel.

### LQ-14 (Session 15) — Marketing regulation & ethics
**Prompt.** Discuss the role of ASCI and CCPA in regulating marketing in India. Highlight three recent areas of concern (greenwashing, influencer disclosure, AI-generated content). Propose a self-regulatory framework for a brand.

**Skeleton**: ASCI — voluntary self-regulation, code on truthful, decent, honest, safe ads; influencer guidelines (2021); endorsement disclosure rules. CCPA — statutory, product liability, e-commerce rules. Greenwashing — ASCI's 2024 guidelines; require evidence for sustainability claims. Influencer disclosure — #ad #sponsored mandatory; child influencer rules. AI-generated content — emerging regulations on deepfakes, AI labelling. Self-regulation framework: pre-publishing legal review, evidence binder for claims, influencer contract clauses, post-launch monitoring, complaint handling.

### LQ-15 (Session 15) — B2B marketing & buying centre
**Prompt.** Discuss the unique characteristics of industrial (B2B) marketing. Apply the buying centre concept to a B2B SaaS sale to an Indian bank. Recommend a sales & marketing strategy.

**Skeleton**: Characteristics — derived demand, fewer/larger buyers, multi-stakeholder, professional buyers, longer cycle, geographic concentration. Buying centre roles for SaaS to bank: Initiator (Head of Digital), User (branch staff, ops team), Influencer (CIO, IT analyst), Decider (CFO/MD), Approver (Board), Buyer (Procurement), Gatekeeper (CIO's office, legal). Strategy: account-based marketing (ABM), tailored content for each role (CTO — security, CFO — TCO, CHRO — change management), demos for users, ROI calculator for procurement, SLA + compliance for legal. Long sales cycle implies pipeline + nurture. Reference targeted segmentation (HSTalk on Targeting and Segmentation).

### LQ-16 (Session 16) — Marketing strategy for Amazon India (mandatory case)
**Prompt.** Using the **Amazon India case** (Pearson Indian Cases, Case 21), critically analyse how Amazon adapted its global retail playbook to India. Apply Ansoff matrix, BCG matrix, and STP to evaluate.

**Skeleton**: Amazon India context — vs Flipkart and Reliance JioMart; regulatory constraints (FDI rules); language + payment + logistics challenges. Adaptations: Amazon Seller Network for SMBs, Hindi/regional language UI, Amazon Pay UPI, Cloudtail/Appario marketplace structure (later restructured), Amazon Prime + Prime Video bundling. Ansoff: Market Penetration (deep India coverage), Market Development (Tier-2/3), Product Development (Amazon Fresh, Pharmacy), Diversification (AWS, Studios). BCG: Marketplace = Star/Question Mark; AWS India = Star; Prime Video = Question Mark; Kindle = Cash Cow (small but profitable). STP: segments — urban premium (Prime); mass-market (cash-on-delivery, regional language); SMB sellers (Amazon Saheli). Position: trust + selection + delivery. Lessons: localisation, ecosystem-building, patient capital, regulatory navigation.

### LQ-18 (Cross-cutting) — Sustainable marketing (HSTalks tie-in)
**Prompt.** Discuss "Sustainable advertising" (HSTalk by Thomas Kolster). What is "Goodvertising" and why does it work? Recommend a sustainable marketing approach for an Indian FMCG brand to address greenwashing concerns.

**Skeleton**: Goodvertising = advertising that is good for the brand AND good for society. Why works: Gen Z values brand purpose, ESG investor preference, regulatory tailwinds. Indian FMCG case (HUL/ITC/Dabur): tangible action + transparent reporting > slogans. Avoid greenwashing: substantiate claims (third-party certifications, life-cycle assessment), avoid vague terms ("eco-friendly" alone), align with ASCI 2024 guidelines, integrate with operations (don't market what you can't deliver).
