### Q1. (15 m) Discuss the evolution of marketing from production orientation to AI-driven holistic marketing. Illustrate each phase with an example from Indian or global business history.

**Framework**

| Phase | Premise | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Production | Make goods cheaply, supply will create demand | Ford Model T; HMT watches in 1960s India |
| Product | Build superior products; customers will come | Sony Walkman, BlackBerry early years |
| Selling | Aggressive selling/promotion to push existing inventory | Insurance hard-selling, encyclopaedia salesmen |
| Marketing | Identify customer needs first, then design | Hindustan Unilever's rural sachet revolution |
| Societal | Customer + society + ESG | Tata's "Trusts" model; Patagonia |
| Holistic | Relationship + integration + internal + performance | Apple's ecosystem play |
| Digital | Data-driven targeting, real-time engagement | Flipkart Big Billion Days, Cred |
| AI-first | Personalisation at scale, predictive, GenAI | Netflix recs, ChatGPT-powered marketing copy at HUL |

**Conclusion**: Each phase didn't replace the previous; they layered. Modern firms still need product excellence (Apple), relationship depth (Tata), and now AI-fluency (Reliance Jio).

### Q2. (12 m) Apply the PESTEL framework to assess the macro environment for an Indian electric two-wheeler manufacturer (e.g., Ather, Ola Electric) launching in 2026.

**Answer framework**

- **Political**: FAME-III incentives, state-level subsidies (Maharashtra, Karnataka), tariff war with China on lithium imports. Risk: subsidy phase-out.
- **Economic**: GDP growth ~6.5%, rising disposable income, fuel-price volatility favouring EV switch. Two-wheeler market ₹X lakh crore.
- **Social**: Tier-2/3 youth aspiration, Gen Z preference for clean tech, female-rider segment growing.
- **Technological**: Battery cost ₹/kWh declining 8% per year; battery-swapping ecosystem maturing; OTA software updates as differentiator.
- **Environmental**: Mandatory EPR for batteries; pressure on grid but rooftop solar growing.
- **Legal**: BIS standards for batteries (AIS-156); DPDP Act 2023 for connected-vehicle data; ARAI homologation cycles.

**Strategic implications**:
- Capitalise on subsidies via aggressive pricing.
- Invest in service/charging network as moat.
- Position on connected/AI features.
- Hedge battery-supply risk via diversified sourcing.

### Q3. (10 m) Discuss the consumer decision process. Map out a typical Indian online shopper's journey for buying a smartphone worth ₹40,000.

**Framework**

**Five-stage process applied**

1. **Need recognition** — old phone slow, peer using new launch, festival season trigger.
2. **Information search** — YouTube reviewer videos, Reddit threads, comparison sites (91Mobiles, Smartprix), brand websites, Amazon/Flipkart product pages, friend recommendations.
3. **Evaluation of alternatives** — Spec comparison (camera, battery, processor), brand reputation (Apple, Samsung, Xiaomi, OnePlus), price-to-value, after-sales service, EMI options.
4. **Purchase decision** — Festival sale + bank cashback + exchange offer trigger purchase. Channel: Flipkart Big Billion Days, possibly with no-cost EMI.
5. **Post-purchase** — Joy of unboxing, social-media flex (Instagram), review writing, peer recommendation; or dissonance if performance disappoints → return / RMA.

**Marketer takeaways**:
- Show up at every stage with relevant content.
- Reviews and influencer credibility crucial in evaluation.
- Frictionless EMI/exchange unlocks the purchase.
- Post-purchase delight (extended warranty, free accessories) drives advocacy.

### Q4. (15 m) Mini-case: A premium speciality coffee brand wants to expand from metros to Tier-2/3 Indian cities. Apply the STP framework to recommend a launch strategy.

**Answer framework**

**Segmentation (4 m)**
- *Demographic*: Age 22–40, monthly household income ₹50k+, professionals.
- *Geographic*: Top 30 Tier-2/3 cities — Pune, Coimbatore, Indore, Lucknow, Vizag, Chandigarh, etc.
- *Psychographic*: Aspirational, well-travelled, brand-conscious, prefers experiences over possessions.
- *Behavioural*: Daily/weekly cafe visitors; café as workspace, social spot; high engagement on Instagram.

**Targeting (3 m)**
- Differentiated targeting — two segments:
  - *Office-going professionals*: morning take-away coffee.
  - *Aspirational youth (students + early-career)*: weekend hangout, study/work spot.
- Concentrate first on cities with high purchasing power and existing café culture.

**Positioning (4 m)**
- POPs: Espresso-based menu, comfortable seating, Wi-Fi, food.
- POD: "Speciality single-origin coffee, ethically sourced, café-as-third-place."
- Statement: *"For young urban professionals who appreciate quality, [brand] is the speciality café that delivers globally curated coffee in a community-driven space because we source directly from Indian estates and train every barista to international standards."*

**Mix (4 m)**
- Product: Speciality coffees + locally inspired food (filter-coffee variant).
- Price: Premium-but-accessible (₹250 cappuccino) below Starbucks but above Café Coffee Day.
- Place: Standalone outlets in malls + high-street; D2C app for delivery; partnership with Swiggy/Zomato.
- Promotion: Influencer-led launch in each city, in-app loyalty, Instagram-first content, limited-edition city-specific drinks.

### Q5. (10 m) Discuss the Product Life Cycle. Recommend marketing strategies for a product currently in the maturity stage (example: Maggi 2-minute noodles).

**Framework**

**PLC stages and characteristics** (3 m): introduction → growth → maturity → decline. Maturity: peak sales, slowing growth, intense competition, profit margins under pressure.

**Strategies for Maggi at maturity (7 m)**
- **Product modification**: new variants (Atta, Oats, Veg Masala), premium tiers (Hot Heads, fusion).
- **Market modification**: target new users — corporate offices (vending machines), micro-meals for travellers.
- **Marketing-mix modification**: pack-size SKUs (5-pack family), cross-sell with sauces.
- **Repositioning**: Health, convenience, "2-minute happiness."
- **Premiumisation**: Maggi Cuppa Mania for on-the-go.
- **Geographic expansion**: deeper rural penetration.
- **Co-branding / collaboration**: limited-edition with celebs/influencers.
- **Defend share**: aggressive trade promotions to retailers; counter Wai-Wai, Yippee.
- **Loyalty plays**: app-based recipe content, recipe contests.

### Q6. (15 m) Discuss Keller's Customer-Based Brand Equity (CBBE) pyramid. Apply it to a brand of your choice (e.g., Apple, Tata, Royal Enfield).

**Framework**

**CBBE pyramid (5 m)**
1. **Brand identity (salience)** — depth and breadth of awareness. Who are you?
2. **Brand meaning** — performance + imagery. What are you?
3. **Brand response** — judgements + feelings. What about you?
4. **Brand resonance** — loyalty, attachment, community, engagement. What about you and me?

**Apple application (10 m)**
- *Salience* — instant recognition globally; logo simplicity; aspirational presence.
- *Performance* — premium engineering, integrated hardware-software, seamless updates.
- *Imagery* — minimalist design, creative-class lifestyle, sustainability commitments.
- *Judgements* — perceived quality (highest), credibility (high), superiority (high), consideration (very high in target).
- *Feelings* — pride, excitement, security, social approval.
- *Resonance* — ecosystem lock-in (iCloud, AirDrop, iMessage), strong communities (developers, photographers), loyal repeat buyers, willingness to pay premium, evangelism.

Apple's brand equity allows premium pricing, faster product launches, easier extensions (Watch, Vision), and resilience to crises.

### Q7. (12 m) Compare cost-plus pricing, value-based pricing and dynamic pricing. Identify when each is appropriate.

**Framework**

| Method | How | Best for |
|--------|-----|----------|
| Cost-plus | Cost + markup | Stable B2B, regulated utilities, tenders |
| Value-based | Price = perceived customer value | Differentiated brands (Apple, premium services) |
| Dynamic | Real-time, demand-supply matching | Travel, ride-hailing, e-commerce flash sales |

**Trade-offs**:
- Cost-plus: simple, fair-looking, ignores customer willingness to pay → leaves money on table or prices out market.
- Value-based: maximises capture but requires deep customer research and brand strength.
- Dynamic: maximises yield, can damage trust if perceived as unfair (Uber surge backlash).

**Recommendation**: Hybrid — value-based as anchor, dynamic for time-sensitive inventory, cost-plus as floor.

### Q8. (10 m) Explain channel design and channel intensity. Recommend the right channel structure for (a) a new D2C ayurvedic skincare brand and (b) an industrial robotics manufacturer.

**Framework**

**Channel design** considers:
- Customer needs (volume, location, service).
- Product characteristics (perishable, complex, expensive).
- Company resources.
- Competitive intensity.

**Channel intensity**: intensive / selective / exclusive.

**(a) D2C ayurvedic skincare**
- D2C app/web (zero-level) for direct customer + data.
- Marketplaces (Amazon, Nykaa) — selective; high-traffic but cannibalisation risk.
- Limited offline (modern trade like Shoppers Stop, Health & Glow) — selective for trial.
- Influencer & affiliate channels.

**(b) Industrial robotics manufacturer**
- Direct sales (account-based) for marquee accounts (auto, aerospace).
- Authorised system-integrators (exclusive territories) for mid-market.
- Possibly online catalogue for spares + after-sales.
- Strong service & training network = competitive moat.

### Q9. (15 m) Design an Integrated Marketing Communications (IMC) plan for the launch of a new electric scooter targeting urban millennials.

**Framework**

**Objective**: 25,000 unit sales in first 6 months across 8 cities; brand awareness 70% in target.

**Target & insight**: 25–35-year-old urban professionals; sustainability-conscious; fuel costs hurting; aspirational.

**Big Idea**: "Move Smarter, Move Cleaner."

**Channels (paid, owned, earned, shared)**
| Channel | Activity | KPI |
|---------|----------|-----|
| TV/OTT | 30-second hero film; sports IPL slots | Reach, recall |
| Digital — Search | "Best EV scooter 2026" SEO + SEM | CPC, conversions |
| Social — Meta + Insta | Reels challenge "#MoveSmarter" | Engagement, UGC |
| Influencer | 20 micro-influencers in cycling/sustainability/tech | Reach + leads |
| YouTube | Hands-on reviews, ride-along | Watch time, leads |
| OOH | Hoardings near EV charging zones | Recall |
| PR | Founder interviews on Mint, ET | Earned media |
| Experiential | Pop-up test-ride events in metros | Test drives |
| Retail / D2C app | Booking + financing flow | Conversion rate |
| Email/CRM | Nurture pre-bookings → conversion | Conversion |

**Budget split (illustrative ₹50 cr)**: Digital 40%, Influencer 15%, OTT 15%, Experiential 10%, OOH 10%, PR 5%, CRM/Tech 5%.

**Measurement**: Brand awareness (lift study), test drives, bookings, conversion %, CAC, ROAS.

### Q10. (12 m) Discuss the SERVQUAL gap model. Apply it to diagnose service quality issues at an Indian airline.

**Framework**

**SERVQUAL** — five dimensions: Reliability, Assurance, Tangibles, Empathy, Responsiveness (RATER).

**Five gaps**:
- Gap 1: Customer expectations vs management perception. (Mgmt thinks customers want low fares; customers want on-time + clean cabin too.)
- Gap 2: Mgmt perception vs service-quality specifications. (Mgmt knows customers want clean cabins but doesn't budget enough turnaround time.)
- Gap 3: Specifications vs actual delivery. (Cabin crew rushed, leaves dirt.)
- Gap 4: Delivery vs external communication. (Ads show smiling, helpful crew; reality differs.)
- Gap 5: Customer expected vs perceived service. The one customers feel.

**Application to airline**:
- *Reliability gap*: Frequent delays — re-engineer turnaround processes; transparent on-time scorecard.
- *Assurance*: Train ground staff to handle disruptions confidently; empower with policy authority.
- *Tangibles*: Aircraft cleanliness audit, seat upholstery refresh.
- *Empathy*: Profile high-value flyers in CRM; proactive accommodation.
- *Responsiveness*: 24×7 social-media response team, voice + chat coverage.

**Recovery plan**: Service Recovery Paradox — well-handled disruptions can lift loyalty; train and empower frontline.

### Q11. (10 m) Discuss customer lifetime value (CLV). Why is CLV becoming the central marketing metric in the AI era?

**Framework**

**Definition** — net present value of cash flows from a customer over the lifetime of the relationship.

Simple formula: CLV = (Annual margin × Avg lifespan) − Acquisition cost.

NPV form: CLV = Σ_t (Margin_t × Retention^t) / (1+r)^t.

**Why CLV is rising**
- AI enables prediction of CLV at the individual level; hence personalised acquisition spend.
- Subscription / SaaS / D2C models depend on retention not transactions.
- Allows prioritising high-value customers in service, upsell, cross-sell.
- Justifies long-payback acquisition (e.g., Cred's huge initial losses).
- Aligns marketing with finance (NPV thinking).

**Levers to grow CLV**
1. Increase margin per period (premium tiers, bundles).
2. Reduce churn (retention programmes, success teams).
3. Extend lifespan (community, ecosystem lock-in).
4. Reduce CAC through referrals, organic.
5. Cross-sell / up-sell using AI propensity models.

### Q12. (15 m) Discuss the Ansoff growth matrix. Apply it to suggest growth options for a mid-sized Indian B2B SaaS company.

**Framework**

**Ansoff matrix**
| Product → \\ Market ↓ | Existing | New |
|---|---|---|
| Existing | Market penetration | Market development |
| New | Product development | Diversification |

Risk increases moving down-right.

**Application — B2B SaaS firm**

**Existing product / Existing market — Market Penetration**
- Increase usage (more seats per account).
- Win competitor accounts via aggressive comparative campaigns.
- Pricing tactics (annual prepay discounts).

**Existing product / New market — Market Development**
- Geographic: enter SEA / Middle East.
- Vertical: from BFSI into healthcare.
- Channel: launch partner-led sales programme.

**New product / Existing market — Product Development**
- Add AI-augmented features (GenAI summarisation).
- Add complementary modules (new SKUs in same suite).
- Move from on-prem add-on to SaaS.

**New product / New market — Diversification**
- Spin off a vertical SaaS (high risk).
- Acquire a complementary firm in a new geography.

**Recommendation**:
- 60% bandwidth: Penetration + Product development (lowest risk, fastest payoff).
- 30%: Market development (geography expansion).
- 10%: Strategic diversification via M&A — selective, well-funded.

KPIs: net-revenue retention, gross logo retention, expansion revenue %, ARR growth, payback period.
