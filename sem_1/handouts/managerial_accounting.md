### **Introduction to Accounting**

- Understand the concept and importance of accounting in business.

- The importance of financial and management accounting as a source of
  value creation.

- Linkage between Business Strategy **and** Financial Statements to
  assess business performance (maximize operating profit).

- Overview of the Financial Accounting Process (Cycles) and expected
  outcomes.

### **Accounting Principles and Financial Reporting Framework**

- Fundamental principles and assumptions in financial accounting.

- Understanding Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP)**.**

- Overview of International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) and key
  IAS/IFRS standards:

  - IFRS S1 & S2 (Sustainability and Climate Disclosures)

  - IFRS 5, 7, 8, 13

  - IFRS 15 (Revenue), IAS 2 (Inventory), IAS 16 (PPE)

- Comparison of US GAAP vs IFRS, especially on R&D accounting.

- Identify accounting red flags: bias and noise due to rule
  rigidity**.**

- Understand areas where financial reporting falls short.

### **Accounting Mechanics and Transaction Flow**

- Categories of Accounts: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Income, Expenses

- Understanding the Accounting Equation and Dual-Entry System

- Flow of accounting transactions:

  - Recording in Journal (book of first entry)

  - Posting to Ledger

  - Preparation of Trial Balance

  - Compilation of Financial Statements

### **Understanding Financial Statements: Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement**

- Structure and purpose of the Income Statement (Profit & Loss Account)

- Structure and purpose of the Balance Sheet

- Components and interpretation of the Cash Flow Statement**:**

  - Operating activities

  - Investing activities

  - Financing activities

- Preparation and interconnection of financial statements.

### **Integrating Financial Statements: A Holistic View**

- Integrating P&L**,** Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement for
  business performance analysis.

- Using real-world financial statements for business evaluation.

### **Ratio Analysis – Concepts and Classifications**

- Types of financial ratios:

  - Liquidity

  - Solvency

  - Profitability

  - Efficiency/Activity

- Definitions, formulae, and usage of key financial ratios.

### **Ratio Analysis – Analysis and Interpretation**

- Trend analysis and comparison with industry benchmarks

- Case-based evaluation of financial ratios

- Using real-life company financials to evaluate performance.

### **Cost Concepts and Cost-Volume-Profit (CVP) Analysis**

- Types of Costs:

  - Fixed, Variable, Direct, Indirect

  - Prime and Conversion Costs

- Break-even Point calculation and interpretation

- Contribution Margin and Margin of Safety

- Use of CVP analysis in short-term decision making.

### **Managerial Decisions Using Cost Accounting Information**

- Cost-based decision-making scenarios:

  - Make or Buy decisions

  - Special order acceptance

- Cost considerations in pricing strategy and profit planning

### **Cost Allocation, Inventory Valuation & Budgeting**

- Impact of Inventory Valuation Methods (FIFO, LIFO, Weighted Average)
  on financial statements

- Types and preparation of Budgets**:**

  - Master Budget

  - Operating Budget

  - Capital Budget

  - Zero-Based Budget

- Variance Analysis and its importance in performance monitoring.

**<u>Experiential Learning Components (ELC):</u>**

> This course will feature experiential learning components in the form
> of projects / assignments. ELC is designed to enable the participants
> to learn by doing. This will also form part of the Evaluation
> Components for the course.

**Structure of Experiential Learning:**

- Each participant will select his / her own organization (If the
  selected organization does not have an annual report then select any
  other organization of similar in nature) and collect annual report
  containing financial statements (statement of income and expenditure,
  statement of sources & application of funds and cash flow statement)
  for 5 years.

**What will you do?**

- Apply concepts discussed in class, and do financial statements
  analysis and interpretation to understand financial health of selected
  organization(s) using tools (ratios) and techniques (costing)

- You need to analyze and interpret financial health of an organization
  i.e.

  1.  how organization has been doing for last 5 years (trend analysis),

  2.  how organization is doing vis-à-vis its competitors in last 5
      years (comparative analysis) and

  3.  how organization is doing vis-à-vis its industry average in last 5
      years (industry comparative analysis).

**How will you do?**

Collect financial statements of organizations from Bloomberg database or
any other open sources and, analyse and interpret financial health in
terms of its

1.  Riskiness arising out of its cost structure (Do CPV Analysis),

2.  Liquidity position i.e. organization(s) ability to meet current
    liabilities from current assets and ability to convert current
    assets into cash (Assets Utilization),

3.  Leverage position – Both Operating leverage (how EBIT is sensitive
    due to change in revenue) and financial leverage (how EPS in
    sensitive due to change in EBIT), Organizations ability to meet
    financial obligations (Interest on loan), etc.

4.  Profitability Position– Economic Profitability (profit generating
    capacity from effective utilization of its assets or capacity),
    Commercial Profitability (profit generating capacity from revenue)
    and Owners Profitability (contribution of profit to owners)

**Who will do?**

> This experiential learning assignment can be done by one student or
> group of students. Group can be of 2 or 4 or 6 or 8 students only.

 

- Individual (1 student) - Analyze and interpret financial health of an
  organization for 5 years (Doing Trend Analysis of 5 years).

- Group of 2 students - Analyze and interpret financial health of an
  organization of 5 years (Doing Trend Analysis of 5 years of one
  organization). Obviously this group work has to be better than of
  individual submission.

- Group of 4 students - Analyze and interpret financial health of two
  organizations of 5 years (Do Trend & Comparative Analysis of two
  organizations in same industry group).

- Group of 6 students - Analyze and interpret financial health of three
  organizations of 5 years (Do Trend, Comparative and Industry Analysis
  of three organizations in same industry group).

- Group of 8 students- Analyze and interpret financial health of four
  organizations of 5 years (Do Trend, Comparative and Industry Analysis
  of four organizations in same industry group).

**How will you submit?**

1.  Name of the participant/group members and their IDs should be
    mentioned on the first page.

2.  For group assignments, one person can submit it on the behalf of
    their group members.
