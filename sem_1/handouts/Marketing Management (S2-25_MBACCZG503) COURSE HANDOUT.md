**Course Description:**

Definition and scope, Consumer behavior, competitive behavior, Demand
estimation, new product introduction, Product/brand management, Pricing
policies, Channels of distribution, credit management,

Advertising and other sales promotion, Positioning, marketing
regulation, Market research basics of industrial marketing.

**Course Objectives:**

|  |  |
|:---|:---|
| **No.** | **Course Objective** |
| **CO1** | To understand the fundamental marketing concepts and the processes that influence the market orientation of a firm. |
| **CO2** | To understand the role of marketing within the organization. |
| **CO3** | To analyze the consumer behaviour and its importance for marketing in the competitive world. |
| **CO4** | To critically examine the marketing process and its environmental context, with focus on the strategic use of the marketing mix. |
| **CO5** | To equip learners with foundational knowledge of market research principles and their application in understanding industrial markets and decision-making. |

**<u>Learning Outcomes: Students will be able to</u>**

|  |  |
|:--:|----|
| **LO1** | Demonstrate a comprehensive understanding of core marketing concepts such as value creation, segmentation, targeting, positioning, and branding, and explain how these concepts contribute to developing a market-oriented approach within an organization. |
| **LO2** | Evaluate the strategic role of the marketing function to achieve business goals, contributes to cross-functional integration, and drives innovation and customer satisfaction in both domestic and global markets. |
| **LO3** | Analyze consumer behavior using psychological, social, and cultural frameworks, and formulate effective marketing strategies in dynamic and competitive market. |
| **LO4** | Critically examine the components of the marketing process—market analysis, opportunity identification, strategy formulation, and tactical implementation—and apply the marketing mix. |
| **LO5** | Apply foundational market research methods to gather insights and interpret data for informed marketing decision-making in both consumer and industrial markets. |

**Cesim Simulation:**

With the objective of enhancing the experiential learning in our
Marketing Management course, we have subscribed to CESIM Simulation.
This simulation is made available to all Students as part of the
Management Lab. The business management simulations from CESIM help
improve decision-making skills, business competence, and overall
engagement in the online learning environment.

Guidelines:

The simulation game is best viewed on a desktop/laptop. It is
recommended to play the game with decent internet speed.

The simulation game consists of 1 practice and 3 actual rounds (1 round
= 1 financial year)

The decisions can be taken or changed multiple times before the round
deadline. Once the deadline is over the game will proceed automatically
to the next round with the performance report of the previous round
visible in the Results tab.

To save decisions in each round, visit Decision Checklist \>\> Copy as
Team Decisions

**<u>Experiential Learning Assignments</u>**

The experiential learning assignments covered in this course are:-

**<u>Experiential Learning- Assignment</u>**

**<u>Objective:</u>**

To stimulate and check the understanding and ability to apply the
marketing concepts covered during the course with real life brand and
products/services.

**<u>Scope:</u>**

Comprehensive analysis of any ten different marketing concepts/model to
define and then apply it in real-life illustrations. They could include
ads (print, billboards, shop signage, TV or radio ads), packages, or any
other form of marketing communication based on your market visit or
observation.

This is an individual assignment.

Consider any Indian or Global brand or different brands.

1)  You are free to fill up the definition part from the lecture slides.
    They don’t carry any credits. However, omission or wrong definition
    would lead to a complete forfeiture of marks for that question

2)  Please give your justification for the illustration. Explain why you
    think the example used by you serves as an apt illustration of the
    concept.

3)  You are encouraged to use examples of small companies or regional
    brands. You are free to use examples of locality clinics, coaching
    centers or restaurants, etc. based on your real-life observation or
    experience during the market visit

Each of the above sections need to be dealt with and explained in a
detailed manner.

Please include diagrams and pictures, wherever possible.
