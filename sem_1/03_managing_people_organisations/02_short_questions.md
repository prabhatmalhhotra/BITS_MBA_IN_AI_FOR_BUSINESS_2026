# Managing People and Organizations — Short Questions (handout-aligned)

> Aligned to `MBACC ZG511` 16-session plan. Mid-Sem (closed book, **35 % weight**) covers S1-S8 (OB foundations, Individual). Comprehensive (open book, 2.5 hrs) covers S1-S16.
> Each Q has a 1-3 line ideal answer.

## Session 1 — Introduction to OB
1. **Define Organisational Behaviour.** Field that studies impact of individuals, groups and structure on behaviour in organisations to improve effectiveness.
2. **List Mintzberg's 3 categories of managerial roles.** Interpersonal (Figurehead, Leader, Liaison); Informational (Monitor, Disseminator, Spokesperson); Decisional (Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator).
3. **State Katz's 3 managerial skills.** Technical, Human (Interpersonal), Conceptual; conceptual becomes more important at higher levels.
4. **State Fayol's four managerial functions.** Planning, Organising, Leading, Controlling.
5. **Disciplines contributing to OB.** Psychology, Social Psychology, Sociology, Anthropology, Political Science.
6. **Why are interpersonal skills critical for managers?** Most workplace performance issues are people issues; soft skills differentiate mid-to-senior leaders.

## Session 2 — Introduction to HRM
7. **Define HRM.** Strategic and integrated approach to managing the people who individually and collectively contribute to organisational objectives.
8. **State four functions of HRM.** Acquisition (Recruit/Select), Development (Training), Motivation (Compensation/Performance), Maintenance (Welfare/IR).
9. **Distinguish HRM from Personnel Management.** PM = traditional, transactional, compliance focus; HRM = strategic, integrated, value-creation focus.
10. **What is the strategic role of HR (Ulrich's model)?** Strategic Partner, Administrative Expert, Employee Champion, Change Agent.
11. **Why is HRM context-sensitive?** It must adapt to economic, technological, demographic, legal, and cultural environments of the organisation.

## Session 3 — Attitudes, Job Satisfaction, Diversity
12. **Three components of an attitude.** Cognitive (belief), Affective (feeling), Behavioural (intention to act).
13. **Define Job Satisfaction.** Positive feeling about one's job resulting from evaluation of its characteristics.
14. **State 4 outcomes of job satisfaction.** Higher productivity, OCB, customer satisfaction; lower absenteeism/turnover/CWB.
15. **Define cognitive dissonance.** Psychological discomfort from inconsistency between attitude and behaviour; person motivated to reduce it.
16. **Surface-level vs Deep-level diversity.** Surface = demographic (age, gender, race); Deep = values, personality, beliefs.
17. **Define Organizational Citizenship Behaviour (OCB).** Discretionary behaviour not part of formal job, but contributes to org effectiveness (e.g., helping colleagues).

## Session 4 — Personality and Values
18. **Define personality.** Sum total of ways an individual reacts to and interacts with others; relatively stable.
19. **List Big Five personality dimensions (OCEAN).** Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism (Emotional Stability).
20. **Which Big Five trait best predicts job performance?** Conscientiousness across most jobs.
21. **What is MBTI?** Myers-Briggs Type Indicator — 16 personality types from 4 dichotomies (E/I, S/N, T/F, J/P); popular but limited reliability.
22. **Distinguish terminal and instrumental values (Rokeach).** Terminal = desirable end-states (happiness, freedom); Instrumental = preferred modes of behaviour (honesty, ambition).
23. **State 4 of Hofstede's cultural dimensions.** Power Distance, Individualism-Collectivism, Masculinity-Femininity, Uncertainty Avoidance, Long-term Orientation, Indulgence vs Restraint.

## Session 5 — Perception & Decision Making
24. **Define perception.** Process by which individuals organise and interpret sensory impressions to give meaning to their environment.
25. **What is fundamental attribution error?** Tendency to under-estimate situational factors and over-estimate personal factors when judging others' behaviour.
26. **Define self-serving bias.** Attribute own successes to internal factors; failures to external/situational factors.
27. **Three steps in rational decision making (any 3).** Define problem → Identify criteria → Allocate weights → Generate alternatives → Evaluate → Choose best.
28. **Bounded rationality (Simon).** People simplify complex problems and seek **satisficing** (good-enough) solutions, not optimal ones.
29. **Common decision biases (any 3).** Anchoring, Availability, Confirmation, Overconfidence, Hindsight, Escalation of commitment.

## Session 6 — Emotions and Moods
30. **Distinguish emotion vs mood.** Emotion = intense, short-lived, with specific cause; Mood = mild, longer-lasting, often without clear cause.
31. **Define emotional labour.** Employee expression of organisationally desired emotions during interactions (e.g., service smile).
32. **What is surface acting vs deep acting?** Surface = display emotions you don't feel; Deep = modify inner feelings to match required emotions; deep acting causes less burnout.
33. **Define Emotional Intelligence (Goleman).** Ability to perceive, understand, manage, and use emotions in self and others; 4 components: Self-awareness, Self-management, Social-awareness, Relationship-management.
34. **One application of EI in workplace.** Conflict management, leadership effectiveness, customer service, team collaboration.

## Session 7 — Motivation Concepts
35. **Define motivation.** Processes that account for an individual's intensity, direction, and persistence of effort toward achieving a goal.
36. **State Maslow's 5 needs hierarchy.** Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-actualisation.
37. **Difference: Theory X vs Theory Y (McGregor).** X = workers dislike work, need control; Y = workers see work as natural, can self-direct.
38. **Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory.** Hygiene factors prevent dissatisfaction (salary, working conditions); Motivators create satisfaction (achievement, recognition, growth).
39. **State McClelland's 3 needs.** Need for Achievement (nAch), Affiliation (nAff), Power (nPow).
40. **Equity Theory (Adams).** Employees compare own input/output ratio with others'; perceived inequity → reduce inputs, change outputs, leave, or alter perception.
41. **Expectancy Theory (Vroom).** Motivation = Expectancy (E→P) × Instrumentality (P→R) × Valence (value of R).
42. **Self-Determination Theory.** Intrinsic motivation thrives with autonomy, competence, and relatedness; over-reliance on extrinsic rewards may undermine intrinsic motivation.

## Session 8 — Motivation Applications
43. **MBO (Management by Objectives).** Specific, measurable goals jointly set by manager and employee; reviewed periodically.
44. **Job characteristics that motivate (JCM).** Skill variety, Task identity, Task significance, Autonomy, Feedback → Motivating Potential Score (MPS).
45. **What is job enrichment?** Vertical loading: adding planning/control responsibilities to make work more meaningful.
46. **What is job rotation?** Periodic shifting of employees across jobs/tasks to reduce monotony and broaden skills.
47. **Variable pay programs (any 3).** Piece-rate, Profit sharing, ESOPs, Bonus, Gain-sharing, Skill-based pay.
48. **Goal-Setting Theory (Locke & Latham) — SMART criteria.** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.
49. **What is positive reinforcement (Skinner)?** Following desired behaviour with a positive consequence increases its likelihood of recurrence.

## Session 9 — Foundations of Group Behaviour
50. **Difference: Group vs Team.** Group = shared info; sum of contributions. Team = collective effort, complementary skills, shared accountability; output > sum.
51. **Tuckman's 5-stage group development.** Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning.
52. **Define group cohesiveness.** Degree to which members are attracted to and motivated to stay in the group; high cohesion + high norms → high productivity.
53. **What is groupthink (Janis)?** Norm for consensus overrides realistic appraisal; symptoms: illusion of invulnerability, self-censorship, mindguards.
54. **Define social loafing.** Tendency to exert less effort in a group than alone; reduce by smaller groups, individual accountability, peer evaluation.
55. **What is group polarisation?** Group decision tends to be more extreme than the average of individual member positions.

## Session 10 — Work Teams and Communication
56. **Four types of teams.** Problem-solving, Self-managed, Cross-functional, Virtual.
57. **Characteristics of effective teams.** Common purpose, specific goals, mutual accountability, complementary skills, mutual trust, leadership, conflict management.
58. **Three communication directions.** Downward, Upward, Lateral (also Diagonal).
59. **Communication barriers (any 4).** Filtering, Selective perception, Information overload, Emotions, Language, Silence, Apprehension.
60. **What is active listening?** Full attention, minimal interruption, paraphrasing, asking clarifying questions; key to effective communication.
61. **What is the grapevine?** Informal communication channel through social networks; fast, often accurate, can be managed but not eliminated.

## Session 11 — Leadership Theories & EI
62. **Distinguish leadership and management.** Management = administer, control, status quo; Leadership = inspire, change, vision.
63. **Trait theory.** Searches for personality traits common to all leaders; modest predictive power; Big Five + EI helpful.
64. **Behavioural theories (Ohio State).** Two dimensions: Initiating Structure (task orientation) and Consideration (people orientation).
65. **Fiedler's Contingency Model.** Effectiveness = match between leader's style (LPC) and situational favourableness (leader-member relations, task structure, position power).
66. **Hersey-Blanchard Situational Leadership.** Leader style depends on follower readiness: Telling, Selling, Participating, Delegating.
67. **Transformational vs Transactional leadership.** Transactional = exchange (rewards for performance, management-by-exception). Transformational = inspires via the **4 I's**: **I**dealised influence (charisma/role-model), **I**nspirational motivation (vision, meaning), **I**ntellectual stimulation (challenge assumptions), **I**ndividualised consideration (coach each follower).
68. **Servant leadership.** Leader serves followers; focus on listening, empathy, stewardship, growth of others.
69. **Why is EI important for leadership?** Self-aware leaders manage own emotions, empathise with team, build trust → better team outcomes; meta-analysis: EI predicts leadership effectiveness.

## Session 12 — Organisation Structure
70. **6 elements of organisational structure.** Work specialisation, Departmentalisation, Chain of command, Span of control, Centralisation/Decentralisation, Formalisation.
71. **Define mechanistic structure.** High specialisation, rigid departmentalisation, narrow span, high formalisation, centralised; works in stable environments.
72. **Define organic structure.** Low specialisation, cross-functional teams, wide span, low formalisation, decentralised; works in dynamic environments.
73. **Distinguish functional vs divisional structure.** Functional = grouped by specialty (Marketing, Finance); Divisional = grouped by product/geography/customer.
74. **What is matrix structure?** Dual reporting (functional + project); pros: flexibility, expertise sharing; cons: confusion, power struggles.
75. **What are boundaryless / network organisations?** Eliminate vertical/horizontal boundaries via teams, alliances, outsourcing; enabled by ICT.

## Session 13 — Organisational Culture & Change
76. **Define organisational culture (Schein).** Pattern of basic assumptions a group has invented, discovered, or developed in learning to cope with problems.
77. **Schein's 3 levels of culture.** Artifacts (visible), Espoused values (stated), Basic underlying assumptions (taken for granted).
78. **Strong vs weak culture.** Strong = core values intensely held and widely shared → high commitment, low turnover; risk = resistance to change.
79. **Lewin's 3-step change model.** Unfreeze → Change → Refreeze.
80. **Sources of resistance to change.** Habit, security, economic factors, fear of unknown, selective info processing.
81. **Kotter's 8-step change model.** Establish urgency → Build coalition → Vision → Communicate → Empower action → Short-term wins → Sustain → Anchor in culture.
82. **Force field analysis.** Identify driving forces (for change) and restraining forces (against); strengthen drivers, reduce restrainers.

## Session 14 — Recruitment & Selection
83. **Define recruitment.** Process of attracting qualified applicants for jobs.
84. **Internal vs External sources of recruitment.** Internal: promotion, transfer, employee referral. External: advertisement, campus, agencies, online portals, social media.
85. **Define selection.** Process of choosing the right candidate from the applicant pool, matching person-job and person-organisation fit.
86. **Steps in selection process.** Application → Initial screening → Tests → Interviews → Reference check → Medical → Offer.
87. **Types of selection tests.** Ability/Aptitude, Personality, Interest, Achievement, Work sample, Assessment centre.
88. **What is structured interview?** Pre-determined questions asked of all candidates; higher reliability and validity than unstructured.
89. **What is realistic job preview (RJP)?** Sharing both positive and negative aspects of a job to set accurate expectations; reduces early turnover.
90. **Common selection errors.** Halo effect, similar-to-me bias, contrast effect, primacy/recency, stereotyping.

## Session 15 — Compensation Management
91. **Define compensation.** All forms of financial returns and tangible services and benefits employees receive as part of employment.
92. **Components of compensation.** Direct (basic, allowances, incentives, bonus); Indirect (PF, gratuity, insurance, leave, perks).
93. **Job evaluation methods (any 2).** Ranking, Job Classification, Point method, Factor comparison.
94. **What is broadbanding?** Collapsing many narrow pay grades into a few wide bands → flexibility, supports flat structures.
95. **Internal vs External equity.** Internal = fairness across jobs within firm. External = competitive vs market.
96. **What is pay-for-performance?** Linking compensation to individual/team/organisation performance; merit pay, bonus, incentive, ESOP.
97. **Define total rewards.** Comprehensive view: compensation + benefits + work-life balance + performance & recognition + development & career.

## Session 16 — Performance Appraisal & Management
98. **Define performance appraisal.** Systematic evaluation of an employee's job performance and potential for development.
99. **Purpose of performance appraisal.** Compensation decisions, training needs, promotion, succession planning, feedback, motivation, legal documentation.
100. **Traditional vs Modern methods of appraisal.** Traditional: Ranking, Forced Distribution, Graphic Rating Scale, Critical Incident, Essay. Modern: BARS, MBO, 360-degree, Assessment Centre.
101. **What is BARS?** Behaviourally Anchored Rating Scale — combines critical incidents with rating scale; specific behavioural anchors per scale point.
102. **What is 360-degree feedback?** Multi-source feedback from supervisors, peers, subordinates, customers, and self; comprehensive view but expensive.
103. **Common appraisal errors.** Halo, Horn, Leniency, Strictness, Central tendency, Recency, Similarity, Stereotyping.
104. **Difference: Performance Appraisal vs Performance Management.** Appraisal = periodic event, evaluative. Performance Management = continuous, developmental, integrated cycle (planning → coaching → review → development).
105. **What is OKR?** Objectives and Key Results; popularised by Google; ambitious objectives + 3-5 measurable key results; quarterly cadence.
