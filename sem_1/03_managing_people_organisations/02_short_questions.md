# MPO — Short Questions (with Answers)

> 35 short answers, 2–5 marks each.

---

## Set A — OB foundations

**Q1. Define organisational behaviour. State its three levels of analysis.**
OB is the study of how individuals, groups and structure influence behaviour in organisations to improve effectiveness. Three levels: individual, group, organisation system.

**Q2. State any three disciplines that contribute to OB.**
Psychology (individual behaviour), sociology (groups), social psychology (attitudes), anthropology (culture), political science (power).

**Q3. List three challenges facing modern organisations.**
Workforce diversity, technology/AI disruption, gig economy, globalisation, mental health.

**Q4. Differentiate Theory X and Theory Y.**
Theory X: people dislike work, must be coerced, prefer direction. Theory Y: people view work as natural, accept responsibility, exercise self-direction.

**Q5. State the Big Five personality dimensions.**
OCEAN — Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism. Conscientiousness predicts performance most strongly.

## Set B — Perception & learning

**Q6. Define attribution. State three factors that determine internal vs external attribution.**
Attribution = process of assigning causes to behaviour. Factors: distinctiveness, consensus, consistency.

**Q7. What is the fundamental attribution error?**
Tendency to over-attribute others' behaviour to internal/dispositional factors and underweight situational factors.

**Q8. Differentiate classical and operant conditioning.**
Classical: paired-stimuli automatic response (Pavlov). Operant: behaviour shaped by consequences — reinforcement, punishment (Skinner).

**Q9. State four reinforcement schedules.**
Continuous, fixed-interval, variable-interval, fixed-ratio, variable-ratio. Variable-ratio is most resistant to extinction.

## Set C — Motivation

**Q10. State Maslow's hierarchy of needs.**
Physiological → Safety → Social/Belongingness → Esteem → Self-actualisation.

**Q11. Differentiate Herzberg's hygiene factors and motivators.**
Hygiene (extrinsic — pay, conditions): absence causes dissatisfaction; presence does not motivate. Motivators (intrinsic — achievement, recognition, growth): drive satisfaction & motivation.

**Q12. Write Vroom's expectancy theory equation.**
Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence. If any term is zero, motivation is zero.

**Q13. State the SMART goals framework.**
Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. Clear, difficult goals with feedback drive higher performance.

**Q14. List the five core dimensions of the Job Characteristics Model.**
Skill variety, Task identity, Task significance, Autonomy, Feedback. Combine into Motivating Potential Score (MPS).

---

## Set D — Groups & teams

**Q15. State Tuckman's stages of group development.**
Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning.

**Q16. Define groupthink. State three symptoms.**
Cohesion-driven suppression of dissent leading to poor decisions. Symptoms: illusion of invulnerability, peer pressure, self-censorship, illusion of unanimity, rationalisation.

**Q17. Differentiate group and team.**
Group: information-sharing, individual accountability, neutral synergy, varied skills. Team: collective performance goal, mutual accountability, positive synergy, complementary skills.

**Q18. Name three group-decision-making techniques.**
Brainstorming, Nominal group technique, Delphi, electronic meetings.

---

## Set E — Communication

**Q19. Draw the communication process model.**
Sender → Encoding → Channel → Decoding → Receiver → Feedback. Noise can interfere at any stage.

**Q20. State three barriers to effective communication.**
Filtering, selective perception, information overload, language differences, emotions, gender style differences.

**Q21. Differentiate high-context and low-context cultures.**
High-context (Japan, India): meaning embedded in context, relationships, non-verbal cues. Low-context (US, Germany): meaning explicit in words.

---

## Set F — Leadership

**Q22. State the four 'I's of transformational leadership.**
Idealised influence, Inspirational motivation, Intellectual stimulation, Individualised consideration.

**Q23. Describe Hersey-Blanchard's situational leadership styles.**
Telling (R1), Selling (R2), Participating (R3), Delegating (R4) — match style to follower readiness.

**Q24. List French & Raven's 5 bases of power.**
Legitimate, Reward, Coercive, Expert, Referent. Personal power (expert + referent) is more enduring than positional.

**Q25. Differentiate transactional and transformational leadership.**
Transactional: contingent reward, management by exception — works in stable settings. Transformational: vision-led, inspires followers beyond self-interest — better in dynamic/change settings.

---

## Set G — Conflict, structure, culture

**Q26. List the five conflict-handling styles (Thomas-Kilmann).**
Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Avoiding, Accommodating — mapped on assertiveness vs cooperativeness.

**Q27. What is BATNA in negotiation?**
Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement. Defines your walk-away point and bargaining strength.

**Q28. State the six elements of organisation structure.**
Work specialisation, departmentalisation, chain of command, span of control, centralisation, formalisation.

**Q29. Differentiate mechanistic and organic structures.**
Mechanistic: high specialisation, rigid hierarchy, many rules — for stable environments. Organic: flexible, decentralised, few rules — for dynamic environments.

**Q30. State Schein's three levels of culture.**
Artefacts (visible), Espoused values (stated), Basic underlying assumptions (deepest, taken for granted).

**Q31. List Hofstede's cultural dimensions.**
Power distance, Individualism-Collectivism, Masculinity-Femininity, Uncertainty Avoidance, Long-term Orientation, Indulgence-Restraint.

---

## Set H — Change and HRM

**Q32. State Lewin's three-step change model.**
Unfreeze → Change → Refreeze.

**Q33. List Kotter's first three steps of leading change.**
Create urgency, form a powerful coalition, create a vision (then communicate it).

**Q34. Differentiate job description and job specification.**
Job description: what the job involves (duties, responsibilities). Job specification: what the role requires from the person (skills, qualifications, traits).

**Q35. State Kirkpatrick's four levels of training evaluation.**
Reaction → Learning → Behaviour → Results.
