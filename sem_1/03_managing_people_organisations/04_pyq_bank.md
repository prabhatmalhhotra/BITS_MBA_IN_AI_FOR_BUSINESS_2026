## Mid-Semester papers

### Mid-Sem Paper A — Closed book, 2 hours, 60 marks

**Section A — Short answer (5 × 4 = 20)**
1. Define organisational behaviour. State its three levels.
2. Differentiate Theory X and Theory Y.
3. Explain the Big Five personality model.
4. State the difference between values and attitudes.
5. List Tuckman's stages of group development.

**Section B — Medium answer (3 × 8 = 24)**
6. Compare Maslow's, Herzberg's and McClelland's motivation theories. Which is most useful for managing knowledge workers?
7. Explain perception process and three perceptual biases that affect managerial decisions.
8. Distinguish functional and dysfunctional conflict. Explain Thomas-Kilmann styles with one example each.

**Section C — Case (1 × 16 = 16)**
9. **Case** (paragraph describing a software firm where a high-performing senior engineer is increasingly disengaged after returning from parental leave; team-lead micromanages remotely; performance is dropping):
   (a) Diagnose using OB frameworks (motivation, JCM, perception, communication).
   (b) Recommend a 30-day action plan for the team-lead.
   (c) What organisational practices would prevent recurrence?

---

### Mid-Sem Paper B — 50 marks

1. (5 m) Define attribution theory. State three factors that influence attribution.
2. (5 m) State Vroom's expectancy theory equation. Explain each term.
3. (8 m) Discuss Hackman & Oldham's JCM. Compute MPS for a job with SV=4, TI=5, TS=6, A=7, F=6.
4. (8 m) Compare classical and operant conditioning. Give a workplace example for each.
5. (10 m) Distinguish virtual teams from face-to-face teams. What four practices help virtual teams succeed?
6. (14 m) **Case** (paragraph: marketing team of a consumer-goods firm, 8 members, formed 3 months ago, struggling with conflict between research-driven member and creative-led manager). Apply Tuckman, Belbin team roles, and conflict frameworks.

## Comprehensive papers

### Comprehensive Paper A — 2 hours, 100 marks

**Section A — Short answer (5 × 4 = 20)**
1. State French & Raven's five bases of power.
2. List Hofstede's cultural dimensions.
3. Differentiate transactional and transformational leadership.
4. State Lewin's three-step change model.
5. Define employee engagement. Name two drivers.

**Section B — Medium answer (4 × 10 = 40)**
6. Compare functional, divisional, and matrix structures. When is each appropriate?
7. Discuss Schein's three levels of culture. How can leaders shape culture?
8. Distinguish leader, manager, and entrepreneur. Are they mutually exclusive?
9. Explain Kotter's 8-step change model. Apply any 4 steps to a real-world Indian company example.

**Section C — Long case (1 × 25 = 25)**
10. **Case (~600 words)**: A 40-year-old auto manufacturer in Pune is launching an AI-based predictive maintenance system. The plant has 1,200 workers, strong unions, average age 48. Senior leadership has approved investment but middle managers fear loss of authority and shop-floor staff fear job displacement. The CEO has asked HR head to design a 12-month change strategy.
    (a) Diagnose using resistance-to-change frameworks (10 m)
    (b) Apply Kotter's 8-step model (8 m)
    (c) Recommend reskilling, communication and HR-policy interventions (7 m)

**Section D — Theory long answer (1 × 15 = 15)**
11. "Effective leadership in the age of AI requires more EQ than IQ." Discuss with reference to contemporary leadership theories.

---

### Comprehensive Paper B — 80 marks

1. (10 m) Discuss four contemporary leadership theories. Compare them on followers, context, and outcomes.
2. (10 m) Describe organisational culture. Apply Hofstede's dimensions to a multinational team with members from India, USA and Japan.
3. (10 m) Diagnose stress in a high-performing employee. Recommend three individual and three organisational interventions.
4. (10 m) Discuss recruitment & selection. What are the validity coefficients of structured interviews vs cognitive ability tests?
5. (15 m) **Mini-case** on team conflict — apply Tuckman, Belbin, and conflict-handling frameworks.
6. (10 m) Discuss diversity, equity and inclusion. What is the business case? What practical steps can a manager take?
7. (15 m) **Mini-case** on managing AI-driven change in a PSU — apply Kotter + ADKAR.

## Sample case-let prompts (frequently appear)

1. New CEO from outside takes over family-run firm. Resistance from old guard. Apply OB lens.
2. Two engineers from different sub-teams in conflict over architecture choice. Lead asked to mediate.
3. Founder-CEO of a 200-person start-up needs to formalise structure as the company scales.
4. Cross-border merger: cultures clash between Indian process-orientation and Israeli "challenge-everything" style.
5. WFH burnout in a tech firm: redesign job, communication norms, and rituals.

## Tips for writing case answers

1. Spend 5 min reading and underlining facts BEFORE you start writing.
2. State the framework you are applying explicitly ("Applying Tuckman's model …").
3. Diagnose first, recommend second. Examiners hate generic recommendations.
4. Quantify recommendations where possible ("90-day plan with 4 milestones …").
5. End with metrics for success ("engagement up 15 pts, attrition down 30% …").
