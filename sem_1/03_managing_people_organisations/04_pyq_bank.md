## Mid-Sem papers (closed book, Sessions 1-8)

### Mid-Sem Paper A — 2 hours, 70 marks (note: MPO mid-sem is 35 %)

**Section 1 — Short answer (5 × 4 = 20 marks)**

1. Define Organisational Behaviour and list any four disciplines that contribute to it.
2. State Mintzberg's three categories of managerial roles with one example each.
3. List the Big Five personality dimensions (OCEAN) and explain which best predicts job performance.
4. Differentiate emotion and mood with one example each.
5. State Maslow's Need Hierarchy and discuss one limitation.

**Section 2 — Application / short essay (3 × 8 = 24 marks)**

6. Discuss the strategic role of HRM in a globally competitive firm. Use Ulrich's HR roles model.
7. Analyse common decision-making biases (anchoring, confirmation, overconfidence, escalation) with one business example each. Suggest debiasing techniques.
8. Compare Theory X vs Theory Y (McGregor) and discuss its implications for management style and employee engagement in modern Indian organisations.

**Section 3 — Long answer (1 × 14 = 14 marks)**

9. "Job satisfaction is more than a feel-good metric — it has tangible business consequences." Discuss its determinants and relationship with OCB, productivity, absenteeism, turnover, and CWB. Recommend three managerial actions.

**Section 4 — Mini-case (1 × 12 = 12 marks)**

10. **Mini-case**: A high-performing software developer was overlooked for a leadership promotion despite meeting all technical criteria. The hiring panel cited "lack of people skills." (a) Apply Katz's skills framework to explain this outcome. (b) What kind of personality and EI traits should leaders develop? (c) Suggest a 6-month development plan.

### Mid-Sem Paper B — 2 hours, 60 marks

1. (5 m) Explain the cognitive dissonance theory with one workplace example.
2. (5 m) Distinguish between surface-level and deep-level diversity. Give one example each from your workplace.
3. (5 m) Explain the concept of Emotional Labour. Distinguish surface acting vs deep acting. Which is healthier for employees and why?
4. (8 m) Compare Maslow's, Herzberg's and McClelland's motivation theories. Which is most useful for designing a modern compensation & engagement system?
5. (8 m) Discuss the Job Characteristics Model (JCM). Apply it to redesign the role of a customer-service agent in a BPO. (Compute MPS conceptually before and after.)
6. (10 m) Discuss the Big Five personality dimensions and their relevance to workplace performance, leadership and team formation. Discuss ethical considerations in using personality assessments for selection.
7. (8 m) Define HRM. Discuss its evolution from Personnel Management to a strategic function. Explain Ulrich's HR roles model.
8. (11 m) **Case**: An Indian IT services firm faces 25 % attrition despite competitive pay. As HR head, diagnose the issue using motivation theories (Herzberg, Equity, Self-Determination) and propose three interventions beyond pay.

### Mid-Sem Paper C — Practice paper aligned to S2-25

1. (4 m) State Fayol's 5 functions of management and Mintzberg's 10 managerial roles.
2. (5 m) Explain the three components of an attitude (cognitive, affective, behavioural). Give one workplace example.
3. (8 m) Discuss the Fundamental Attribution Error and Self-Serving Bias. Give one example each from a performance review context.
4. (8 m) Goleman's EI model — discuss the four components and their role in leadership effectiveness.
5. (10 m) Apply Equity Theory to analyse why a salary cut for a high performer (relative to a peer's promotion) damaged morale across a team. Recommend HR actions.
6. (10 m) Discuss four contemporary challenges in OB: workforce diversity, gig economy, remote/hybrid work, AI augmentation.
7. (15 m) **Mini-case**: A sales team is missing targets repeatedly. Diagnose using Vroom's Expectancy Theory (E×I×V). Propose 3 specific interventions to restore motivation.

## Comprehensive papers (open book, Sessions 1-16)

### Comprehensive Paper A — 2.5 hours, 100 marks

**Section A — Short answer (5 × 4 = 20 marks)**
1. State Tuckman's stages of group development.
2. Distinguish leadership and management with two examples each.
3. List the 6 elements of organisation structure.
4. Define BARS in performance appraisal.
5. State four types of teams.

**Section B — Application (4 × 10 = 40 marks)**

6. Compare Trait, Behavioural, Contingency, and Transformational theories of leadership. Cite Indian business leaders for at least two of them.

7. Discuss organisational culture using Schein's 3 levels. Differentiate strong vs weak culture. Discuss culture–strategy alignment with one example.

8. Design an end-to-end recruitment & selection process for a Data Scientist role in an Indian bank. Discuss bias-mitigation tactics and use of structured interview.

9. Design a modern performance management system to replace a traditional bell-curve graphic-rating system. Justify each design choice (continuous feedback, OKRs, 360-degree, calibration, decoupling from comp).

**Section C — Case / Long answer (2 × 15 = 30 marks)**

10. **Case**: A traditional Indian PSU is undergoing digital transformation. Apply Lewin's and Kotter's change models. Diagnose resistance and propose interventions including HR levers (training, comp, leadership development).

11. Discuss compensation strategy for (a) a global IT services firm, (b) a tech startup, (c) a public-sector bank. Cover internal/external equity, pay-for-performance, broadbanding, and total rewards.

**Section D — Application (1 × 10 = 10 marks)**

12. Build a 2-year leadership development plan for a high-potential manager. Cover competencies (Big Five + EI), development methods (coaching, stretch assignments, 360 feedback), and milestones.

---

### Comprehensive Paper B — 2.5 hours, 80 marks

1. (10 m) Discuss group cohesiveness, groupthink, social loafing, and group polarisation. Suggest mitigation tactics.

2. (10 m) Compare the four types of teams (Problem-solving, Self-managed, Cross-functional, Virtual). Which works best for a global software product company and why?

3. (12 m) Discuss communication barriers (filtering, selective perception, information overload, emotions, language, silence). Propose three interventions to make communication effective in a virtual team.

4. (12 m) Compare functional, divisional, matrix, and network/boundaryless structures. Recommend the right structure for an e-commerce startup at three stages: 50 employees, 500 employees, 5000 employees.

5. (12 m) Discuss internal and external equity in compensation. Explain Ulrich's broadbanding concept. Apply both to designing compensation for an Indian technology firm with 5,000 engineers.

6. (12 m) Compare traditional and modern methods of performance appraisal. Discuss BARS, MBO, 360-degree, and OKRs with examples. Address common rater errors.

7. (12 m) **Mini-case**: A unicorn startup CEO realised that the rapid scaling has eroded the original culture. Apply OB frameworks (Schein's 3 levels, OCAI) to diagnose. Recommend a culture revival plan.

---

### Comprehensive Paper C — Practice paper aligned to S2-25

1. (8 m) Discuss the impact of organisational culture on employee engagement and innovation. Use Schein's framework.
2. (10 m) Compare Hersey-Blanchard situational leadership styles (Telling, Selling, Participating, Delegating). Apply each to a different employee scenario.
3. (10 m) Discuss compensation components (direct + indirect). Explain pay-for-performance and ESOPs. Propose a total rewards framework.
4. (12 m) Discuss the 360-degree feedback method. State its benefits and limitations. Suggest implementation guidelines.
5. (14 m) Diagnose a service company with low employee engagement and high attrition using motivation theories + JCM + EI. Propose a 6-month recovery plan.
6. (14 m) **Case**: ELC-style problem from your own organisation — diagnose an HR/OB problem using course concepts; propose company actions; document references.
7. (12 m) Discuss diversity and inclusion in organisations (HSTalks tie-in). Explain the business case. Propose three D&I interventions for a male-dominated engineering firm.

- Tab T1 (Robbins) by chapter (Ch 1, 2-8, 9-10, 12-16) and T2 (Aswathappa) by chapter (Ch 1-2, 6-7, 9, 12).

- For HSTalks-related questions, cite the specific HSTalk title.
