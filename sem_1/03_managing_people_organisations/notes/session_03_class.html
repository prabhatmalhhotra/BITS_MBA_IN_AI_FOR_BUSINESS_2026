💬 Class Example

A is a young, recently-hired male college graduate with a business degree, raised in a Punjabi-speaking neighbourhood in Bangalore. B is an older, long-tenured woman raised in rural Meerut who worked her way up from high-school graduate. At first they may differ in communication style based on education, ethnicity, region, and gender (surface). As they work together they may find they share a common way of thinking about work problems and a preference to collaborate (deep).

6 Attitudes

Attitudes are evaluative statements or judgments concerning objects, people, or events.

The Three Components of an Attitude (Exhibit 3-1)

ComponentMeaningExample (negative attitude toward supervisor)
CognitiveEvaluation / opinion"My supervisor gave a promotion to a coworker who deserved it less than me. My supervisor is unfair."
AffectiveFeeling / emotion"I dislike my supervisor!"
BehavioralAction / intention"I'm looking for other work; I've complained about my supervisor to anyone who would listen."

Cognition, affect, and behavior are closely related.

7 Attitudes Follow Behavior — Leon Festinger

🧠 Cognitive Dissonance

Cognitive Dissonance: any incompatibility between two or more attitudes or between behavior and attitudes. People are motivated to reduce this dissonance — sometimes by changing the attitude to match the behavior.

8 Job Attitudes

  • Job Satisfaction — positive feeling about one's job
  • Job Involvement — degree of psychological identification with the job
  • Organizational Commitment — identification with org & its goals
  • Perceived Organizational Support (POS) — belief that org values their contribution & cares
  • Employee Engagement — involvement, satisfaction, and enthusiasm for work

9 Job Satisfaction — Measurement

One of the primary job attitudes measured. A broad term involving a complex individual summation of a number of discrete job elements.

How to measure?

  • Single global rating — one question, one answer
  • Summation score — many questions averaged

Causes of Job Satisfaction

  • Job Conditions
  • Pay
  • Personality
  • Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

Outcomes of Job Satisfaction

  • Job Performance
  • Organizational Citizenship Behavior (OCB)
  • Customer Satisfaction

10 Employee Responses to Dissatisfaction

Four responses on two axes — Active/Passive and Constructive/Destructive:

⬆️ Active

  • Exit (destructive) — behavior directed toward leaving the organization
  • Voice (constructive) — active & constructive attempts to improve conditions

⬇️ Passive

  • Neglect (destructive) — allowing conditions to worsen
  • Loyalty (constructive) — passively waiting for conditions to improve

11 In-Class Exercise — Assess Your Job Satisfaction

Rate each on a 7-point scale (1 = Extremely dissatisfied … 4 = Neutral … 7 = Extremely satisfied):

  1. The amount of job security I have
  2. The amount of pay and fringe benefits I receive
  3. The amount of personal growth and development I get in doing my job
  4. The people I talk to and work with on my job
  5. The degree of respect and fair treatment I receive from my boss
  6. The feeling of worthwhile accomplishment I get from doing my job
  7. The chance to get to know other people while on the job
  8. The amount of support and guidance I receive from my supervisor
  9. The degree to which I am fairly paid for what I contribute to this organization
  10. The amount of independent thoughts and action I can exercise in my job
  11. How secure things look for me in future in this organization
  12. The chance to help other people while at work
  13. The amount of challenge in my job
  14. The overall quality of the supervision I received on my work
Adding the 14 ratings gives a summation score — illustrating the second measurement approach for job satisfaction.

📌 Session 3 — Key Takeaways

  • Diversity has surface-level (demographic) and deep-level (cognitive/social/experiential) layers — both matter for OB
  • Attitudes have 3 components: Cognitive, Affective, Behavioral
  • Cognitive Dissonance (Festinger) — incompatibility drives attitude/behavior change
  • Job attitudes: Satisfaction, Involvement, Commitment, POS, Engagement
  • Dissatisfaction → 4 responses: Exit, Voice, Loyalty, Neglect (active/passive × constructive/destructive)
