3 The Four Management Functions

FunctionDefinition
PlanningDefine goals, establish strategy, and develop plans to coordinate activities.
OrganizingDetermining the What, Where, Who, When and How of tasks to be accomplished.
LeadingMotivating employees, resolving conflicts, directing employees, and choosing effective communication channels.
ControllingMonitoring activities to ensure on-time completion meeting set objectives.

4 Mintzberg's Managerial Roles

Mintzberg classified managerial activities into 10 roles across three categories.

🤝 Interpersonal Roles

  • Figurehead — symbolic head; performs routine duties
  • Leader — motivates and directs employees
  • Liaison — maintains network of outside contacts

📡 Informational Roles

  • Monitor — receives wide variety of information
  • Disseminator — transmits information received from outsiders to org members
  • Spokesperson — transmits info to outsiders on org's plans/policies

⚖️ Decisional Roles

  • Entrepreneur — searches org for opportunities, initiates projects
  • Disturbance handler — corrective action when org faces disturbances
  • Resource allocator — makes/approves significant org decisions
  • Negotiator — represents org at major negotiations
Case discussions covered in class:
  • Brandi sharing info from a production meeting with her team → Disseminator
  • Connie launching a new product company → Entrepreneur

6 Luthans' Study on Managerial Activities

Fred Luthans identified four categories of activities managers engage in:

ActivityWhat it involves
Traditional ManagementDecision making, planning, and controlling
CommunicationExchanging routine information and processing paperwork
Human Resource ManagementMotivating, disciplining, managing conflict, staffing, and training
NetworkingSocializing, politicking, and interacting with others

Successful vs. Effective Managers

Manager TypeTraditionalCommunicationHRMNetworking
Average32%29%20%19%
Successful (promoted fast)13%28%11%48%
Effective (productive units, satisfied employees)19%44%26%11%
Key insight: Successful managers spend most time on Networking, while Effective managers spend most time on Communication. The two are not the same activity profile.

7 What is Organizational Behavior (OB)?

OB is a field of study that investigates the impact that individuals, groups, and structure have on behavior within organizations, for the purpose of applying such knowledge toward improving an organization's effectiveness.

Topics that OB Covers

  • Motivation
  • Leader behavior and power
  • Interpersonal communication
  • Group structure and processes
  • Conflict and negotiation
  • Work design

8 Few Absolutes in OB

"Two people often act very differently in the same situation, and the same person's behavior changes in different situations."

⇒ Therefore, OB concepts must reflect situational or contingency conditions — there are few universal "if X then Y" rules.

📌 Session 1 — Key Takeaways

  • Managers get things done through other people via Planning, Organizing, Leading, Controlling
  • Mintzberg grouped managerial work into Interpersonal, Informational, and Decisional roles
  • Katz's three skills: Technical, Human, Conceptual
  • Luthans showed successful managers ≠ effective managers — the time-allocation profiles differ
  • OB studies individuals, groups, and structure to improve organizational effectiveness — but offers few absolutes due to situational variation
