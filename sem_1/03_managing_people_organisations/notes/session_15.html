Compensation Management

How do you design a pay system that's fair, competitive, and legally compliant? This session covers total rewards, job evaluation, pay structures, and Indian compensation law.

Real-World Problem

A Bangalore startup offers 30 LPA to a fresher — while their senior engineer earns 25 LPA. Chaos ensues.

In 2024, a well-funded SaaS startup in Bangalore hired an IIT campus fresher at 30 LPA (including ESOPs). An existing 5-year engineer discovered this and felt deeply inequitable. She raised the issue — management had no structured pay bands, no job evaluation system, and no justification framework.

Without a proper compensation system, pay decisions become arbitrary and demotivating. Session 15 covers how organisations design fair, competitive, and legally compliant compensation structures.

Indian Salary Structure

CTC (Cost to Company)
Total cost incurred by employer
▼ minus employer PF/gratuity/insurance
Gross Salary
What you see on paper
▼ minus employee PF + TDS + Professional Tax
Net Salary (Take-Home)
What actually hits your bank account

2 Determinants of Compensation Intermediate

External Factors

  • Labour market supply/demand
  • Cost of living
  • Government regulation (min wages)
  • Union pressure
  • Industry norms

Internal Factors

  • Ability to pay (company profits)
  • Business strategy
  • Job evaluation results
  • Employee performance
  • Seniority/experience

3 Job Evaluation Methods Intermediate

CategoryMethodHow It Works
Non-AnalyticalRankingRank jobs from highest to lowest overall value
Job Classification (Grading)Pre-define grade levels, slot jobs into grades
AnalyticalPoint MethodAssign points to compensable factors (skill, effort, responsibility, conditions); total points determine pay
Factor ComparisonCompare jobs factor-by-factor against benchmark jobs

Hay Plan (most widely used globally): Evaluates jobs on Know-how, Problem-solving, and Accountability.

Pay Structure Design

  • Pay grades — group similar-valued jobs together
  • Pay ranges — min, midpoint, max for each grade
  • Broadbanding — fewer, wider bands for flexibility
  • Compa-ratio = Actual salary / Midpoint of range (1.0 = at market)

4 Statutory Compensation in India Advanced

Law/CodeKey Provision
Code on Wages 2019National floor wage; minimum wages; equal remuneration; payment of bonus
Payment of Bonus Act 19658.33% minimum, 20% maximum of salary
Payment of Gratuity Act 197215 days salary x years of service (for 5+ years)
EPF Act 195212% employer + 12% employee contribution
ESI ActMedical benefits if salary ≤ Rs 21,000/month
Trending Example (2024-25)

Pay transparency laws & Indian startups: Following global trends (EU Pay Transparency Directive 2023), some Indian startups like Razorpay and Cred are publishing pay bands. This addresses equity concerns but creates "compression" issues where long-tenured employees earn less than new hires at inflated market rates. The solution: regular pay equity audits and structured compa-ratio reviews.

Session 15 — Key Takeaways

  • Total Rewards: Direct (base + allowances + variable) + Indirect (benefits) + Non-financial
  • Indian structure: CTC → Gross → Net (take-home)
  • Determinants: External (market, law, unions) + Internal (strategy, job evaluation, performance)
  • Job evaluation: Non-analytical (ranking, classification) vs Analytical (point, factor comparison)
  • Hay Plan: Know-how, Problem-solving, Accountability
  • Pay structure: Grades, Ranges, Broadbanding; Compa-ratio = actual/midpoint
  • Indian statutes: Code on Wages, Bonus Act (8.33-20%), Gratuity (15 days x years), EPF (12+12%)
