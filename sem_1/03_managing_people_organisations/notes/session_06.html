Emotions and Moods

Emotions are not weaknesses at work — they are powerful drivers of behaviour. This session covers emotional labour, EI, and why managing feelings matters for performance.

Real-World Problem

A Tata Cliq call-centre agent handles 80 angry customers daily — and burns out in 6 months. Why?

Ravi works at a Tata Cliq customer support centre. His job requires him to be cheerful and apologetic even when customers scream at him. By month 4, he feels hollow. By month 6, he quits citing "exhaustion." His manager is confused: "The workload wasn't that heavy."

Ravi wasn't physically exhausted — he was emotionally exhausted from emotional labour. The gap between what he felt (anger, frustration) and what he had to display (empathy, calm) drained him. Session 6 explains why emotions at work matter more than we think.

1 Affect, Emotions & Moods Beginner

ConceptDurationIntensityCauseExample
AffectUmbrella termVariesVariesCovers both emotions and moods
EmotionBrief (seconds to minutes)IntenseSpecific trigger (event/person)Anger at a rude customer
MoodLonger (hours to days)MildOften diffuse, no clear causeFeeling "off" on a Monday

Basic emotions (Ekman): Anger, Fear, Sadness, Happiness, Disgust, Surprise — universal across cultures.

Sources of Emotions and Moods

  • Personality (stable baseline)
  • Time of day (positive mood peaks mid-morning) and day of week (happier on weekends)
  • Weather, stress, sleep quality, exercise
  • Age (older = fewer negative emotions), gender (small differences in expression)

2 Emotional Labour Intermediate

Emotional Labour = managing emotional expression as part of one's job role. Required in service jobs (call centres, airlines, hospitals, teaching).

Surface Acting

Hiding true feelings, displaying required emotion

"I'm smiling but dying inside."

Leads to: Emotional dissonance, burnout, job dissatisfaction

Deep Acting

Genuinely trying to feel the required emotion

"Let me try to understand why this customer is upset."

Healthier; less dissonance; better customer outcomes

Daily Life Example

When a relative gives you a terrible birthday gift and you say "Wow, I love it!" — that's surface acting. When you genuinely think "They put thought into this, that's sweet" — that's deep acting. The first drains you; the second doesn't.

3 Affective Events Theory (AET) Intermediate

AET (Weiss & Cropanzano) explains how workplace events trigger emotional reactions, which then influence attitudes and behaviour:

Work Environment
Job characteristics, demands, emotional labour requirements
▼
Work Events
Hassles (rude customer) or Uplifts (praise from boss)
▼
Emotional Reactions
Anger, joy, frustration, pride (filtered by personality & mood)
▼
Job Attitudes & Behaviour
Satisfaction, commitment, performance, OCB or CWB

4 Emotional Intelligence (Goleman) Intermediate

Emotional Intelligence (EI/EQ) is the ability to perceive, understand, manage, and use emotions effectively. Daniel Goleman's 4-domain model:

Self-Awareness
Knowing your emotions and their impact
Self-Management
Controlling emotions, adaptability, drive
Social Awareness
Empathy, reading others' emotions
Relationship Mgmt
Influence, conflict resolution, teamwork

Why EI matters: EI predicts job performance, leadership effectiveness, and conflict resolution ability. It's especially critical for managers and leaders (covered again in Session 11).

Emotion Regulation Strategies

StrategyHow It WorksEffectiveness
Cognitive ReappraisalReframe the situation ("This criticism helps me grow")Most effective; reduces negative emotion genuinely
SuppressionHide emotional expressionLeast effective; the emotion stays, body stress remains
Affect LabellingName the emotion ("I'm feeling anxious")Reduces intensity; "Name it to tame it"
MindfulnessObserve emotions without judgementLong-term benefits; reduces reactivity
Trending Example (2024-25)

Mental health at Indian workplaces post-EY tragedy: After the death of a young EY employee in 2024 (attributed to overwork stress), Indian companies like Deloitte, Wipro, and HCL rushed to strengthen EAPs (Employee Assistance Programmes) and "no-meeting Fridays." This is emotion management at the organisational level — recognising that unmanaged negative emotions have real consequences.

Exam Tip: EI (Goleman's 4 domains) and emotional labour (surface vs deep acting) are high-frequency exam topics. Be ready to apply AET to a workplace scenario — trace event to emotion to behaviour.

Session 6 — Key Takeaways

  • Affect = umbrella; Emotions = intense, brief, triggered; Moods = mild, longer, diffuse
  • Emotional Labour: Surface acting (fake it) vs Deep acting (feel it) — surface acting causes burnout
  • AET: Work events → emotions → attitudes & behaviour
  • EI (Goleman): Self-awareness, Self-management, Social awareness, Relationship management
  • Emotion regulation: Cognitive reappraisal is most effective; suppression is least
  • EI predicts leadership, job performance, and conflict resolution
