Real-World Problem

Flipkart's Big Billion Day needs 100,000 temporary workers in 2 weeks. How?

Every October, Flipkart scales from ~35,000 employees to over 100,000 including temporary warehouse workers, delivery partners, and customer support agents. They must hire, train, deploy, pay, and eventually off-board these workers — all in a matter of weeks.

  • Planning — How many people, what skills, which locations?
  • Recruitment — Where to find 65,000 extra people fast?
  • Training — How to onboard them in 2 days instead of 2 weeks?
  • Compensation — What to pay? How to handle labour law compliance?
  • Separation — What happens after the sale ends?
This is HRM in action — the systematic process of managing people from planning to separation. Every function we study today plays a role in making Big Billion Day possible.

1 What is HRM? Beginner

Human Resource Management (HRM) is the process of managing human resources (people) to achieve individual, organisational, and societal goals.

Nature of HRM

  • Pervasive — every manager does HR (not just the HR department)
  • Action-oriented — focuses on doing, not just policy-making
  • People-oriented — treats employees as the most valuable asset
  • Future-oriented — workforce planning for tomorrow, not just today
  • Continuous — not a one-time activity; ongoing throughout the employee lifecycle
Daily Life Example

Even your college fest committee does HRM: you plan how many volunteers you need, recruit by putting up notices, train them on their roles, motivate with free passes and certificates, and separate when the fest is over. Every organisation — from a chai stall to Tata Group — manages people.

3 HRM Functions Intermediate

HRM functions are divided into two categories:

Managerial Functions

  • Planning — forecasting HR needs
  • Organising — structuring the HR department
  • Directing — motivating, leading employees
  • Controlling — auditing HR outcomes

Same as Fayol's POLC from Session 1!

Operative Functions

  • Procurement — recruitment, selection, placement
  • Development — training, career growth
  • Compensation — pay, benefits, incentives
  • Integration — motivation, participation, grievance
  • Maintenance — welfare, safety, health
  • Separation — retirement, resignation, layoffs
Exam Tip: The 6 operative functions (Procurement, Development, Compensation, Integration, Maintenance, Separation) are a favourite for listing questions. Use the mnemonic PDCIMS.

5 Context of HRM — The Environment Intermediate

HRM doesn't operate in a vacuum. External and internal environments shape HR practices:

External Environment

  • Economic — recession affects hiring freezes
  • Political-Legal — labour codes, POSH Act
  • Technological — AI replacing manual jobs
  • Social-Cultural — diversity expectations
  • Demographic — ageing workforce, Gen Z entry

Internal Environment

  • Strategy — growth vs cost-cutting affects HR
  • Structure — flat vs hierarchical
  • Culture — innovation-friendly or bureaucratic
  • HR Systems — HRIS, policies, processes

Indian Labour Law Context

Other key laws: POSH Act 2013 (sexual harassment prevention), Maternity Benefit Amendment Act 2017 (26 weeks paid leave).

Trending Example (2024-25)

Gig worker protections under the Social Security Code 2020: For the first time, India's labour law framework includes gig and platform workers (Swiggy, Uber, Urban Company). The code proposes social security benefits for them, though implementation has been slow. This blurs the traditional employee vs contractor line — a major HRM challenge.

6 Strategic HRM Advanced

Strategic HRM means aligning HR practices with business strategy to create competitive advantage.

Two Types of Fit

Vertical Fit

HR practices aligned with business strategy

If strategy = innovation, then HR should hire creative people, allow experimentation, reward risk-taking

Horizontal Fit

HR practices are internally consistent with each other

If you want teamwork, don't have purely individual-based incentives

HR as Competitive Advantage — VRIO Test

CriterionQuestionHR Example
ValuableDoes it improve efficiency or effectiveness?Skilled workforce reduces errors, improves quality
RareDo few competitors have it?Unique training programme that builds rare capabilities
InimitableIs it costly to copy?Strong culture (like Tata's values) is hard to replicate
OrganisedIs the org structured to exploit it?HR systems, policies, and leadership support the talent

HR Scorecard — Measuring HR's Contribution

Key metrics: HR cost per employee, time-to-hire, attrition rate, engagement scores, training hours per employee, internal mobility rate.

Daily Life Example

Think of two restaurants with identical menus and location. One has friendly, well-trained waiters who remember your name; the other has indifferent staff who get your order wrong. The difference? HRM — hiring, training, motivating, and retaining the right people. That's HR as competitive advantage.

7 OB vs HRM — How They Connect

OB (Session 1)

  • Studies why people behave as they do
  • Rooted in behavioural sciences
  • Descriptive & explanatory
  • Focus: understanding behaviour

HRM (Session 2)

  • Designs systems to manage people
  • Rooted in management practice
  • Prescriptive & action-oriented
  • Focus: policies, processes, practices

Connection: OB provides the theory; HRM applies it. You need to understand motivation (OB) before you can design compensation systems (HRM). This course covers both.

Session 2 — Key Takeaways

  • HRM = managing people to achieve individual, organisational, and societal goals
  • Nature: Pervasive, Action-oriented, People-oriented, Future-oriented, Continuous

  • Evolution: Personnel Mgmt → HRM → Strategic HRM → People Analytics
  • Environment: External (EPTSD) + Internal (Strategy, Structure, Culture, Systems)
