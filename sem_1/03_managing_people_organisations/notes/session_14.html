Real-World Problem

TCS receives 4 million applications for 40,000 positions. How do you find the right 1%?

Every year, TCS runs one of the world's largest recruitment drives. With a 1:100 application-to-hire ratio, the challenge isn't attracting candidates — it's efficiently selecting the right ones without bias, while ensuring legal compliance across Indian labour laws.

Session 14 covers the science and practice of recruitment (attracting the right people) and selection (choosing the best fit) — the most consequential HR decisions an organisation makes.

1 Recruitment — Sources & Process Beginner

Internal Sources

  • Promotion, Transfer
  • Employee referrals
  • Internal Job Posting (IJP)
  • Re-hires

Pros: Faster, cheaper, known quantity. Cons: Limited talent pool, inbreeding.

External Sources

  • Campus recruitment
  • Job portals (Naukri, LinkedIn)
  • Consultants, Agencies
  • Walk-ins, Job fairs
  • Social media, GitHub

Pros: Fresh ideas, diversity. Cons: Expensive, longer onboarding.

Recruitment Metrics

MetricWhat It Measures
Yield ratio% of applicants who move to next stage
Cost-per-hireTotal recruitment cost / hires made
Time-to-hireDays from job posting to offer acceptance
Quality-of-hirePerformance rating of new hires after 1 year

Realistic Job Preview (RJP): Share both pros AND cons of the job upfront. Reduces early turnover because expectations match reality.

Selection Tests — Validity Comparison

MethodValidityNotes
GMA (Cognitive Ability)HighestBest single predictor of job performance across ALL roles
Work Sample TestsVery HighE.g., coding test for developers; presentation for consultants
Assessment CentresHighMultiple methods + multiple assessors; used for managerial selection
Structured InterviewHighSame questions, scoring rubric, behavioural/STAR format
Personality TestsModerateBig Five; Conscientiousness best for performance
Unstructured InterviewLowSubjective, prone to biases, yet most commonly used

Common Interview Errors

First impression, Halo/Horns, Contrast effect, Similar-to-me bias, Leniency/Strictness.

Trending Example (2024-25)

AI in recruitment at Indian companies: Companies like HCL and Wipro use AI for resume screening, video interview analysis (body language, speech patterns), and chatbot-based initial screening. While efficient, this raises concerns about algorithmic bias — Amazon scrapped an AI hiring tool that discriminated against women. The human element in selection remains critical.

Exam Tip: Know the hierarchy of validity: GMA > Work sample > Assessment centre > Structured interview > Personality > Unstructured interview. Also know reliability vs validity distinction.

Session 14 — Key Takeaways

  • Recruitment: Internal (promotion, referral, IJP) vs External (campus, portals, agencies)
  • Metrics: Yield ratio, Cost-per-hire, Time-to-hire, Quality-of-hire
  • RJP: Realistic job preview reduces early turnover
  • Selection process: 6 steps from screening to offer
  • GMA tests = highest validity predictor of job performance
  • Structured interviews (STAR) >> Unstructured interviews in reliability
  • Interview errors: First impression, halo, contrast, similar-to-me
  • Indian context: Article 16 (equal opportunity), PwD Act, POSH during selection
