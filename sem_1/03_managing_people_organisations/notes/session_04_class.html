2 What is Personality?

"The dynamic organization within the individual of those psychophysical systems that determine his unique adjustments to his environment." — Gordon Allport

Operationally: the sum of ways in which an individual reacts to and interacts with others.

Personality Determinants

  • Heredity
  • Personality Traits

3 The Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)

The most widely used personality instrument in the world. Participants are classified on four axes to determine one of 16 possible personality types (e.g., ENTJ).

AxisOne endOther end
EnergyExtroverted (E) — sociable, assertiveIntroverted (I) — quiet, shy
InformationSensing (S) — practical, orderlyIntuitive (N) — unconscious processes
DecisionsThinking (T) — reason and logicFeeling (F) — values and emotions
LifestyleJudging (J) — order & structurePerceiving (P) — flexible, spontaneous

Sample Types & Their Uses

  • Visionaries (INTJ) — original, stubborn, driven
  • Organizers (ESTJ) — realistic, logical, analytical, businesslike
  • Conceptualizers (ENTP) — entrepreneurial, innovative, individualistic, resourceful
Validity: Research results are mixed. MBTI is a good tool for self-awareness and counseling — but should NOT be used as a selection test for job candidates.

4 The Big Five Personality Model (OCEAN)

Research has shown this to be a better framework than MBTI.

TraitDescription
Openness to ExperienceCurious, imaginative, artistic, sensitive
ConscientiousnessResponsible, dependable, persistent, organized
ExtroversionSociable, gregarious, assertive
AgreeablenessGood-natured, cooperative, trusting
Neuroticism / Emotional Stability (reverse)Calm, self-confident, secure under stress (positive) vs nervous, depressed, insecure (negative)

How Big Five Traits Predict Behavior

  • Highly conscientious people develop more job knowledge, exert greater effort, and have better performance
  • Emotional stability → job satisfaction; lower stress
  • Extroverts tend to be happier in their jobs and have good social skills; enhanced leadership
  • Open people are more creative and can be good leaders; more adaptable to change
  • Agreeable people are good in social settings; better liked; lower deviant behavior

5 The Dark Triad

  1. Machiavellianism — the degree to which an individual is pragmatic, maintains emotional distance, and believes that ends can justify means.
  2. Narcissism — the tendency to be arrogant, have a grandiose sense of self-importance, require excessive admiration, and have a sense of entitlement.
  3. Psychopathy — the tendency for a lack of concern for others and a lack of guilt or remorse when their actions cause harm.

6 Values — Terminal and Instrumental

Values = basic convictions about what is right, good, or desirable. Values lay the foundation for understanding attitudes and motivation, and generally influence attitudes and behaviors.

🎯 Terminal Values

Desirable end-states of existence — what one wants to achieve in life (e.g., happiness, freedom, security).

🛠️ Instrumental Values

Preferred modes of behavior or means of achieving terminal values (e.g., honest, ambitious, helpful).

8 GLOBE — Global Leadership and Organizational Behavior Effectiveness

The GLOBE framework extends Hofstede with 9 dimensions:

  • Power Distance
  • Institutional Collectivism
  • Uncertainty Avoidance
  • Future Orientation
  • In-Group Collectivism
  • Gender Egalitarianism
  • Assertiveness
  • Humane Orientation
  • Performance Orientation
📖 Case Discussion — On the Costs of Being Nice

Agreeable people are kinder and more accommodating, which you might think adds to their success. Recent research, however, has shown some surprising downsides:

  1. Agreeable individuals are less adept at distributive bargaining — and therefore often negotiate lower salaries. They also tend to have lower credit scores.
  2. They may choose to work in "caring" industries (education, healthcare, public sector, non-profits) which earn lower salaries.
  3. Their earnings may also be reduced by lower drive to emerge as leaders and lower proactive task behavior.
  4. But agreeable individuals are better liked, help others more, and are happier at work and in life.

Discussion Question

Do you think employers must choose between agreeable employees and top performers?

Class solution: Not necessarily. In the natural state the agreeable employee may not be as productive as the disagreeable one, but with appropriate training and leadership, the agreeable employee can be motivated to greater success.

Discussion Question 2

Can you think of some job situations in which agreeableness is an important virtue?

Class solution: Relationship roles like sales or customer service suit agreeable people. Disagreeable people might fit jobs needing isolation, such as assembly work or accounting.

📌 Session 4 — Key Takeaways

  • Personality = consistent ways one reacts & interacts; shaped by heredity + traits
  • MBTI — 16 types on 4 axes; good for self-awareness, NOT for selection
  • Big Five (OCEAN) — research-supported; Conscientiousness predicts performance most strongly
  • Dark Triad = Machiavellianism + Narcissism + Psychopathy
  • Hofstede (5 dims) and GLOBE (9 dims) explain national-culture differences in OB; India is high power-distance
  • Agreeableness has trade-offs — kinder, happier, but often earn less in distributive contexts
