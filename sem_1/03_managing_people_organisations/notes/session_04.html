Personality and Values

Real-World Problem

A startup founder hires two brilliant engineers — one thrives, one quits in 3 months. Same role, same pay. What went wrong?

A Bangalore startup hired two IIT graduates for identical roles. Engineer A loved the chaos, took initiative, and pushed back on the founder's ideas. Engineer B wanted clear instructions, predictable schedules, and hated ambiguity. Within 3 months, B quit saying "this isn't for me."

The difference wasn't skill — it was personality. Engineer A scored high on Openness and low on Neuroticism (Big Five). Engineer B was the opposite. Understanding personality helps predict person-job fit and reduce costly mis-hires.

1 What is Personality? Beginner

Personality is the sum total of ways an individual reacts to and interacts with others. It's the consistent pattern of thoughts, feelings, and behaviours that makes you you.

What Determines Personality?

2 The Big Five (OCEAN) Intermediate

The most scientifically validated personality framework. Mnemonic: OCEAN

TraitHigh Score Means...Low Score Means...Job Relevance
OpennessCreative, curious, adventurousPractical, conventional, routine-lovingInnovation roles, R&D, design
ConscientiousnessOrganised, dependable, disciplinedCareless, impulsive, flexibleBest predictor of job performance across ALL roles
ExtraversionOutgoing, energetic, talkativeReserved, quiet, independentSales, leadership, customer-facing roles
AgreeablenessCooperative, trusting, helpfulCompetitive, sceptical, challengingTeamwork, customer service, nursing
NeuroticismAnxious, moody, emotionally reactiveCalm, stable, resilientLow N = better stress management
Exam Tip: Conscientiousness is the #1 predictor of job performance across roles — this is one of the most tested facts in OB. Extraversion predicts leadership emergence. Know these connections.
Daily Life Example

Think of your friend group: The one who always plans trips in advance = high Conscientiousness. The one who talks to strangers at parties = high Extraversion. The one who watches foreign films and reads philosophy = high Openness. The one who avoids arguments = high Agreeableness. The one who panics before every exam = high Neuroticism.

3 MBTI & Other Frameworks Intermediate

MBTI (Myers-Briggs Type Indicator)

16 personality types from 4 dichotomies:

E / I
Extraversion vs Introversion
S / N
Sensing vs Intuition
T / F
Thinking vs Feeling
J / P
Judging vs Perceiving

Popular but limited validity for job prediction. Good for self-awareness and team discussions; poor for hiring decisions.

Dark Triad

PsychopathyLack of empathy, impulsive, antisocialRisk-taking; can be charming superficially

4 Person-Job & Person-Organisation Fit Intermediate

Holland's RIASEC Model (Person-Job Fit)

John Holland proposed that job satisfaction and turnover depend on matching personality to occupation:

TypePersonalityBest-Fit Jobs
RealisticPractical, hands-onEngineer, mechanic, farmer
InvestigativeAnalytical, intellectualScientist, researcher, doctor
ArtisticCreative, expressiveWriter, designer, musician
SocialHelping, teachingTeacher, counsellor, nurse
EnterprisingAmbitious, competitiveManager, lawyer, salesperson
ConventionalOrganised, detail-orientedAccountant, analyst, admin

Person-Organisation Fit: Beyond the job, does the person's values match the organisation's culture? A creative rebel won't thrive in a rigid bureaucracy, even if the technical job fits.

5 Values Intermediate

Values are basic convictions about what is important — they guide attitudes and behaviour.

Terminal Values (Rokeach)

Desired end-states of existence

  • Happiness, Freedom, Self-respect
  • Family security, Wisdom
  • A comfortable life, Recognition

Instrumental Values (Rokeach)

Preferred modes of behaviour

  • Honesty, Ambition, Courage
  • Responsibility, Independence
  • Helpfulness, Self-control

Trending Example (2024-25)

Gen Z values vs Boomer values: Gen Z employees in Indian IT firms prioritise work-life balance, mental health, and purpose over salary and job security. This generational values gap is causing friction — senior managers expect long hours as "dedication" while Gen Z sees it as "toxic culture." Understanding Hofstede's Indulgence dimension helps explain this shift.

Session 4 — Key Takeaways

  • Personality = heredity + environment + situation
  • Big Five (OCEAN): Openness, Conscientiousness (best job predictor), Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism
  • MBTI: Popular but low validity; 16 types from 4 dichotomies
  • Dark Triad: Machiavellianism, Narcissism, Psychopathy
  • CSE: Self-esteem + self-efficacy + locus of control + emotional stability
  • Holland's RIASEC: Match personality to job type for satisfaction
  • Person-Org Fit: Values alignment with culture matters as much as skills
  • Hofstede's 6 dimensions: Power Distance, Individualism, Masculinity, Uncertainty Avoidance, Long-term Orientation, Indulgence
