Real-World Problem

Wipro's annual appraisal cycle results in 60% of employees feeling "unfairly rated." How to fix this?

After the annual performance review at a large Indian IT firm, internal surveys revealed that 6 out of 10 employees felt their rating didn't reflect their actual contribution. Managers admitted they were influenced by recency bias (remembering only the last quarter) and central tendency (rating everyone "average" to avoid conflict).

Performance appraisal is one of the most dreaded HR processes — yet it drives promotions, bonuses, and development. Session 16 covers methods from traditional rating scales to modern OKRs, common errors, and how to make appraisals actually useful.

1 Performance Management vs Appraisal Beginner

Performance Appraisal (PA)

  • Annual/biannual event
  • Retrospective (looking backward)
  • Rating and ranking
  • Administrative focus

Performance Management (PM)

  • Continuous process
  • Forward-looking (development)
  • Coaching and feedback
  • Developmental focus

PM cycle: Planning (set goals) → Coaching (ongoing) → Reviewing (assess) → Rewarding (outcomes)

Objectives of PA

  • Administrative: Pay decisions, promotions, transfers, training needs
  • Developmental: Feedback, career planning, coaching
  • Documentation: Legal defensibility for termination decisions

Daily Life Example

Think of rating professors on RateMyProfessor: You tend to rate based on the last class (recency), give higher ratings if the prof is friendly regardless of teaching quality (halo), and compare them to the previous prof you rated (contrast). These are the same errors that plague workplace appraisals.

Examples: Adobe's "Check-in" (no ratings, continuous feedback), Deloitte's 4 questions approach.

Indian Context

  • Most Indian companies follow April-March appraisal cycle (fiscal year)
  • Variable pay (15-25% of CTC) is performance-linked
  • Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946 — clear conduct/performance rules
  • Documentation is crucial for legally defensible terminations
Trending Example (2024-25)

AI-powered performance analytics: Companies like Infosys and HCL use AI to analyse employee performance patterns, predict attrition risk, and suggest development plans. "Pulse surveys" (weekly 2-3 question check-ins) replace annual engagement surveys. The trend is from "event-based appraisal" to "always-on performance intelligence" — but employees worry about surveillance disguised as development.

Exam Tip: The 6 appraisal errors (halo, leniency, central tendency, recency, similar-to-me, stereotyping) are very commonly tested. Be ready to identify which error is at play in a given scenario. Also know the difference between PA and PM — trick question territory.
