Work Teams & Communication

What makes a team more than just a group of people? How does communication flow — and fail — in organisations? This session covers team effectiveness and communication channels.

Real-World Problem

Google's Project Aristotle discovers that WHO is on a team matters less than HOW they interact

Google spent 2 years studying 180+ teams to find the secret of high-performing teams. The surprise: it wasn't about hiring the smartest people. The #1 factor was psychological safety — team members feeling safe to take risks and be vulnerable.

Session 10 covers what makes teams effective and how communication (the bloodstream of any organisation) enables or destroys teamwork.

1 Groups vs Teams Beginner

Work Group

  • Individual accountability
  • Sum of individual contributions
  • Share info, but no synergy
  • No collective work product

Work Team

  • Individual AND mutual accountability
  • Synergistic (1+1=3)
  • Complementary skills
  • Collective work product

Cross-Cultural Communication

High-Context (India, Japan)

Meaning from context, relationships, non-verbal cues. Indirect communication.

Low-Context (USA, Germany)

Meaning from explicit words. Direct, precise communication.

Trending Example (2024-25)

Slack/Teams overload in Indian IT: The average knowledge worker receives 200+ messages per day across Slack, Teams, and email. This information overload causes "notification fatigue" and reduces deep work. Companies like Zerodha introduced "async-first" communication policies — communicate via documents, not meetings.

Session 10 — Key Takeaways

  • Teams vs Groups: Teams have synergy, mutual accountability, collective work product
  • 5 team types: Problem-solving, Self-managed, Cross-functional, Virtual, Multi-team
  • Team effectiveness: Context + Composition + Work Design + Process
  • Belbin's 9 roles: 3 Thinking + 3 Action + 3 People roles
  • Google Project Aristotle: Psychological safety is the #1 factor for team success
  • Communication process: Sender → Encoding → Channel → Decoding → Receiver + Feedback + Noise
  • Channel richness: Face-to-face (richest) to memo (leanest); match richness to message complexity
  • Barriers: Filtering, selective perception, overload, emotions, language, silence
  • Cross-cultural: High-context (India) vs Low-context (US/Germany)
