## Session 1 — Introduction to OB
**Source:** T1 Ch. 1. **Mandatory video:** HST1 *Positive organizational behavior*.

**What is OB?** A field of study that investigates the impact of individuals, groups and structure on behaviour within organisations, with the aim of improving organisational effectiveness.

**Why it matters for managers**
- Most performance issues are people issues, not technical ones.
- "Soft" skills (interpersonal) are the differentiator at mid-senior levels.

**Disciplines that contribute to OB**
- Psychology (individual behaviour)
- Social Psychology (group dynamics)
- Sociology (org systems, culture)
- Anthropology (cross-cultural)
- Political Science (power, conflict)

**Managerial functions** (Henri Fayol)
- Planning, Organising, Leading (Directing), Controlling.

**Mintzberg's managerial roles** (10 roles, 3 categories)
- **Interpersonal**: Figurehead, Leader, Liaison.
- **Informational**: Monitor, Disseminator, Spokesperson.
- **Decisional**: Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator.

**Katz's managerial skills**
- **Technical**, **Human (Interpersonal)**, **Conceptual**. As you rise, conceptual > technical.

**Challenges & opportunities**
- Workforce diversity, globalisation, gig economy, remote/hybrid, AI augmentation, employee well-being, ethics.

**Three levels of OB analysis**
1. Individual (personality, motivation, perception)
2. Group (teams, leadership, communication)
3. Organisation (structure, culture, change)

## Session 2 — Introduction to HRM
**Source:** T2 (Aswathappa & Dash) Ch. 1, 2 (alt: R2 Dessler & Varkkey Ch. 1-2). **Mandatory video:** HST2 *Managing strategy and HRM*.

**HRM definition** — managing human resources to achieve individual, organisational and societal goals.

**Nature of HRM**
- Pervasive (every manager does HR).
- Action-oriented.
- People-oriented.
- Future-oriented (planning).
- Continuous.

**Scope**
- HR Planning, Job analysis & design, Recruitment & Selection, Training & Development, Performance Appraisal, Compensation, Employee Welfare, Industrial Relations, Health & Safety.

**HRM functions**
- **Managerial**: Planning, Organising, Directing, Controlling.
- **Operative**: Procurement, Development, Compensation, Integration, Maintenance, Separation.

**Evolution of HR** — Personnel Management → HRM → Strategic HRM → Human Capital Management → People Analytics.

**Context of HRM (the environment)**
- **External**: Economic, Political-Legal, Technological, Social-Cultural, Demographic.
- **Internal**: Strategy, Structure, Culture, HR Systems.
- **Indian context**: Labour Codes (Wages 2019, Industrial Relations 2020, Social Security 2020, OSH 2020), POSH Act 2013, Maternity Benefit Amendment Act 2017.

**Strategic HRM**
- Vertical fit: HR practices aligned with business strategy.
- Horizontal fit: HR practices internally consistent.
- HR as competitive advantage (VRIO test).

**HR scorecard** — measure HR contribution: HR cost per employee, time-to-hire, attrition, engagement, training hours, internal mobility.

## Session 3 — Attitudes, Job Satisfaction, Diversity
**Source:** T1 Ch. 2 & 3. **Mandatory video:** HST3 *Diversity and inclusion in organizations*.

**Attitudes** — evaluative statements about objects/people/events.
- Three components: **Cognitive** (belief), **Affective** (feeling), **Behavioural** (intention).
- **Cognitive Dissonance** (Festinger) — discomfort from inconsistent attitudes/behaviours; people seek consistency.

**Major job attitudes**
- **Job Satisfaction** — positive feeling about one's job.
- **Job Involvement** — psychological identification with job.
- **Organisational Commitment** — Meyer-Allen's three components: Affective, Continuance, Normative.
- **Perceived Organisational Support (POS)** — belief that org values their contribution.
- **Employee Engagement** (Gallup Q12) — involvement, satisfaction, enthusiasm.

**Job satisfaction — measurement**
- Single global rating; Job Descriptive Index (JDI).
- **Drivers**: nature of work, pay, supervision, promotion, co-workers, working conditions.

**Outcomes of job satisfaction**
- ↑ Productivity, ↑ Customer satisfaction, ↑ OCB (Organisational Citizenship Behaviour), ↓ Absenteeism, ↓ Turnover.
- **Exit-Voice-Loyalty-Neglect (EVLN)** framework — responses to dissatisfaction.

**Diversity in Organisations**
- **Surface-level**: age, gender, race, ethnicity, disability.
- **Deep-level**: personality, values, cognition.
- **Why diversity matters**: better decisions, broader market reach, innovation, talent pool.
- **Bias types**: stereotyping, in-group favouritism, halo, contrast.
- **DEI** (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion): Diversity = invited to the party; Inclusion = asked to dance; Equity = playlist matches your taste.
- **Indian context**: Reservation policies, gender diversity targets (Companies Act § 149 — 1 woman director), POSH compliance.

## Session 4 — Personality and Values
**Source:** T1 Ch. 4.

**Personality** — sum total of ways an individual reacts to and interacts with others.

**Determinants** — Heredity, Environment, Situation.

**Personality frameworks**
- **MBTI (Myers-Briggs)** — 16 types from 4 dichotomies (E/I, S/N, T/F, J/P). Popular but limited validity.
- **Big Five (OCEAN)** — strongest predictor of job performance:
  - **O**penness to Experience
  - **C**onscientiousness (best predictor of job performance across roles)
  - **E**xtraversion
  - **A**greeableness
  - **N**euroticism (Emotional Stability is reverse)
- **Dark Triad**: Machiavellianism, Narcissism, Psychopathy.
- **HEXACO** — adds Honesty-Humility to Big Five.

**Other relevant traits**
- **Core Self-Evaluation (CSE)** — self-esteem, self-efficacy, locus of control, emotional stability.
- **Self-monitoring** — adjusting behaviour to situation.
- **Risk-taking propensity**.
- **Type A/B personality** — Type A: competitive, time-urgent; Type B: relaxed.
- **Proactive personality** — initiates change.

**Person-Job Fit (Holland)** — RIASEC: Realistic, Investigative, Artistic, Social, Enterprising, Conventional.
**Person-Organisation Fit** — values match.

**Values**
- **Terminal values** — desired end-states (happy life, freedom, recognition).
- **Instrumental values** — preferred behaviours (honesty, ambition, courage).
- **Hofstede's dimensions** (cross-cultural) — Power Distance, Individualism vs Collectivism, Masculinity vs Femininity, Uncertainty Avoidance, Long-term Orientation, Indulgence.
- **Schwartz's 10 universal values**.
- **GLOBE study** — leadership values across 62 countries.

**Generational values** — Baby Boomers, Gen X, Millennials, Gen Z.

## Session 5 — Perception & Decision Making
**Source:** T1 Ch. 5.

**Perception** — process of organising and interpreting sensory impressions to give meaning to environment.

**Factors influencing perception**
- **Perceiver**: attitudes, motives, interests, experience, expectations.
- **Target**: novelty, motion, sound, size, background, proximity.
- **Situation**: time, work setting, social setting.

**Attribution theory** (Kelley)
- Why people behave as they do — attribute to internal vs external causes based on:
  - **Distinctiveness** (different across situations?),
  - **Consensus** (do others behave similarly?),
  - **Consistency** (does the person always behave this way?).
- **Fundamental Attribution Error**: over-attribute to internal causes for others.
- **Self-Serving Bias**: claim credit for success, blame externals for failure.

**Common perceptual shortcuts**
- **Selective perception** — interpret based on interest/background.
- **Halo effect** — single trait colours overall judgement.
- **Contrast effect** — compared to recent contrast.
- **Stereotyping** — judging on group membership.

**Decision-making models**
- **Rational** model — clear problem, all alternatives, all info, optimal choice. Assumes perfect rationality.
- **Bounded rationality** (Simon) — satisfice rather than optimise; cognitive limits.
- **Intuitive** decision making — pattern recognition from experience.

**Common decision biases**
- Overconfidence, Anchoring, Confirmation, Availability, Escalation of Commitment, Hindsight bias, Randomness error.

**Group decision making**
- **Pros**: more info/diversity, increased acceptance.
- **Cons**: time-consuming, conformity pressure, dominance, ambiguous responsibility.
- **Groupthink** (Janis) — illusion of invulnerability, suppression of dissent.
- **Techniques**: Brainstorming, Nominal Group Technique, Delphi, Devil's Advocate.

**Ethics in decisions**
- Utilitarian, Rights, Justice, Care.

## Session 6 — Emotions and Moods
**Source:** T1 Ch. 6 (alt: R3 Goleman, *Emotional Intelligence*, Parts I-III). **Mandatory video:** HST9 *Emotional intelligence: your success as a leader depends on it*.

**Emotion vs Mood vs Affect**
- **Affect** = umbrella term.
- **Emotion** = intense, short-lived, directed at someone/something.
- **Mood** = less intense, longer, often diffuse.

**Basic emotions** — Anger, Fear, Sadness, Happiness, Disgust, Surprise.

**Sources of emotions/moods**
- Personality, Time of day/week, Weather, Stress, Sleep, Exercise, Age, Gender (small effects).

**Emotional Labour**
- Managing emotional expression as part of the job (call-centre agents, nurses, teachers).
- **Surface acting** vs **Deep acting**. Surface acting → emotional dissonance → burnout.
- Felt vs displayed emotions.

**Affective Events Theory (AET)** — workplace events trigger emotions, which influence attitudes and behaviour.

**Emotional Intelligence (EI/EQ)** — Daniel Goleman's 4 domains:
1. **Self-awareness**
2. **Self-management**
3. **Social awareness** (empathy)
4. **Relationship management**

EI predicts job performance, leadership effectiveness, conflict resolution skill.

**Emotion regulation strategies**
- **Cognitive reappraisal** (reframing) — most effective.
- **Suppression** — hides expression but doesn't reduce experience.
- Affect labelling, mindfulness, perspective-taking.

**OB applications**
- Selection (EI assessments).
- Decision making (mood affects information processing).
- Creativity (positive moods enhance).
- Leadership (emotional contagion).
- Customer service (emotional labour).
- Negotiation (anger can be strategic; happiness reduces concession).

## Session 7 — Motivation Concepts
**Source:** T1 Ch. 7.

**Definition** — the processes that account for an individual's intensity, direction and persistence of effort toward attaining a goal.

**Early theories**
- **Maslow's Hierarchy** — Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-actualisation. Lower needs must be (largely) satisfied before higher needs activate.
- **Alderfer's ERG** — Existence, Relatedness, Growth. Multiple needs active simultaneously; frustration-regression.
- **McGregor's Theory X/Y** — X: people dislike work, need control; Y: people enjoy work, are creative.
- **Herzberg's Two-Factor (Motivation-Hygiene)**:
  - **Hygiene factors** (extrinsic) — pay, supervision, policy, working conditions; presence prevents dissatisfaction but doesn't motivate.
  - **Motivators** (intrinsic) — achievement, recognition, work itself, responsibility, growth.
- **McClelland's Three Needs** — nAch, nPow, nAff.

**Contemporary theories**
- **Self-Determination Theory (SDT)** — autonomy + competence + relatedness drive intrinsic motivation. **Cognitive Evaluation Theory** subset: extrinsic rewards may reduce intrinsic motivation if perceived as controlling.
- **Goal-Setting Theory (Locke)** — specific, difficult, accepted goals + feedback → higher performance. **SMART** goals.
- **Self-Efficacy Theory (Bandura)** — belief in own capability. Boost via enactive mastery, vicarious modelling, verbal persuasion, arousal.
- **Reinforcement Theory (Skinner)** — behaviour = function of consequences. Positive reinforcement, negative reinforcement, punishment, extinction. Schedules: continuous / intermittent (fixed/variable interval/ratio).
- **Equity / Organisational Justice** (Adams) — compare own input/outcome ratio to referent. Restore equity by changing inputs, outputs, perception, referent, or leaving.
  - **Distributive** justice (outcomes), **Procedural** (process), **Interactional / Informational** (treatment).
- **Expectancy Theory (Vroom)** — Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence.
  - **E**: effort → performance.
  - **I**: performance → reward.
  - **V**: reward valued.
- **Job Characteristics Model (Hackman-Oldham)** — Skill variety + Task identity + Task significance → Meaningfulness; Autonomy → Responsibility; Feedback → Knowledge of results. **Motivating Potential Score (MPS)** = ((SV+TI+TS)/3) × Autonomy × Feedback.

**Integration** — most modern motivation programmes blend SDT, goal-setting, expectancy.

## Session 8 — Motivation Applications
**Source:** T1 Ch. 8.

**Job redesign**
- **Job rotation** — periodic shifting tasks across same level.
- **Job enlargement** — horizontal expansion (more tasks).
- **Job enrichment** — vertical expansion (more autonomy/responsibility).
- **Relational job design** — emphasise impact on beneficiaries.

**Alternative work arrangements**
- **Flextime** — flexible start/end with core hours.
- **Job sharing** — two people one job.
- **Telecommuting / Remote / Hybrid**.
- **Compressed workweek**.

**Employee involvement programmes**
- **Participative management** — joint decisions with subordinates.
- **Representative participation** — works councils, board representation.
- **Quality circles**.

**Variable pay programmes**
- **Piece-rate, Merit-based, Bonuses, Profit-sharing, Gain-sharing, Employee Stock Ownership (ESOP), Restricted Stock Units (RSU)**.

**Skill-based pay** — pay for skills/competencies acquired.

**Recognition programmes** — non-monetary, often more powerful per ₹.

**Flexible benefits (cafeteria plans)** — modular, core-plus, flexible spending accounts.

**Employee well-being**
- Wellness programmes: mental health, EAP, fitness, financial wellness.

**Indian context**
- ESOPs heavily used in startups; tax treatment (perquisite at vesting + capital gains at sale).
- Gratuity, PF, Gratuity & Pension reforms (Code on Social Security 2020).
- Variable pay = 15-25 % of CTC for managers in India typically.

## Session 9 — Foundations of Group Behaviour
**Source:** T1 Ch. 9.

**Group definition** — two or more individuals, interacting and interdependent, to achieve particular objectives.

**Types of groups** — Formal (Command, Task) vs Informal (Friendship, Interest).

**Why people join groups** — security, status, self-esteem, affiliation, power, goal achievement.

**Tuckman's 5-stage model**
1. **Forming** — orientation, uncertainty.
2. **Storming** — intra-group conflict.
3. **Norming** — close relationships, cohesiveness.
4. **Performing** — group fully functional.
5. **Adjourning** — disbandment (for temporary groups).

**Punctuated Equilibrium** — for time-constrained groups: phase 1 (inertia) → midpoint transition → phase 2 (action).

**Group properties**
- **Roles** — sets of expected behaviour. Role conflict, role ambiguity, role overload.
- **Norms** — acceptable standards of behaviour. Asch conformity experiments.
- **Status** — socially defined position. Status incongruence creates problems.
- **Size** — small (5-7) for problem-solving; large for fact-finding.
- **Cohesiveness** — mutual attraction; high cohesion + high norms = highest productivity.
- **Diversity** — surface vs deep level; **fault lines**.

**Group decision making**
- Strengths: more complete info, diverse views, increased acceptance.
- Weaknesses: time, conformity pressure, dominance, ambiguous responsibility, **groupthink** & **groupshift**.

**Techniques**: brainstorming, NGT, Delphi, electronic meeting.

## Session 10 — Work Teams and Communication
**Source:** T1 Ch. 10 & 12. **Mandatory videos:** HST4 *Building high-performing teams* + HST5 *What makes for a great team?*.

### Teams (vs Groups)
- Group = sum of contributions.
- Team = synergistic, complementary skills, shared accountability.

**Types of teams**
- **Problem-solving** — ad-hoc.
- **Self-managed work teams** — high autonomy.
- **Cross-functional** — across departments.
- **Virtual / Distributed** — geographically separated; communication tech-enabled.
- **Multi-team systems** — interdependent teams sharing super-ordinate goal.

**Team effectiveness model**
- **Context**: resources, leadership, climate of trust, performance evaluation.
- **Composition**: abilities, personality, allocating roles, diversity, size, member preferences.
- **Work design**: autonomy, skill variety, task identity, task significance.
- **Process**: common purpose, specific goals, team efficacy, identity, cohesion, mental models, conflict, social loafing.

**Belbin's 9 team roles**
- Plant, Resource Investigator, Coordinator, Shaper, Monitor-Evaluator, Teamworker, Implementer, Completer-Finisher, Specialist.

**High-performing teams (Google's Project Aristotle)** — psychological safety #1; dependability, structure, meaning, impact.

### Communication
**Functions** — Information, Motivation, Emotional expression, Control, Coordination.

**Process** — Sender → Encoding → Channel → Decoding → Receiver → Feedback. Noise everywhere.

**Direction** — Downward, Upward, Lateral.

**Modes**
- **Oral** (rich, instant feedback, distortion in chain).
- **Written** (verifiable, slow, no immediate feedback).
- **Non-verbal** (body language, paralanguage).
- **Electronic** (email, IM, video, intranet).
- **Social media** (broad reach, rapid, public).

**Channel richness** — Face-to-face > Video > Phone > IM > Email > Memo > Bulk mail.
- Use rich channels for non-routine, ambiguous messages.

**Barriers to effective communication**
- Filtering, Selective perception, Information overload, Emotions, Language, Silence, Communication apprehension, Lying.

**Cross-cultural communication**
- High-context (Asia: meaning from context, relationship) vs Low-context (US/Germany: meaning in words).
- Cultural barriers: semantics, connotations, tone differences, perception.

**Crisis communication / formal vs grapevine**
- Active management of grapevine reduces rumour damage.

## Session 11 — Leadership Theories & EI
**Source:** T1 Ch. 13 (alt: R3 Goleman for EI sections). **Mandatory videos:** HST6 *What is leadership?* + HST7 *Leadership vs management* + HST8 *An overview and history of leadership and leadership development* + HST9 *EI: your success as a leader*.

**Leadership vs Management**
- Manager: cope with complexity, plan-budget, organise-staff, control.
- Leader: cope with change, set direction, align people, motivate-inspire.

**Trait Theories** — leaders born with traits. Big Five Extraversion + Conscientiousness + Openness + EI predict leadership emergence/effectiveness.

**Behavioural Theories**
- **Ohio State** — Initiating Structure + Consideration.
- **Michigan** — Production-oriented vs Employee-oriented.
- **Blake-Mouton Managerial Grid** — Concern for People (1-9) × Concern for Production (1-9). Team management (9,9) is ideal.

**Contingency Theories**
- **Fiedler's model** — leader style (relationship vs task) × situational favourability (leader-member relations, task structure, position power). LPC scale.
- **Hersey-Blanchard Situational Leadership** — leadership style varies with **follower readiness**: Telling, Selling, Participating, Delegating.
- **Path-Goal (House)** — Directive, Supportive, Participative, Achievement-oriented; choice depends on subordinate & environment characteristics.
- **Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)** — in-group vs out-group; in-group enjoys higher trust, opportunities.

**Contemporary Theories**
- **Charismatic leadership** — vision, willingness to take personal risks, sensitivity to followers' needs, unconventional behaviour.
- **Transformational leadership** — Idealised influence, Inspirational motivation, Intellectual stimulation, Individualised consideration. Outperforms transactional in most metrics.
- **Transactional leadership** — Contingent reward, Management by Exception (active/passive). Effective baseline.
- **Servant leadership** (Greenleaf) — leaders serve followers' growth.
- **Authentic leadership** — self-awareness, relational transparency, balanced processing, internalised moral perspective.
- **Ethical leadership** — uses power morally; sets ethical standards.

**Emotional Intelligence in Leadership** (Goleman)
- Self-awareness, Self-management, Social awareness, Relationship management.
- **EI is the strongest single predictor** of leadership effectiveness in 90 % of competency models.

**Trust** — basis of leadership; built on integrity, benevolence, ability.

**Mentoring**
- Career function (sponsorship, exposure, coaching, protection).
- Psychosocial function (counselling, friendship, role modelling).

**Cross-cultural leadership** (GLOBE) — universally desired: charismatic/value-based, team-oriented, integrity. Universally undesired: malevolent, autocratic.

## Session 12 — Organisation Structure
**Source:** T1 Ch. 14 (alt: R1 Daft, *Theory and Design of Organizations*, Ch. 3-4 — primary alt for structure & design).

**Six key elements of structure**
1. **Work specialisation** (division of labour).
2. **Departmentalisation** (function, product, geography, process, customer, matrix).
3. **Chain of command** (unity of command, authority).
4. **Span of control** (narrow vs wide).
5. **Centralisation vs Decentralisation**.
6. **Formalisation** (rules, procedures).

**Common structural designs**
- **Simple structure** — flat, low formalisation, owner-manager. Startups.
- **Bureaucracy** — high specialisation, formalised, rules, narrow span. Large stable orgs.
- **Matrix** — dual reporting (function + product). Innovation but power conflicts.
- **Virtual / Network** — outsource non-core; small core. Highly flexible.
- **Boundaryless** — minimise vertical/horizontal/external boundaries.
- **Team structure** — horizontal, empowered teams.
- **Modular / Holacracy** — self-organising circles (Zappos).

**Organic vs Mechanistic** (Burns & Stalker)
- **Mechanistic**: high specialisation, rigid, hierarchical. Suited for stable environments, mass production.
- **Organic**: flexible, decentralised, adaptive. Suited for dynamic environments, innovation.

**Determinants of structure**
- **Strategy** — innovation → organic; cost minimisation → mechanistic; imitation → hybrid.
- **Size** — bigger orgs more mechanistic.
- **Technology** — Woodward (unit, mass, process production).
- **Environment** — uncertainty (capacity, volatility, complexity) → organic.

**Effects on employee behaviour**
- Specialisation: high productivity but possible boredom.
- Span of control: wider → more autonomy + less supervision.
- Centralisation: ↓ employee discretion.

## Session 13 — Organisational Culture & Change
**Source:** T1 Ch. 15 & 16 (alt: R1 Daft Ch. 9 for culture, Ch. 11 for change & innovation). **Mandatory video:** HST10 *Managing cultures*.

### Organisational Culture
**Definition** — shared meaning system that distinguishes one organisation from another.

**Seven primary characteristics** (Robbins)
- Innovation & risk-taking, Attention to detail, Outcome orientation, People orientation, Team orientation, Aggressiveness, Stability.

**Strong vs Weak culture** — strong = widely shared, intensely held → consistent behaviour, lower turnover.

**Culture's functions** — boundary-defining, identity, commitment, social-system stability, sense-making, control mechanism.

**Culture as a liability** — barrier to change, diversity, M&A integration.

**Creating & sustaining culture**
- Founders' philosophy → top management actions → socialisation (anticipatory, encounter, metamorphosis).

**How employees learn culture** — Stories, Rituals, Material symbols, Language.

**Types of culture**
- **Schein's 3 levels** — Artefacts (visible) → Espoused values → Underlying assumptions.
- **Cameron-Quinn Competing Values** — Clan, Adhocracy, Market, Hierarchy.

**Ethical / Positive / Spiritual culture** — emerging dimensions.

### Organisational Change
**Forces for change** — workforce, technology, economic shocks, competition, social trends, world politics.

**Resistance to change**
- Individual: habit, security, economic, fear of unknown, selective info processing.
- Organisational: structural inertia, limited focus of change, group inertia, threat to expertise/power, resource allocation.

**Overcoming resistance** — Education, Participation, Facilitation, Negotiation, Manipulation, Coercion (Kotter & Schlesinger).

**Change models**
- **Lewin's 3-step**: Unfreeze → Change → Refreeze.
- **Kotter's 8-step**: Urgency → Coalition → Vision → Communicate → Empower → Quick wins → Consolidate → Anchor.
- **ADKAR (Prosci)**: Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement.
- **Action research** — diagnostic, data-driven cyclical change.

**Organisation Development (OD) interventions**
- Sensitivity training, Survey feedback, Process consultation, Team building, Intergroup development, Appreciative inquiry.

**Creating a learning organisation** (Senge's 5 disciplines) — Personal mastery, Mental models, Shared vision, Team learning, Systems thinking.

**Stress at work**
- Sources: task, role, interpersonal, organisational structure/leadership.
- Symptoms: physiological, psychological, behavioural.
- Management: individual (time mgmt, exercise, social support), organisational (selection, goal setting, redesign, EAP).

## Session 14 — Recruitment & Selection
**Source:** T2 (Aswathappa) Ch. 6 & 7 (alt: R2 Dessler & Varkkey Ch. 5-6 — strong on Indian recruitment channels and modern selection science).

### Recruitment
**Definition** — process of attracting qualified applicants to apply.

**Sources**
- **Internal**: promotion, transfer, employee referrals, internal job posting (IJP), re-hires.
- **External**: campus, advertising, employment agencies, consultants, walk-ins, internet (LinkedIn, Naukri), executive search, professional associations.

**Indian channels**: Naukri.com, LinkedIn, employee referrals, campus, Job fairs, IIPM. Increasingly: GitHub for tech, Behance for design.

**Recruitment process**
1. Manpower planning → JD/JS.
2. Source identification.
3. Attract & screen applications.
4. Recruitment evaluation (yield ratio, cost-per-hire, time-to-hire, quality-of-hire).

**Recruitment philosophy**
- "Build vs Buy" — internal develops, external brings new ideas.

**Realistic Job Preview (RJP)** — share both pros and cons; reduces voluntary turnover.

### Selection
**Definition** — choosing the most suitable candidate.

**Process**
1. Application screening
2. Selection tests (ability, aptitude, personality, integrity, work-sample)
3. Selection interview (structured, behavioural, situational, panel, stress)
4. Reference & background check
5. Medical examination
6. Final selection & offer

**Selection tests — types**
- **Cognitive ability** — GMA tests; strongest predictor of job performance.
- **Personality tests** — Big Five; 16PF; OPQ.
- **Integrity tests** — predict counter-productive behaviour.
- **Work-sample** (e.g., coding test, presentation) — high validity.
- **Assessment Centres** — multiple methods, multiple assessors over 1-3 days for managerial selection.

**Interview types**
- **Structured (behavioural / STAR)** — most reliable.
- **Unstructured** — low reliability; subjective.
- **Panel**, **Group discussion**, **Stress interview**.

**Common interview errors** — first impression, halo, contrast, similar-to-me, leniency/strictness.

**Validity & reliability** — selection methods must be reliable (consistent) and valid (predictive of performance).

**Indian legal context** — equal opportunity (Constitution Article 16), reservation policies, Persons with Disabilities Act 2016, POSH Act 2013 (during selection process), Maternity Benefit Act.

## Session 15 — Compensation Management
**Source:** T2 (Aswathappa) Ch. 12 (alt: R2 Dessler & Varkkey Ch. 11-12 for pay structures and incentive systems).

**Definition** — all forms of financial returns and tangible benefits an employee receives as part of employment.

**Components of total rewards**
1. **Direct (cash)**:
   - Base salary (monthly basic)
   - Allowances (HRA, DA, LTA, conveyance, special)
   - Variable pay (bonus, commission, profit-share, ESOP)
2. **Indirect (benefits)**:
   - PF, Gratuity, Pension
   - Health insurance, group life
   - Leave, holidays
   - Perquisites (car, fuel, club, education)
3. **Non-financial**: career growth, recognition, work-life balance, flexible hours.

**Wage and salary terms**
- **CTC (Cost to Company)** — total cost incurred by employer.
- **Gross salary** = CTC − employer's PF/gratuity contribution − insurance.
- **Net salary** = Gross − employee PF − tax (TDS) − professional tax.

**Determinants of compensation**
- External: labour market, cost of living, government policies, union pressure, society.
- Internal: ability to pay, strategy, job evaluation, employee performance.

**Job evaluation methods**
- **Non-analytical**: Ranking, Job Classification (grade).
- **Analytical**: Point method (factors weighted), Factor comparison.
- **Hay Plan** — Know-how, Problem-solving, Accountability.

**Pay structure design**
- Pay grades, pay ranges (min-mid-max), broadbanding.
- Compa-ratio = actual salary / midpoint of range.

**Variable / incentive pay**
- Individual: piece-rate, commission, merit pay.
- Group: gain-sharing (Scanlon, Rucker), team bonus.
- Organisation-wide: profit-sharing, ESOP, RSU.

**Executive compensation**
- Base + bonus + LTI (long-term incentives — RSU/PSU/options).
- Say-on-pay, claw-back clauses.
- SEBI LODR Regs for listed firms (Schedule V).

**Statutory compensation in India**
- **Code on Wages 2019**: minimum wages, wage payment, equal remuneration, payment of bonus.
- **Payment of Bonus Act 1965**: 8.33 % min, 20 % max of salary.
- **Payment of Gratuity Act 1972**: 15 days × years of service.
- **EPF Act 1952**: 12 % employer + 12 % employee.
- **ESI Act**: medical benefits if salary ≤ ₹21,000/month.

**Trends** — pay-for-performance, broadbanding, total rewards approach, transparent pay, ESG-linked exec pay, pay equity audits.

## Session 16 — Performance Appraisal & Management
**Source:** T2 (Aswathappa) Ch. 9 (alt: R2 Dessler & Varkkey Ch. 9 for performance management systems).

**Performance Management vs Performance Appraisal**
- **PA** = annual event, retrospective.
- **PM** = continuous process: planning → coaching → reviewing → rewarding.

**Objectives of PA**
- Administrative: pay, promotion, transfer, training need.
- Developmental: feedback, career planning, coaching.
- Documentation: legal defensibility for terminations.

**Process**
1. Establish performance standards (linked to KRA/KPI/OKR).
2. Communicate standards to employees.
3. Measure actual performance.
4. Compare with standards.
5. Discuss with employee (appraisal interview).
6. Decision and corrective action.

**Methods of appraisal**

| Category | Methods |
|----------|---------|
| **Past-oriented** | Rating Scales, Checklist, Forced Choice, Critical Incident, BARS (Behaviourally Anchored Rating Scale), Field Review, Performance Tests |
| **Future-oriented** | MBO (Management by Objectives), 360-degree feedback, Assessment Centres, Psychological appraisal, OKRs |

**Modern frameworks**
- **OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)** — Google/Intel, quarterly stretch goals + measurable KRs.
- **Continuous performance management** — frequent check-ins replace annual review (e.g., Adobe's "Check-in", GE's "PD@GE").
- **9-box grid** — Performance × Potential matrix for talent decisions.
- **Forced Distribution / Stack Ranking** — controversial; abandoned by GE/Microsoft.

**Common errors in appraisal**
- **Halo / Horns**, **Leniency / Strictness**, **Central Tendency**, **Recency**, **Similar-to-me**, **Stereotyping**, **Spillover**, **First impression**.
- Mitigations: BARS, multiple raters (360°), training, calibration sessions.

**Appraisal interview** — sandwich (positive-negative-positive), problem-solving, mutual goal-setting.

**Linkage to other HR systems**
- Pay (merit increase, bonus).
- Promotion / succession.
- Training & Development.
- Termination decisions (legal documentation).

**Indian context**
- Compliance with Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act 1946 — clear performance/conduct rules.
- Performance-based variable pay common; appraisal cycles align with Apr-Mar fiscal year.
- POSH compliance — workplace safety in performance reviews.

**Trends** — analytics-driven (HRIS like Workday/SuccessFactors), real-time feedback (Pulse surveys), AI-driven nudges, focus on employee experience over rating.
