# Exam Pattern — BITS WILP MBA

Detailed reference on how the three evaluation components actually work, including question paper formats observed across batches.

---

## 1. The three evaluation components (per course)

| Code | Name | Weight | Mode | Duration |
|------|------|-------:|------|---------:|
| EC-1 | Continuous Assessment | 25–30 % | Online (Taxila LMS) | Rolling / window-based |
| EC-2 | Mid-Semester Test | 30 % | In-person at exam centre | 2 hours |
| EC-3 | Comprehensive Exam | 40–45 % | In-person at exam centre | 2 hours |

Per-course handout is binding; the table above represents the modal pattern across MBA WILP courses.

---

## 2. EC-1 — Continuous (online)

Typical sub-components:

- **2 to 3 quizzes**: 30–60 minutes each, MCQ / short-answer, on Taxila. Often a question pool is randomly served. Sometimes 2 attempts allowed (better score retained).
- **1–2 assignments**: descriptive / case / coding, submitted as PDF or Jupyter notebook. ~6–10 hours of effort each.
- **Lab assignments** (for ML / Data Science / AI Apps): hands-on Python/SQL deliverables.
- **Discussion-board participation**: rare but appears in Marketing / OB courses.

Tactics
- Attempt quizzes within first 48 hours of release. The window closes hard; no make-up for missed quizzes.
- Read the rubric in the assignment PDF before writing. Each criterion is graded.
- For coding submissions, push code to GitHub and include a README with setup steps. Helps the grader.

---

## 3. EC-2 — Mid-Semester Test

- **Duration**: 2 hours.
- **Coverage**: typically Modules 1 to ~6 (first ~50% of syllabus).
- **Mode**: in-person at chosen exam centre. Closed-book is the norm; some quantitative courses allow a one-page formula sheet.
- **Structure** (typical for MBA WILP descriptive papers):
  - 5–7 questions
  - Mix of: short answer (3–5 marks), long answer (8–12 marks), and 1 problem / mini-case (10–15 marks)
  - Total 50–60 marks
- **Calculator**: non-programmable allowed for Stats, Accounting, Finance, OM, Economics.

Common pitfalls
- Question paper labelled "**attempt all**" or "**attempt any 4 of 5**". Read the instruction page.
- Time per mark ≈ 2 minutes. Don't spend 30 minutes on a 5-mark question.
- Marks are awarded for **process and intermediate steps**. Show every formula and substitution.

---

## 4. EC-3 — Comprehensive Exam

- **Duration**: 2 hours (3 hours in some older batches).
- **Coverage**: entire syllabus, weighted toward post-mid content.
- **Mode**: in-person at exam centre. **Many MBA courses are open-book** — verify on handout.
- **Structure** (typical):
  - 6–10 questions
  - 2–3 long-form theory / case (10–15 marks)
  - 1 numerical or applied problem (10–15 marks)
  - Several short answers (3–5 marks)
  - Total 80–100 marks
- **Open-book rules** (when applicable):
  - You may carry the prescribed textbook(s) and your own handwritten/typed notes.
  - Photocopies of solutions or test banks are usually banned. Carry only legitimate material.
  - No internet, no laptop, no phone.

Open-book preparation
- An open-book exam is harder, not easier. Questions test application, not retrieval.
- Build a **20–40 page cheat-sheet** with: formula tables, framework one-pagers, worked examples categorised by problem type.
- Tab your textbook with sticky notes by chapter and problem type.

---

## 5. Make-up exams

- Available for documented work-related conflicts (employer letter required).
- Apply through the WILP examinations cell before the announced cutoff.
- Make-up papers tend to be slightly tougher / different. Plan to write the regular paper if at all possible.

## 6. Question-paper conventions observed across MBA WILP

### Numerical / quantitative courses (Stats, Accounting, Finance, Economics, OM)
- Always 1 large numerical (15+ marks) plus 2–3 medium numericals.
- 1–2 theory short-answers (definitions, comparisons, classifications).
- Show all formulas. Underline the final answer.

### Conceptual / case courses (MPO, Marketing, Strategic Mgmt)
- Always 1 mini-case (a paragraph followed by 2–3 sub-questions, total 15–20 marks).
- 3–4 framework-application questions ("Apply Porter's 5 Forces to …").
- 2–3 short-answer definition / model questions.

### Tech courses (AI Apps, Applied DS, ML/DL, NLP/GenAI)
- 1 short coding-style question (write pseudo-code or interpret given code).
- 1 numerical (compute confusion matrix, gradient descent step, regression coefficient).
- 2–3 conceptual long answers (explain transformers, explain CNN backprop, etc.).
- 1 application/case ("design an ML system for X").

## 7. Allowed materials at exam centre

- Hall ticket (printed)
- Government photo ID
- Blue / black ball-point pens (carry 3)
- Non-programmable scientific calculator (Casio FX-991ES / 991EX is the gold standard)
- Pencil + eraser + ruler (for graphs)
- Open-book material if course allows
- Water bottle (transparent)
- **Not allowed**: smart watches, mobile phones (deposited at entry), laptops, programmable calculators

---

## 8. Grading and CGPA mechanics

- **Relative grading** within the course-batch.
- Letter grades & points: A=10, A−=9, B=8, B−=7, C=6, C−=5, D=4, E=2.
- Course units (credits) per MBA course typically 4 or 5; check handout.
- CGPA = Σ(Units × Grade points) ÷ Σ(Units).
- **Minimum CGPA to graduate**: **5.50**, with no E grade and no RRA flag.
- Borderline policy: a 1–2 mark gap can flip you across grade boundaries — fight for every easy mark in EC-1.

---

## 9. Re-evaluation and grievance

- Answer scripts can usually be requested for review within a published window after results.
- File grievances through the LMS Grievance module with specific question references.
- Decisions are usually final after the review committee response.

## 10. Cheat-sheet of "do this NOW" actions

1. After registration, download every course handout from Taxila and save them in `sem_X/<course>/handout.pdf`.
2. Note the EC-1 calendar — block 6 hours per assignment in your work calendar.
3. Reserve exam-centre seat the day registration opens (Pune/Mumbai/Bangalore fill fast).
4. Apply for an updated photo ID matching the name on your hall ticket.
5. Buy a Casio FX-991EX calculator (₹1,200) and learn its statistics & financial functions.
