## 4. EC-2 — Mid-Semester Test

- **Duration**: 2 hours (all four Sem 1 courses).
- **Mode**: in-person, at the chosen exam centre.
- **Type**: **Closed Book** for all four courses.
- **Coverage** (per handout):
  - Business Statistics & Managerial Accounting & Marketing Management: Topics in **Contact Sessions 1 to 8** (= first 50 % of the 16-session plan).
  - Managing People & Organisations: Topics in **Lecture Sessions 1 to 8** (= first 50 %).
- **Closed-book rule** (verbatim from all 4 handouts): *"No books or reference material of any kind allowed during the exam."* This means no formula sheets, no photocopies, no printed notes — only what you've memorised.
- **Calculator**: A non-programmable scientific calculator is the standard for Business Statistics and Managerial Accounting (e.g., Casio FX-991EX or FX-991ES). MPO and Marketing typically do not require a calculator.

**Common pitfalls**
- Question paper instructions ("attempt all" vs "attempt any 4 of 5") are *not* fixed in the handout — read the cover page on the day.
- Time per mark ≈ 2 minutes for a 60-mark, 2-hour paper. Don't burn 30 minutes on a 5-mark question.
- For numerical subjects, marks are awarded for **process + intermediate steps**. Show every formula and every substitution.

## 5. EC-3 — Comprehensive Exam

- **Duration**: **2 hours for Business Statistics**, **2½ hours for the other three**.
- **Mode**: in-person, at the chosen exam centre.
- **Type**: **Open Book** for all four Sem 1 courses.
- **Coverage**: Entire syllabus (all 16 sessions) per all four handouts.
- **Open-book rule** (verbatim from all 4 handouts): *"Open Book means text/reference books prescribed in the course handout are allowed."* Each handout points to the centralised WILP guideline document — see [EC-3 open-book guidelines](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1DJvlhVzOaIw4njc9g30MlBuu0DqTzeIL/) (linked from `sem_1/SOURCES.md`).
- The handouts are silent on whether handwritten/typed personal notes or photocopies are allowed — **rely on the centralised WILP guideline doc, not on hearsay**.
- No internet, no laptop, no phone.

**Open-book preparation**
- An open-book exam is harder, not easier. Questions test *application*, not retrieval.
- Build a **20–40 page personal index** with formula tables, framework one-pagers, worked examples categorised by problem type (see `sem_1/<course>/05_revision_quick_sheet.md`).
- Tab the prescribed textbook(s) with sticky notes by chapter and problem type.

## 6. Make-up exams

- Per WILP regulations (not per individual handout), make-up tests are available for documented work-related conflicts (employer letter required) — apply through the WILP examinations cell before the announced cutoff.
- Make-up papers tend to be slightly different in coverage emphasis. Plan to write the regular paper if at all possible.

## 7. Question-paper conventions (advisory — not from handouts)

> The four S2-25 handouts do **not** prescribe a question-paper structure. The patterns below are observed-conventions across past WILP MBA papers and the published PYQs in `sem_1/<course>/04_pyq_bank.md`. Treat them as a *prep heuristic*, not a contract.

### Numerical / quantitative courses (Bus Stats, Mgmt Accounting)
- Typically 1 large numerical (15+ marks) plus 2-3 medium numericals.
- 1-2 short-answer theory questions (definitions, comparisons, classifications).
- Show all formulas and underline the final answer.

### Conceptual / case courses (MPO, Marketing)
- Typically 1 mini-case (a paragraph followed by 2-3 sub-questions, total 15-20 marks).
- 3-4 framework-application questions ("Apply Porter's 5 Forces to …", "Use Kotter's 8 steps …").
- 2-3 short-answer definition / model questions.
- For Marketing: be ready for a question on one of the **6 mandatory R1 cases** (see `sem_1/SOURCES.md` for the case-to-session map).

## 9. Grading and CGPA mechanics (per BITS Academic Regulations)

- **Relative grading** within the course-batch is the standard at BITS.
- Letter grade-to-points mapping (per BITS Academic Regulations 2023, linked in `sem_1/SOURCES.md`): A = 10, A− = 9, B = 8, B− = 7, C = 6, C− = 5, D = 4, E = 2 (fail).
- Sem 1 course units: each of the four courses is **4 credit units** (verified in handouts).
- CGPA = Σ(Units × Grade points) ÷ Σ(Units).
- **Minimum CGPA to graduate**: 5.50, with no E grade and no RRA flag (per BITS Academic Regulations).
- Borderline policy: a 1-2 mark gap can flip you across grade boundaries → fight for every easy mark in EC-1, especially in MPO where EC-1 is only 25 %.

## 10. Re-evaluation and grievance

- Answer scripts can usually be requested for review within a published window after results.
- File grievances through the LMS Grievance module with specific question references.
- Decisions are usually final after the review committee response.

> Re-evaluation timing and policy is set by the WILP examinations cell, not the course handout. Look out for the official notification on Taxila after each EC-2 / EC-3 result release.
