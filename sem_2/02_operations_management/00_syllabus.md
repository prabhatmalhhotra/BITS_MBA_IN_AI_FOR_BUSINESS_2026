# Operations Management — Syllabus

> BITS code: **QM ZG526 — Operations Management**.
> Textbook anchor: Heizer, Render & Munson, *Operations Management* (12e+).

## Course objective

Develop competence in designing, planning, executing and controlling operations systems that deliver value to customers — applicable to both manufacturing and services. Build a quantitative toolkit for decisions on capacity, location, layout, scheduling, quality, inventory and supply chain.

## Modules / units

### Module 1 — Operations & Productivity
- Definition and scope of OM; manufacturing vs services
- Productivity measurement; single-factor and multi-factor productivity
- 10 strategic OM decisions
- Operations strategy: cost, quality, time, flexibility

### Module 2 — Operations Strategy in a Global Environment
- Global operations strategy
- Mission, strategy, competitive advantage
- Global view of operations and supply chains
- Make-or-buy and outsourcing decisions

### Module 3 — Project Management
- Project life cycle
- Work breakdown structure (WBS)
- CPM (Critical Path Method); PERT (probabilistic)
- Crashing; cost-time trade-off
- Project monitoring & earned value (overview)

### Module 4 — Forecasting
- Time-series and associative models
- Moving average, weighted MA, exponential smoothing (level, trend, seasonality)
- Linear regression, multiple regression
- Error measures: MAD, MSE, MAPE, tracking signal
- ML-based forecasting (intro)

### Module 5 — Design of Goods and Services
- Product life cycle, NPD process
- Quality function deployment (QFD); House of Quality
- Concurrent engineering, value engineering, modular design
- Service design considerations

### Module 6 — Quality Management
- Defining quality; cost of quality (PAF: prevention, appraisal, internal+external failure)
- TQM; Six Sigma; DMAIC
- Quality awards (Deming, Baldrige)
- Tools: 7 QC tools (cause-effect, Pareto, control chart, scatter, histogram, check sheet, flow chart)
- ISO 9000 family

### Module 7 — Statistical Process Control (SPC)
- Variability: common vs special causes
- Control charts for variables (X̄, R)
- Control charts for attributes (p, c)
- Process capability (Cp, Cpk)
- Acceptance sampling (intro): OC curve

### Module 8 — Process Strategy & Sustainability
- Process types: process focus, repetitive, product focus, mass customization
- Process analysis: flowcharts, time-function maps
- Service process matrix
- Capacity planning: design vs effective capacity, utilization, efficiency
- Sustainability in operations

### Module 9 — Capacity & Location Decisions
- Break-even analysis (single + multi-product)
- Decision trees for capacity
- Location factors; factor rating method; centre of gravity; transportation method (LP)

### Module 10 — Layout Strategies
- Layout types: process, product, fixed, cellular, retail, warehouse, office
- Line balancing for product layout (cycle time, theoretical min stations, efficiency)
- Process layout: load-distance method, CRAFT (intro)

### Module 11 — Inventory Management
- Functions of inventory; ABC analysis
- EOQ model; production order quantity; quantity discount
- Reorder point with safety stock; service level
- Probabilistic models (continuous, periodic review)
- Vendor-managed inventory; JIT

### Module 12 — Aggregate Planning & MRP
- Aggregate planning options: chase, level, mixed
- Master Production Schedule (MPS)
- Material Requirements Planning (MRP); BOM, MRP logic
- ERP overview

### Module 13 — Short-Term Scheduling
- Loading: Gantt charts, assignment method (Hungarian)
- Sequencing: priority rules (FCFS, SPT, EDD, LPT, CR), Johnson's rule
- Theory of constraints (TOC); finite capacity scheduling

### Module 14 — JIT, Lean & TPS
- JIT philosophy; pull system; kanban
- Lean: 8 wastes (TIMWOODS); 5S; value stream mapping
- Toyota Production System (TPS) pillars: JIT + Jidoka

### Module 15 — Maintenance & Reliability (overview)
- Reliability; MTBF; series vs parallel systems
- Preventive vs breakdown maintenance
- Total Productive Maintenance (TPM)

### Module 16 — Supply Chain Management (overview)
- Bullwhip effect
- Sourcing: outsourcing, insourcing, single vs multi-sourcing
- Logistics: warehousing, transportation modes
- E-procurement, blockchain in supply chain
- Sustainability and resilience post-COVID
