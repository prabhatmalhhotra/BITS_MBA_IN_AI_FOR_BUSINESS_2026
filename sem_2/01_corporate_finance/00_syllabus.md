# Corporate Finance — Syllabus

> Likely BITS code: **MBA ZG521 / FIN ZG521 — Financial Management**. Confirm on Taxila handout.

## Course objective

Build the analytical toolkit to make value-creating financial decisions: capital budgeting, capital structure, working capital, dividend policy, and risk-return trade-offs.

## Modules / units

### Module 1 — Introduction to Corporate Finance
- Goal of the firm: maximise shareholder value (vs profit maximisation)
- Agency problem; corporate governance
- Time value of money (PV, FV, annuities, perpetuities)
- Indian financial system overview (regulators, markets, instruments)

### Module 2 — Risk and Return
- Expected return; standard deviation as risk
- Portfolio theory (Markowitz)
- CAPM: E(R) = Rf + β(Rm − Rf)
- Beta estimation; systematic vs unsystematic risk
- WACC

### Module 3 — Valuation
- Bond valuation: YTM, current yield, premium/discount
- Equity valuation: Dividend discount model (Gordon, multi-stage), FCFE/FCFF
- Relative valuation: P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B

### Module 4 — Capital Budgeting
- NPV, IRR, MIRR, Profitability Index, Payback, Discounted Payback
- Cash-flow estimation (incremental, after-tax, terminal value)
- Real options
- Risk in capital budgeting (sensitivity, scenario, simulation)

### Module 5 — Cost of Capital
- Cost of debt (after-tax)
- Cost of equity (CAPM, DCF, bond-yield-plus-risk-premium)
- Cost of preference
- WACC and its uses
- Marginal cost of capital

### Module 6 — Capital Structure
- Theories: Net Income, Net Operating Income, Modigliani-Miller (with/without tax), Trade-off, Pecking Order
- Optimal capital structure
- Financial leverage; degree of financial leverage; combined leverage

### Module 7 — Dividend Policy
- Theories: Walter, Gordon, MM irrelevance, Bird-in-hand
- Practical determinants
- Forms: cash, stock, bonus, buybacks
- Signalling

### Module 8 — Working Capital Management
- Operating cycle; cash conversion cycle
- Inventory, receivables, cash management
- Sources of short-term finance
- Working capital financing strategies (conservative, aggressive, matching)

### Module 9 — Mergers, Acquisitions and Corporate Restructuring (overview)
- Types of M&A; motives; valuation
- Synergy
- Hostile vs friendly takeovers

### Module 10 — International Finance (overview)
- Exchange rate basics; forex risk
- Hedging instruments

### Module 11 — Behavioural Finance (overview)
- Heuristics, biases (anchoring, loss aversion)
- Implications for corporate decision-making

### Module 12 — Sustainability & ESG Finance (overview)
- Green bonds, sustainability-linked loans
- Climate-risk in capital decisions
