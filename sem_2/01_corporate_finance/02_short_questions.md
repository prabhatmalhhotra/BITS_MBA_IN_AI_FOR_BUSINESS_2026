# Corporate Finance — Short Questions (with Answers)

> 30 short answers. 2–5 marks each.

**Q1. State the goal of financial management.** Maximise long-term shareholder wealth (market value per share), not short-term profit.

**Q2. Define agency problem.** Conflict between managers (agents) and owners (principals); managers may pursue self-interest at the cost of shareholders.

**Q3. State three differences between profit maximisation and wealth maximisation.** Profit ignores time value, risk, and quality of earnings; wealth accounts for all three.

**Q4. Compute FV of ₹1,00,000 at 10% for 5 years compounded annually.** FV = 1,00,000 × (1.1)⁵ = 1,00,000 × 1.6105 = **₹1,61,050**.

**Q5. PV of an annuity of ₹50,000 for 10 years at 8%.** PV = 50,000 × [(1 − 1.08⁻¹⁰)/0.08] = 50,000 × 6.7101 = **₹3,35,505**.

**Q6. State CAPM equation.** E(R) = Rf + β(Rm − Rf).

**Q7. Differentiate systematic and unsystematic risk.** Systematic = market-wide, undiversifiable, rewarded. Unsystematic = firm-specific, diversifiable, not rewarded.

**Q8. Beta = 1.4, Rf = 7%, market premium = 6%. Cost of equity?** ke = 7 + 1.4 × 6 = **15.4%**.

**Q9. Define WACC.** Weighted average cost of capital = blended cost of debt, equity, preference using market-value weights, after tax for debt.

**Q10. Compute WACC: D = 40%, E = 60%, kd = 8% (before tax), ke = 15%, t = 30%.** WACC = 0.6 × 15 + 0.4 × 8 × (1 − 0.3) = 9 + 2.24 = **11.24%**.

**Q11. Differentiate NPV and IRR.** NPV = absolute value addition (in ₹) at given discount rate. IRR = discount rate where NPV = 0. NPV preferred for mutually exclusive projects.

**Q12. State the IRR rule.** Accept project if IRR > cost of capital.

**Q13. Define profitability index.** PI = PV of inflows / Initial investment. Accept if PI > 1.

**Q14. NPV of project: Investment ₹10 cr, annual CF ₹3 cr for 5 years, discount rate 12%.** PV = 3 × 3.6048 = 10.81; NPV = 10.81 − 10 = **₹0.81 cr**. Accept (positive).

**Q15. Define discounted payback period.** Time taken for cumulative discounted cash flows to equal the initial investment.

**Q16. State Modigliani-Miller's Proposition I (without tax).** Value of firm is independent of capital structure in a perfect market.

**Q17. State the trade-off theory of capital structure.** Optimal capital structure balances tax shield benefits of debt against financial distress costs.

**Q18. State the pecking order theory.** Firms prefer internal financing → debt → equity (last resort), driven by information asymmetry.

**Q19. DOL formula.** DOL = % change in EBIT / % change in Sales = Contribution / EBIT.

**Q20. DFL formula.** DFL = % change in EPS / % change in EBIT = EBIT / (EBIT − Interest).

**Q21. Define dividend irrelevance theory.** MM (1961) — in perfect markets, dividend policy doesn't affect firm value; investors can create homemade dividends.

**Q22. State Walter's model.** P = (D + (r/k)(E − D)) / k. Shows dividend policy depends on r vs k; if r > k → retain; r < k → pay out; r = k → indifferent.

**Q23. Define cash conversion cycle.** CCC = Inventory days + Receivables days − Payables days. Days from cash outflow for raw material to cash inflow from sale.

**Q24. EOQ formula.** EOQ = √(2DS/H), D = annual demand, S = order cost, H = holding cost per unit per year.

**Q25. Compute EOQ: D = 10,000, S = ₹500, H = ₹4.** EOQ = √(2 × 10,000 × 500 / 4) = √(25,00,000) = **1,581 units**.

**Q26. Differentiate aggressive and conservative working capital financing strategies.** Aggressive = more short-term debt (cheaper, riskier). Conservative = more long-term capital (safer, costlier).

**Q27. Define Operating Cycle.** Inventory days + Receivables days. Time from raw material purchase to cash collection from sale.

**Q28. Differentiate horizontal, vertical and conglomerate mergers.** Horizontal = same industry. Vertical = supply-chain integration (forward/backward). Conglomerate = unrelated industries.

**Q29. Define synergy in M&A.** Combined entity is more valuable than sum of parts (1+1 > 2). Sources: cost synergy (scale, redundancy elimination), revenue synergy (cross-sell, market access).

**Q30. State Purchasing Power Parity.** Exchange rates adjust to equalise prices of identical goods across countries; rate change ≈ inflation differential.
